My Morning: Fast or Slow?

Describe your morning step by step. What do you do first? What do you never skip? How long does it take?

🎪 Show & Tell 💉 Vocab 🔀 Choose & Speak 🃏 Questions ✨ My Sentence
🎪
Phase 1
Show & Tell
Show your alarm time on your phone and describe your first 30 minutes
5 MIN
Today's Object

Open your alarm app or clock on your phone. Show your alarm time and describe what you do in the first 30 minutes of your day.

Teacher models first:
"OK, look — my alarm is at 6:15. But I don't wake up at 6:15! I hit snooze 3 times, so I really wake up at 6:45. Then I check my phone for 10 minutes — WhatsApp, news, weather. Then I take a shower — always a hot shower. I make coffee, I eat toast, and I leave the house at 7:30. My morning is fast — only 45 minutes from bed to car. OK, now you — show me YOUR alarm."

Phase 4
Question Cards
The teacher answers first. Then it's your turn.
12 MIN
Question 1
What time do you wake up on a normal day?
Teacher: "I wake up at 6:45. My alarm is at 6:15 but I always hit snooze. I can't wake up with the first alarm — I need 3 alarms! I know it's a bad habit."
Now you: "I wake up at _______ . My alarm is at _______ but I..."
Tip: The gap between "alarm time" and "real wake up time" is always funny. Push: "How many times do you hit snooze? Can you wake up without an alarm on weekends?"
Question 2
What is the first thing you do every morning?
Teacher: "The first thing I do is check my phone. I read WhatsApp messages, then the news, then the weather. I spend about 10 minutes in bed with my phone before I get up. I know — bad habit!"
Now you: "The first thing I do is _______ . I spend _______ minutes on..."
Tip: Most people say "phone." If they say something different (prayer, water, exercise), celebrate it: "That's very unusual! Most people check their phone. You have discipline."
Question 3
Do you eat breakfast every day? What do you eat?
Teacher: "I always eat breakfast. I can't work without food. I usually eat eggs with toast and I always drink black coffee. I never eat cereal — I think cereal is for children, not for adults."
Now you: "I _______ eat breakfast. I usually eat _______ and I drink..."
Tip: Stack frequency adverbs: "Do you ALWAYS eat breakfast? Or do you SOMETIMES skip it? Do you NEVER eat fruit?" Three adverbs = three sentences.
Question 4
Can you get ready in 20 minutes?
Teacher: "Yes, I can! On a normal day I take 45 minutes, but when I'm late, I can do it in 20. I skip breakfast, I take a very fast shower, and I don't check my phone. It's stressful but I can do it."
Now you: "I can / can't get ready in 20 minutes. I need at least _______ because..."
Tip: Great "can/can't" moment. Push the contrast: "What do you SKIP when you're late? What can you NOT skip — even when you're late?" Forces prioritization in English.
Question 5
What do you never skip in the morning?
Teacher: "Coffee. I never skip coffee. I can skip breakfast, I can skip the shower on a really bad day, but I can NEVER skip coffee. Without coffee, I can't think, I can't talk, I can't work."
Now you: "I never skip _______ . I can skip _______ but I can never skip..."
Tip: This is the personality question — what's non-negotiable reveals values. Push the can/can't pattern: "Can you live without coffee for one day? Can you work without breakfast?" Strong production.
Question 6
Is there traffic on your way to work? How long is your commute?
Teacher: "Yes, there is always traffic in Celaya at 7:30. My commute is 25 minutes, but with traffic it's 40 minutes. There are too many cars on the main road. I usually listen to podcasts in the car."
Now you: "There is / There isn't traffic on my way to work. My commute takes..."
Tip: "There is traffic" is perfect there is/are practice. Push: "Is there traffic every day? Are there alternative routes? What do you do in the car — music? Podcasts? Nothing?"
Question 7
What is different about your morning on weekends?
Teacher: "On weekends, I wake up at 9. I don't use an alarm. I make a big breakfast — eggs, fruit, juice, coffee. I don't rush. I read the news slowly. My weekend morning is 2 hours. I love it."
Now you: "On weekends, I wake up at _______ . I don't _______ . I _______ because..."
Tip: Contrast is key: "On Monday you wake up at 6 — on Saturday at 10? That's 4 hours difference!" Push: "Do you eat the same breakfast? Do you check your phone first?"
Question 8
Do you like mornings or do you hate mornings?
Teacher: "I like mornings when I have time. A slow morning with coffee and music is beautiful. But a fast morning when I'm late is terrible. I think mornings show your personality — fast people are organized, slow people enjoy life more."
Now you: "I like / hate mornings because I _______ . I think mornings are..."
Tip: This is the closing opinion. Let the student talk freely. If they say "I hate mornings," push: "Why? Is it the alarm? The rush? The traffic? Or do you just like the night more?"
