Cancun vs.
Real Mexico
Tourists love Cancun. Mexicans have opinions about that. Strong ones.
🪝
The Trigger
Phase 1 · Provocation & Opening
⏱ 4 min
The Guardian · 2023
"Mexico received 42 million international tourists in 2023 — and most of them went to Cancun, Los Cabos, or Tulum. Less than 8% visited any place that wasn't on the beach."
Teacher script: Read it. Then ask: "When a foreign friend or colleague asks you for travel recommendations in Mexico — where do you send them? And do you ever feel a little bothered when they only go to Cancun?" Wait. That second question usually generates a strong reaction. Let it happen naturally.

The Conversation Engine
Phase 4 · 10 Questions · 4 Blocks
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 · Personal Experience
Q1
Have you ever been to Cancun, Tulum, or Los Cabos? What was your honest opinion?
Teacher Tip Both "I loved it" and "I hated it" are valid — push for specifics either way. "What did you do? Who were the other people there? Did it feel like Mexico to you?" The last question usually generates the most interesting answer.
Q2
Have you ever recommended Mexico to a foreign friend or colleague? Where did you tell them to go?
Model Answer "Yes — my colleague from Canada asked me. I told him: go to Guanajuato, go to Oaxaca, go to Puebla. Don't just go to Cancun. He went to Cancun anyway. I don't think he really listened."
Q3
Have you ever felt embarrassed or proud when a foreign person talked about Mexico in front of you?
Teacher Tip This often generates a strong personal memory. "What did they say? Was it about the food, the crime, the beach? How did you react — did you correct them or stay quiet?"
Block 2 · Opinion
Q4
Is Cancun "fake Mexico" — or is it just a different part of Mexico that is valid?
Teacher Tip No wrong answer. Push them to defend their position: "If someone who lives and works in Cancun is Mexican, how is Cancun fake? And if it's not fake — why does it feel different?"
Q5
Do you think tourists who only go to Cancun are missing something important? Or is that their choice and it's fine?
Model Answer "They are missing something. But I also think it's their vacation and their money. I can't tell someone how to relax. I just feel a little sad when they go home and think they know Mexico when they've only been to a hotel."
Q6
Do you think Mexico does a good job of showing foreigners what the country is really like — or do we hide it?
Teacher Tip Great identity and culture question. "What would you show a foreigner in YOUR city? What does your city have that Cancun doesn't? How would you explain it in English?" This last question is a speaking challenge in itself.
Block 3 · Hypothetical
Q7
What would you do if a foreign coworker visited Mexico, only went to Cancun, and then said: "Mexico is beautiful but very American"?
Model Answer "I would laugh first. Then I would say: 'I want to show you something different. Can we plan a weekend trip before you go home?' And I would take them to a market, a local restaurant, and a place in my city. No beach. No resort."
Q8
What would you do if you had $20,000 pesos for a vacation — would you go to Cancun or somewhere else in Mexico? Why?
Teacher Tip Personal and concrete. Push: "Where exactly? What would you do there? Is there a place in Mexico you have always wanted to go but haven't gone yet — and why not?"
Block 4 · Provocative
Q9
If someone from another country described YOUR city or state as "boring" or "not worth visiting," how would you feel — and what would you say?
Teacher Tip This personalizes the whole debate. "What is special about where you are from that a tourist would never find on Google? What do you know about your city that most visitors never discover?" Great identity-building exercise.
Q10
What is one thing about Mexico that you think foreigners completely misunderstand — and what would you want to tell them?
Model Answer "That we are dangerous. Mexico has problems, yes. But the Mexico I live in every day — my neighborhood, my street, my family's table — is warm, safe, and full of life. The Mexico on the news and the Mexico I know are very different places."

The Exit Shot
Phase 5 · Final Dilemma · No right answer
⏱ 5 min
💣 Exit Shot
A foreign colleague visits Mexico for 5 days. They have $30,000 pesos to spend. They ask you: "Should I go to Cancun or somewhere different?" You have one chance to send them somewhere that will show them the Mexico you are proud of. Where do you send them — and what do you tell them to do, eat, and see?
