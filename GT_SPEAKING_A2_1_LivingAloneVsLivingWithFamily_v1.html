A2.1 Daily Life & Lifestyle 🌡 Medium 50 min

Living Alone vs. Living With Family

Freedom or company? Silence or noise? Your rules or their rules? Which one is really better?

🪝
Phase 1
The Trigger
⏱ 4 min
"In Mexico, 70% of adults under 35 still live with their parents. In countries like Germany or the USA, that number is 30%. Is it about money, culture, or love for family?"
— INEGI / Pew Research Center
Teacher script: "70% of young adults in Mexico live with their family. In other countries — much less. So — do you live alone or with family?" [Pause for response.] "And which one do you think is better? Today we find out."

Phase 4
The Conversation Engine
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 — Personal Experience (Past Simple)
Q1
Did you ever live alone? What was the best part?
Past simple. "Yes, I lived alone for..." / "No, I never lived alone." If never — ask: "Do you want to? Why or why not?" Both answers open great conversation. Best part: freedom, quiet, my own rules. Let them tell the story.
Q2
What was the most difficult thing about your home situation? With family — or alone?
Past simple describing difficulties. "The most difficult thing was..." With family: no privacy, arguments, questions. Alone: cooking, loneliness, paying bills. Model: "The most difficult thing was cooking. I ate eggs every day for a month."
Q3
Did you ever argue with a family member about a rule at home? What happened?
Past simple conflict story. "Yes, one time I argued with my mom because..." Common conflicts: what time to come home, noise, cleaning, visitors. Let the story develop. Fun and relatable — almost everyone has a family rule story.
Block 2 — Opinion (Simple Present)
Q4
What do you think is better — living alone or with family? Why?
Core opinion. "I think living alone is better because..." / "I think family is better because..." Simple present + "because." Both sides are valid. Lucía vs Ricardo tension. Let them defend their position with their own experience.
Q5
Do you think adults over 30 who live with their parents are doing something wrong — or is it a good decision?
Andrés's provocateur view. "I think it is okay because..." / "I think adults need to be independent because..." Very personal in Mexican culture — many students live with family. No judgment from you. Let the debate happen.
Q6
What is more important — saving money or having independence?
Values comparison. "I think money / independence is more important because..." Ricardo vs Lucía. Very direct question, easy to answer with simple grammar. Connects to real life decisions most students face or have faced.
Block 3 — Future Plans (Going To / Will)
Q7
Are you going to move — to live alone or with family — in the next year? Why?
Going to for near future plans. "Yes, I'm going to move because..." / "No, I'm going to stay because..." Very personal and concrete. Reveals real life situations. Allow them to explain the reasons — money, relationship, job change.
Q8
When your children grow up, will you want them to stay at home or move out?
Will for future preference. "I will want them to stay because..." / "I will want them to move out because..." Parent vs child perspective flip. Many students say "stay" — ask: "Until what age?" That follow-up always generates debate.
Block 4 — Provocative
Q9
Be honest — what is something your family does at home that makes you a little crazy?
Confession question. Simple present. Common answers: too many questions ("where are you going?"), TV too loud, too much food, no privacy. Model: "My mom asks me questions every day when I come home. Every day. The same questions." Said with affection — keep it funny, not mean.
Q10
Your parents ask you to come back and live with them for 6 months to save money. It is a good deal. What are you going to say?
Going to for immediate decision. "I'm going to say yes because..." / "I'm going to say no because..." The 6-month detail makes it concrete and not forever. Good for conditions: "I'm going to say yes, but I need my own room." Very realistic for Mexican professionals.

Phase 5
The Exit Shot
⏱ 5 min
Final Question
You have a good job and a little money. You can move out and live alone — but it is expensive and difficult. OR you can stay with your family — free, with food, but no privacy and many questions. What are you going to do?
