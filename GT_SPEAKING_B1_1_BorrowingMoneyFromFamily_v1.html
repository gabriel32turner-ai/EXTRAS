Borrowing Money
From Family:
Good Idea?
The money arrives. Then the questions. Then the comments. Then the silence at Christmas dinner.
🪝
The Trigger
Phase 1 · Provocation & Opening
⏱ 4 min
The Guardian · 2023
"67% of people who borrowed money from a family member said the relationship was permanently damaged — even when they paid it back."
Teacher script: Read it. Then ask: "Has anyone here ever lent money to a family member — or borrowed from one?" Wait. Then: "Did it change the relationship? How?" This hits fast and personally. Let the room do the work.

The Conversation Engine
Phase 4 · 10 Questions · 4 Blocks
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 · Personal Experience
Q1
Have you ever lent money to a family member or friend? Did they pay you back?
Teacher Tip Almost everyone has a story. Push for the specifics: "How much? How long ago? Do you still talk about it — or is it something nobody mentions anymore?"
Q2
Have you ever asked someone in your family for money? How did it feel to ask?
Model Answer "Yes — I asked my dad once when I was 26. I was so embarrassed. He gave it to me without questions. But every time I saw him for the next year, I felt bad. Even after I paid it back."
Q3
Did you ever see a problem between family members because of money? What happened?
Teacher Tip Very common in Mexican families. "Who was involved? Did it get resolved — or is it still a problem? Was there a Christmas or birthday when everyone was uncomfortable?" Real stories flow easily here.
Block 2 · Opinion
Q4
Is it OK to say no when a family member asks you for money? Or do you have to help?
Teacher Tip This question creates strong reactions. "If it's your parent? Your brother? A cousin you don't know well? Does the answer change? What's the most important factor — how close you are, or why they need it?"
Q5
Do you think it's a good idea to write down a loan agreement with family — or does that feel cold and wrong?
Model Answer "I think it's smart. But I could never do it with my mom or my brother. It would feel terrible — like I don't trust them. Maybe that's the problem. We trust feelings more than paper, and then we're surprised when there's a problem."
Q6
Do you believe that money always changes a relationship — or does it only matter if one person doesn't pay it back?
Teacher Tip Great debate question. "Think about Sofía and her nephew's operation — does money change that relationship? Or Fernando and his sister — where's the real problem: the money or the lie?"
Block 3 · Hypothetical
Q7
What would you do if a family member asked you for $30,000 pesos and you knew they probably wouldn't pay you back?
Model Answer "I think I would give a smaller amount — maybe $5,000 — and say that's all I can give. That way I'm helping, but I'm not losing everything. And I don't say it's a loan. I say it's a gift. That way there's no expectation."
Q8
What would you do if you borrowed money from your parents and you couldn't pay it back this month — would you tell them, or stay quiet?
Teacher Tip The honesty vs. shame dilemma. "Would you avoid their calls? Send a message? Or sit down and explain? What would hurt them more — not getting the money, or not knowing the truth?"
Block 4 · Provocative
Q9
Is there someone in your family right now who owes you money — or who you owe money to? How do you feel about that?
Teacher Tip The direct personal question. Give space — some people will laugh nervously, others will go quiet. Both are good signals. "Is it something you talk about — or something everyone pretends doesn't exist?"
Q10
If you found out your brother or sister borrowed money from your parents without telling you — and never paid it back — how would you feel?
Model Answer "Angry. Very angry. Not because of the money exactly — but because they kept it secret. Secrets in families grow. And then one day you find out and you realize you didn't know your family as well as you thought."

The Exit Shot
Phase 5 · Final Dilemma · No right answer
⏱ 5 min
💣 Exit Shot
Your mom lent your brother $40,000 pesos two years ago. He never paid her back. She never talks about it. You can see it hurts her. Your brother is now doing well — he has a good job and a new car. He says nothing. Do you say something to your brother — or do you stay out of it?
📱 WhatsApp Homework Record 60 seconds in English: Do you talk to your brother — yes or no? And what do you say?
