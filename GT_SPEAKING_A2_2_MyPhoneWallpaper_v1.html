My Phone Wallpaper
Tells My Story

Look at your phone right now. The photo on your screen — your family, your pet, your trip, a cartoon. That image says something about you. Let's talk about it.

🪝
The Trigger
Phase 1 · Let's Talk Starter
⏱ 4 min
A simple question to start
"Psychologists say that your phone wallpaper is a window to your heart. The photo you choose — a person, a place, an animal, or nothing — shows what is most important to you right now. What is your phone wallpaper today?"
Teacher Script: Ask the student to show you their phone wallpaper. Yes — take out the phone right now. Ask: "What is this? Who is this? Why did you choose this photo?" This is the perfect A2.2 warm-up: zero grammar stress, 100% personal engagement, and the student is describing something real in front of them. Let the conversation start from the image.

The Conversation Engine
Phase 4 · 10 Questions · 4 Blocks
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 · Personal Experience — Past Simple
Q1
What was your phone wallpaper before this one? Why did you change it?
Target: past simple (was, changed, chose). Push: "How long did you have the old one?" Model: "Before this, I had a photo of my cat. I changed it 2 months ago because I took a new photo of my family on vacation. The new photo was more beautiful." Simple and descriptive.
Q2
Did you take the photo on your wallpaper, or did someone give it to you?
Good for activating: took, gave, sent, found, downloaded. Push: "Where were you? Who was with you?" Target: past simple + simple location/context. Let the student describe the memory behind the photo. This is the emotional core of the session.
Q3
Was there a time when you looked at your phone wallpaper and it made you sad — not happy?
Connects to Mateo's bubble about his dog. Could be a lost pet, an ex-partner, a person who passed away, or a trip that ended. Push: "Did you change the wallpaper — or keep it?" Target: past simple + simple emotion adjectives. Warm and human.
Block 2 · Opinion
Q4
What do you think a phone wallpaper says about a person? Can you guess someone's personality from their wallpaper?
Fun and accessible. Push: "If someone has a photo of their pet, what does that say? What about just a color?" Target: I think / maybe / probably + simple present. Model: "I think if your wallpaper is your family, you are a family person. If it's just a color, maybe you are very organized. Or boring!" Laugh with the student.
Q5
Is it OK to have a photo of your ex-partner on your wallpaper — or is that a bad idea?
Light spicy question for A2.2. Keep it fun, not serious. Push: "What would your new partner think?" Target: I think it's OK / I think it's a bad idea + because. Model: "I think it's a bad idea. Your new partner will see it and ask questions. Change the photo!" Gets students laughing.
Q6
Do you think a photo of your children is the best wallpaper — or is that a little boring?
Polarizing but fun. Push: "What is a more interesting wallpaper?" Target: present simple + comparative (more interesting, more boring, more personal). No right answer — just opinion production. Good for 2-3 exchanges.
Block 3 · Future Plans — Going to / Will
Q7
Are you going to change your wallpaper soon? What photo are you going to use?
Target: going to + future plans. Push: "Why that photo? When are you going to change it?" Model: "Yes, I'm going to change it after my vacation next month. I want a photo from the beach. Something beautiful and relaxing." Simple and personal.
Q8
Is there a photo you want to take this year that will be perfect for your wallpaper? What will it be?
Target: will + simple future. Push: "Where? With who? When?" Model: "I want to take a photo with my family at the beach this summer. All of us together. I will use that as my wallpaper." Gets the student talking about future plans in simple, concrete terms.
Block 4 · Past Habits — Used to
Q9
Did you use to have a very different wallpaper when you were younger — maybe a celebrity, a cartoon, or a soccer player?
Target: used to + past simple. This is a fun nostalgia question. Push: "Who was it? Are you embarrassed now?" Model: "I used to have a photo of Messi. I was 18. Now I think it's a little funny — but at the time it was very important to me!" Gets students laughing and talking.
Q10
Did your relationship with your phone change in the last 5 years? Did you use to use it differently?
Reflection question. Target: used to + past simple + present simple for contrast. Model: "Yes. I used to use my phone only for calls and messages. Now I use it for everything — shopping, work, news, videos. It's completely different. My phone is like my office now." Simple but meaningful.
💣
The Exit Shot
Phase 5 · One Final Question
⏱ 5 min
Final Question
Look at your phone wallpaper right now. Is the person or place in that photo happy because of you — or do you think you can do more for them? What are you going to do?
Homework Loop: "Take a photo of your phone wallpaper. Then record a 60-second WhatsApp voice message. Tell me: who or what is in the photo, and one thing you are going to do for that person or place this week. In English. Simple words are fine."
