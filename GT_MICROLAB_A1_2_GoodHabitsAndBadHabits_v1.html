Good Habits and Bad Habits

Talk about what you do every day. The good things and the bad things. Be honest!

🎪 Show & Tell 💉 Vocab 🔀 Choose & Speak 🃏 Questions ✨ My Sentence
🎪
Phase 1
Show & Tell
Show something on your phone that represents a habit — good or bad
5 MIN
Today's Object

Show your screen time, a fitness app, your alarm clock, or your Netflix history — something that shows a daily habit.

Teacher models first:
"OK, look — this is my screen time. I use my phone 4 hours a day! That's a bad habit. I check Instagram every morning and I watch YouTube every night before I sleep. But I also have a good habit — I drink 2 liters of water every day. I always carry a water bottle. OK, now you — show me something from YOUR phone that shows a habit."
Your Turn — Sentence Starters

Phase 4
Question Cards
The teacher answers first. Then it's your turn.
12 MIN
Question 1
What is your worst bad habit?
Teacher: "My worst bad habit is my phone. I check it first thing in the morning — before I even drink water! I scroll Instagram, I read the news, I check WhatsApp. Sometimes I spend 30 minutes in bed just on my phone."
Now you: "My worst bad habit is _______ . I always _______ and I can't stop."
Tip: Everyone has a phone habit — it's universal. If student says "nothing," push: "Come on! Phone? Coffee? Staying up late? Everybody has one bad habit!" Honesty produces the best language.
Question 2
What time do you usually wake up? Is it early enough?
Teacher: "I wake up at 6:30. I need to wake up at 6, but I always hit snooze. I have 3 alarms! It's terrible. I can't wake up with just one alarm."
Now you: "I wake up at _______ . I need to wake up at _______ but..."
Tip: "Hit snooze" is a great chunk to teach — model it. Ask: "How many alarms do you have? Do you wake up on the first alarm or the third?" Gets laughs.
Question 3
Do you exercise? How often?
Teacher: "I exercise 3 times a week. I go to the gym on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday. But sometimes I skip Friday because I'm tired. I can run 5 km but I can't do more than that."
Now you: "I exercise _______ times a week. I go to _______ . I can _______ but I can't..."
Tip: Perfect for "can/can't" + frequency. Push: "Can you run? Can you swim? Can you do push-ups? How many?" Physical ability is concrete and produces real sentences.
Question 4
What do you do before you sleep?
Teacher: "I watch Netflix or YouTube. I stay up until 12 almost every night. I know it's bad for my sleep but I like my quiet time. I never read a book before bed — I always choose the screen."
Now you: "Before I sleep, I _______ . I usually _______ until _______ ."
Tip: Nighttime routines reveal personality. Push for sequence: "First you check your phone, then what? Do you eat something? Do you drink something? What do you watch?"
Question 5
Is there a gym near your house? Do you go?
Teacher: "Yes, there is a gym 5 minutes from my house. I go 3 times a week. There are also some parks but I never go to the parks for exercise. I prefer the gym because there is air conditioning!"
Now you: "There is _______ near my house. I go / I don't go because..."
Tip: Natural "there is/there are" moment. Push: "What is INSIDE the gym? Are there machines? Is there a pool? Are there classes?" Every answer is "there is" practice.
Question 6
How many hours do you sleep? Is it enough?
Teacher: "I sleep 6 hours on weekdays. That's not enough — I need 7 or 8. On weekends, I sleep 9 hours. I always feel tired on Monday because I don't sleep enough during the week."
Now you: "I sleep _______ hours. I need _______ hours. I always feel..."
Tip: Sleep is personal and relatable. Ask: "Do you sleep MORE on weekends? How many hours? Is Monday the worst day because of sleep?" Connects habit to feeling.
Question 7
What is one good habit you do every day?
Teacher: "I drink 2 liters of water every day. I always carry a bottle. I also eat fruit every morning — usually an apple or a banana. Those are my two good habits. Everything else is... work in progress!"
Now you: "Every day, I always _______ . I also _______ because..."
Tip: After 6 questions about bad habits, this is the positive moment. Celebrate whatever they say: "That's a great habit! Do you do it EVERY day or sometimes you skip?"
Question 8
Can you change a bad habit in one week?
Teacher: "No! I can't change a bad habit in one week. I think you need one month or more. I always try to change my phone habit but after 3 days I go back to the same thing. It's very difficult."
Now you: "I think I can / I can't change _______ in one week because..."
Tip: Philosophical closer with "can/can't." Push: "What is harder to change — a food habit or a phone habit? Can you live without your phone for one day?" Big opinion, simple grammar.
