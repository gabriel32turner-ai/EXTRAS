The Trigger
Phase 1 · Warm-Up
⏱ 4 min
A real calculation to start
"If you buy one coffee every workday — $45 pesos each — that is $900 pesos a month. $10,800 pesos a year. Just on coffee. What do YOU spend money on without thinking?"
Teacher script: Read the numbers slowly. Let them feel it. Then share your own: "For me, it's [food delivery / taxis / snacks at the OXXO]. What about you?" Ask: "What is one thing you spend money on almost every day?" This opens the confession naturally and makes the class feel safe and personal.

The Conversation Engine
Phase 4 · 10 Questions · 4 Blocks
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 · Past Experience
Q1
What did you buy this week that was maybe not necessary? How much did you pay?
Teacher Tip Very concrete and immediate. Most people have something from this week. "Was it food? A subscription? Something online? Did you think about it before you bought it — or was it fast?"
Q2
When was the last time you looked at your bank account and thought: "Where did all my money go?"
Model Answer "Last Monday. I had $4,000 pesos on Friday. On Monday I had $1,800. I looked at the transactions. Coffee, Uber, food delivery, a shirt. All small. All together — big."
Q3
Did you ever try to stop spending money on something — and then go back to it? What happened?
Teacher Tip The relapse story — everyone has one. "What did you try to stop? Eating out? A subscription? Smoking? How long did you last? What made you go back?" Generates authentic storytelling with simple past.
Block 2 · Opinion
Q4
What is more difficult — to stop buying things you like, or to earn more money? Why?
Teacher Tip Classic "spend less vs. earn more" debate — accessible at A2.1. "Which one is more realistic for you personally right now?" No academic vocabulary needed — just honest simple-present opinions.
Q5
Is it OK to spend money on things that make you happy — like coffee or a nice dinner — even when you don't have a lot of money?
Model Answer "Yes, I think it's OK. But there is a limit. One coffee a day — OK. Five coffees a day — not OK. Happiness doesn't need to be expensive."
Q6
Do you think subscriptions — Netflix, Spotify, Amazon — are worth the money? Or do you pay for things you don't use?
Teacher Tip Very relevant for Mexican professionals. "How many subscriptions do you have? Do you use all of them? When did you last check? Is there one you pay for and forget?" Often generates a surprised reaction.
Block 3 · Future Plans
Q7
Are you going to change one spending habit this month? Which one — and how?
Model Answer "I'm going to stop ordering food delivery on weekdays. I'm going to cook at home Monday to Friday. On weekends — fine, I can order. I think this is going to save me about $1,500 pesos a month."
Q8
Will you look at your bank transactions after this class? Are you scared to see the numbers?
Teacher Tip Funny and honest "will" question. "Are you going to open the app right now? Or are you going to wait until Monday?" Most students laugh because they know the answer. The humor makes the language natural.
Block 4 · Honest Reflection
Q9
Be honest — do you know exactly how much money you spend every month? Or is it a mystery?
Teacher Tip Most people don't know. That's the honest answer and it's fine. "Is it a number you know, or do you only find out when the money is gone? Do you want to know — or is it better not to?"
Q10
Your phone tells you: "You spend $2,800 pesos a month on food delivery." What is your reaction?
Model Answer "I say: 'No way. That is not possible.' Then I look at the list. And it IS possible. And I am embarrassed. And I close the app. And I order pizza that night anyway."

The Exit Shot
Phase 5 · Final Situation
⏱ 5 min
💣 Exit Shot
You want to save $5,000 pesos in the next 2 months. But right now you spend about $1,200 pesos a month on coffee, delivery food, and small things you don't really need. Are you going to change that — or is $5,000 pesos not possible for you right now?
