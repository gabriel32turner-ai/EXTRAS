TikTok: Fun or
Waste of Time?

You open TikTok for 5 minutes. One hour later, you are still there. Why? And is that a problem — or is it OK?

🪝
The Trigger
Phase 1 · Let's Talk Starter
⏱ 4 min
A simple fact to start
"The average person spends 52 minutes on TikTok every day. That is more than 6 hours every week — and more than 13 full days every year. Just on TikTok. Do you think that is too much?"
Teacher Script: Read the numbers slowly. Then ask: "13 days a year — do you think that number is correct for you? More? Less?" Then: "Do you have TikTok? Do you use it every day?" For A2.2 students who don't use TikTok, pivot to: "What about YouTube, Instagram Reels, or Facebook videos? Same idea." Make it immediately personal.

Ask: "Is Alejandro right — is TV the same as TikTok? Or is it different?" And: "Would you like to have 80,000 followers? What would your TikTok be about?"

The Conversation Engine
Phase 4 · 10 Questions · 4 Blocks
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 · Personal Experience — Past Simple
Q1
Did you watch TikTok or short videos last night? What did you watch?
Target: past simple (watched, saw, laughed, learned). Push: "How long? What type of video? Was it funny, educational, or just random?" Keep follow-up questions very short. Let the student describe the content they actually watched — this is real, personal, and vocabulary-rich.
Q2
Did you ever learn something useful from a TikTok or social media video? What did you learn?
Connects to Valeria's bubble. Push: "Did you try it? Did it work?" Target: past simple + simple result. Model: "Yes! I watched a video about how to fix a leaking pipe. I tried it at home and it worked. It saved me money." Gets students thinking positively about screen content.
Q3
When was the last time you opened your phone for "5 minutes" and 1 hour passed? Where were you?
Very universal experience. Push: "How did you feel after? Guilty? Fine? Tired?" Target: past simple + location/time. Model: "Last Sunday. I was in bed. I wanted to check one video and then — two hours later I was still there. I don't know what happened." Gets students laughing at themselves.
Block 2 · Opinion
Q4
Do you think TikTok is good for young people — or is it bad for them?
Simple opinion with two clear positions. Push: "What age is OK? 12? 16? 18?" Target: I think it's good / I think it's bad + because. Model: "I think it's OK for adults. But for children under 13 — no. They don't know what is real and what is not. It's dangerous for them."
Q5
Is it OK to be on your phone during dinner — or is that rude?
Clear, accessible opinion question. Push: "What about at a restaurant? At home with family?" Target: it's OK / it's rude + because. Model: "I think it's rude. Dinner is family time. But I do it sometimes — especially if I'm bored!" The honest contradiction is very human and good for A2.2.
Q6
Do you think Rodrigo is right — is TikTok only for young people? Or can adults enjoy it too?
Direct engagement with Rodrigo's position. Push: "Do you know any adults who use TikTok a lot? How do you feel about that?" Target: I think / I don't think + age/generation vocabulary. Model: "I don't think age matters. My mom is 60 and she loves cooking videos on TikTok. She doesn't care if it's 'for young people.'"
Block 3 · Future Plans — Going to / Will
Q7
Are you going to try to use your phone less next week — or is that impossible for you?
Target: going to + honest assessment. Push: "What will you do instead? Read? Sleep? Talk to someone?" Model: "I'm going to try. I want to stop using TikTok after 9pm. I think I can do it. Maybe. I'm not sure." The realistic uncertainty is very natural and good for A2.2 authenticity.
Q8
If you made a TikTok channel, what will it be about? What are you good at?
Fun, imaginative question. Target: will + infinitive for future plan. Push: "Who will your audience be? How many followers do you want?" Model: "I will make videos about my dog. He is very funny and does strange things. I think people will love him." Gets students enthusiastic and producing future-tense language naturally.
Block 4 · Past Habits — Used to
Q9
What did you use to do before you had a smartphone — how did you spend free time?
Target: used to + past simple. Very rich for personal stories. Push: "Do you miss those times? Was it better?" Model: "I used to read more. I used to talk to my friends on the phone — real calls, not messages. I used to watch one TV show and then stop. Now I watch 4 episodes and don't sleep." Nostalgic and real.
Q10
Did you use to think social media was a bad thing — and then change your mind?
Reflection question. Target: used to + past simple + now + present simple. Model: "Yes! I used to think Facebook was stupid and for old people. Then I used it to find my old friends from school. Now I think it's useful — for some things. But I still think TikTok is too much." Simple but meaningful opinion evolution.
💣
The Exit Shot
Phase 5 · One Final Question
⏱ 5 min
Final Question
Look at your phone screen time right now. How many hours did you spend on your phone today? Are you happy with that number — or are you going to change something tomorrow?
Homework Loop: "Take a screenshot of your screen time today. Then record a 60-second WhatsApp voice message. Tell me the number — and tell me one thing you are going to do differently tomorrow. In English. Simple words are fine."
