B1.1 Daily Life & Lifestyle 🌡 Medium 50 min

The Worst Weekend of My Life

Lost luggage. Sick in a hotel. A cancelled trip. A family disaster. We all have one. It is time to tell the story.

🪝
Phase 1
The Trigger
⏱ 4 min
"A study of 3,000 people found that 68% of adults have a 'holiday disaster' story — a trip or weekend that went completely wrong. And most people say those bad experiences become their favorite stories to tell years later."
— YouGov / The Guardian Travel Survey
Teacher script: "Think about the worst weekend or trip you have ever had. Maybe something went wrong, maybe everything went wrong." [Pause.] "Today we are going to tell those stories. The more disastrous, the better. Who wants to go first?"

Phase 4
The Conversation Engine
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 — Personal Experience
Q1
What is the worst trip or weekend you have ever had? Tell me what happened.
The core question — let them tell the whole story first. Don't interrupt. When they finish, ask follow-up questions: "And then what happened?" / "How did you feel?" This is pure past simple storytelling practice.
Q2
Have you ever missed a flight, a bus, or a train? What did you do?
Very relatable. Present perfect opener + past simple story. Model: "I've missed a flight once. I arrived 10 minutes late and the gate was already closed. I had to sleep in the airport." Listen for problem-solving language: "So I decided to..."
Q3
Have you ever got sick during a holiday or important plan? What was it like?
Physical disaster angle — very common. Good for "I felt..." / "I had to..." / "I couldn't...". Encourage humor if appropriate. Being sick abroad or away from home is a universal misery that everyone relates to.
Block 2 — Opinion
Q4
Do you think bad experiences make you stronger — or do they just make you more careful next time?
Good for "I think..." + "because..." structure. No complex grammar needed. Push: "Did your worst weekend teach you something? Or was it just bad luck?" This is where Mateo's opinion (bad trips = best stories) comes in.
Q5
Is it better to travel with a plan or be spontaneous? Why?
Fernando's provocateur angle. "It is better to... because..." Good for comparative structures. Some students are planners, some are spontaneous — both sides are interesting. Ask: "What is the risk of each approach?"
Q6
What do you think is worse: a bad trip, a bad day at work, or a bad family event?
Ranking question — good for comparatives: "I think... is worse than... because..." Lucía's opinion (family dinner) vs travel disasters. Students have strong instincts here. Let them debate.
Block 3 — Hypothetical
Q7
What would you do if you arrived at your hotel and it was completely different from the photos online?
Second conditional. Very current — happens all the time with booking apps. "If I arrived and the hotel was terrible... I would..." Push: "Would you complain? Ask for money back? Just stay and accept it?" Reveals personality nicely.
Q8
What would you do if your bag with all your money and documents was stolen during a trip?
High-stakes hypothetical. "If my bag was stolen... I would first..." Good for sequencing: "First I would... then I would... and finally..." Forces practical thinking in English. Very engaging for B1.1 students.
Block 4 — Provocative
Q9
Do you think people complain too much when things go wrong on a trip — or is it normal to feel frustrated?
Fernando's perspective. Surprising question — most students expect to be validated, not questioned. "Is it okay to be upset, or should you just accept it and move on?" Creates good debate without requiring complex language.
Q10
What is the worst weekend or trip you have never talked about — until now?
The self-disclosure bomb. Simple grammar, big emotional impact. Some students will laugh. Some will go quiet. The best ones will tell you the real story. Give them space. This is where class connection happens.
💣
Phase 5
The Exit Shot
⏱ 5 min
Final Question
You planned a trip for 6 months. You saved money, you booked everything, you were excited. The day you arrive, it rains for 3 days straight, the hotel is terrible, and you argue with the person you travelled with. You still have 4 days left. Do you go home early — or do you stay and try to enjoy it?
