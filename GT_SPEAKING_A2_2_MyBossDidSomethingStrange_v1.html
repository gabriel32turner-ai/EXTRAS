My Boss Did
Something Strange

Funny, weird, or uncomfortable — bosses do things we don't understand. Let's talk about it.

🪝
The Trigger
Phase 1 · Open the conversation
4 min
A fun question to start
"Describe your boss in ONE word. Just one. Think for 5 seconds... now say it."
Teacher script: Point to each student quickly — they say one word only. Write the words on the board or just listen. Common words: "nice," "serious," "strange," "busy," "angry," "funny." Then say: "OK — who said 'strange' or 'funny'? Tell me one thing your boss did that surprised you." That opens the session perfectly.

The Conversation Engine
Phase 4 · 10 questions · 4 blocks
23 min
Block 1 — Past Experience
Q1
Did your boss ever do something strange or funny at work? What happened?
Teacher tip: This is the main storytelling question. Give time. Help with: "One time, my boss..." / "It was strange because..." / "I didn't know what to do, so I..." Encourage 4–5 simple sentences.
Q2
Did your boss ever say something to you that you didn't understand? What did you do?
Model answer: "Yes! One time my boss called me to his office and said 'We need to have a conversation about your future here.' I was very scared. But then he just wanted to tell me about a new project. I said 'Oh! OK!' and I felt much better."
Q3
When was the last time your boss made you laugh? What did they do?
Teacher tip: Lighter question. If a student says "never," that's also interesting: "Your boss never makes you laugh? Is that OK or does it make work more serious and difficult?"
Block 2 — Opinion
Q4
Do you think it's good when a boss is funny and relaxed — or is it better when a boss is serious?
Teacher tip: Clear two-option debate. Help with: "I think a funny boss is better because..." / "I prefer a serious boss because..." Most will want a mix — push: "But if you had to choose only one?"
Q5
Is it OK when a boss sends work messages after work hours — at night or on weekends?
Model answer: "No, I don't think it's OK. After work, it's my time. I want to rest and be with my family. Work is important but it's not my whole life. My boss can wait until the next day."
Q6
Do you think bosses should give small gifts or celebrations for birthdays at work — like Mateo's boss?
Teacher tip: Reference Mateo's opinion. Very relatable — most Mexican workplaces have birthday traditions. Push: "Does your company do something for birthdays? What do they do?"
Block 3 — Future Plans
Q7
Are you going to talk to your boss about something important this week or next week?
Teacher tip: Simple "going to" practice. Common answers: a salary question, a vacation request, a work problem. Help: "I'm going to talk to my boss about..." + the topic. Good for practicing professional vocabulary simply.
Q8
If you are a boss in the future, will you be similar to your current boss — or very different?
Model answer: "I will be very different. My boss never says 'good job.' I will tell my team when they do good work. I think that's important. People need to hear that they are doing well."
Block 4 — Past Habits & Reflection
Q9
Did you use to have a different type of boss before — and which one was better, the old boss or the current one?
Teacher tip: "Used to" + comparison. Help with: "My old boss used to be..." + adjective. Then: "My current boss is..." + adjective. Students love comparing bosses — it's a rich topic at A2.2.
Q10
Think about your boss right now. What is one thing they do that you don't understand?
Teacher tip: Great closing question. Simple and honest. Help students with: "My boss always/never... and I don't understand why." Accept funny answers — the lighter the better at the end of the session.
💣
The Exit Shot
Phase 5 · One final question
5 min
💣 Exit Shot
Describe your boss to your partner in exactly 5 sentences. Use these words: name, personality, one good thing, one bad thing, and one strange or funny thing.
