My Car

Your car is part of your daily life — describe it in English.

🎪 Show & Tell 5 min

Show a photo of your car (or your dream car) and describe it.

Your Task

Open your phone. Find a photo of your car. If you don't have a car, find a photo of your dream car on the internet. Show it and describe it.

Teacher Script — Model First

"Look — this is MY car. It is a Honda Civic. It is gray. It is old — 2017! But it is good. I like it. It is comfortable and it is my friend for the road. OK — show me YOUR car. What is it?"

Sentence Starters

This is my car. It is a _______ .
It is _______ (color).
It is new / old / big / small.
I like it because it is _______ .

Teacher Tip

Cars are a HUGE engagement topic for Mexican professionals. Men especially will talk for 5 minutes if you let them. Control the time but ride the energy. If they don't have a car: "OK, your dream car — what is it? A BMW? A Toyota? Describe it!" Also works with "my company car" for corporate students.

Teacher Tip

The gas vs. electric pair generates strong opinions — even at A1.1. Let them struggle to explain WHY with "to be" sentences. "Electric is expensive!" "Gas is cheap!" "Electric is the future!" — all valid A1.1 output. Push for 3 sentences per pair minimum.

🃏 Question Cards 12 min

Teacher models the answer first. Then student personalizes.

Question 01
What is your car like?
Now you: describe YOUR car — brand, color, year.
Teacher Model

"My car is a Honda Civic 2017. It is gray. It is small but comfortable. The engine is good — it is reliable. I like it."

Teacher Tip

Students at manufacturing companies often know car brands very well. Let them geek out. Push for adjectives: "Is it fast? Is it comfortable? Is it clean right now or dirty?"

Question 02
Is traffic bad in your city?
Now you: how is traffic where YOU are?
Teacher Model

"Traffic in my city is terrible at 8 AM. It is slow and boring. My commute is 40 minutes. It is not fun."

Teacher Tip

Teach "commute" as a bonus chunk: "My commute is 40 minutes." Don't explain it as a verb — just as a chunk. "How many minutes is your commute?" generates good number practice.

Question 03
What is your dream car?
Now you: what is YOUR perfect car?
Teacher Model

"My dream car is a BMW X5. It is black. It is big and fast. It is very expensive — maybe $60,000! But it is beautiful."

Teacher Tip

This one is gold — students light up talking about dream cars. Push for price: "How much is it? Is it $50,000? $100,000?" Great number + adjective combo practice.

Question 04
Is your car clean or dirty right now?
Now you: what is YOUR car like TODAY?
Teacher Model

"Hmm... my car right now is dirty! It is terrible. There is dust everywhere. But inside it is clean. I am organized inside but lazy outside."

Teacher Tip

Fun honest question — people laugh about their dirty cars. Push for "inside vs outside" contrast: "Inside is ______ but outside is ______." Great use of "but" and "there is".

Question 05
Who is the best driver in your family?
Now you: who is the best — and the WORST?
Teacher Model

"The best driver in my family is my brother. He is calm and careful. The worst? My mom — she is fast and a little scary! But she is fun."

Teacher Tip

This always gets laughs. Family + cars = strong opinions. Push for adjectives: "Is your dad fast or slow? Is your wife careful or scary?" Drill "careful" pronunciation — CAR-ful, not "care-FOOL".

Question 06
How important is your car for your work?
Now you: is YOUR car essential or optional?
Teacher Model

"My car is very important for my work. There is no bus to my office. My car is not a luxury — it is a necessity. Without my car, there is no work!"

Teacher Tip

Good closer linking daily life to professional context. "Necessity" and "luxury" are cognates — easy wins. Push: "Is your car a luxury or a necessity?" This is a strong sentence they can build naturally.

✨ My Sentence 5 min

Build one perfect sentence. Practice it. Record it.

My car is a _______ and it is _______ for me because _______ .

🎙️ Record this sentence as a WhatsApp voice note (30 seconds).

Teacher Tip

Example target: "My car is a Nissan Versa and it is important for me because there is no bus to my office." This is a 20-word sentence at A1.1 — if they produce this, celebrate HARD. Three repetitions for fluency, then record. This is their trophy.
