My Desk at Work

Describe the things on your desk. Your workspace, your words, your English.

🎪 Show & Tell 💉 Vocab 🔀 Choose & Speak 🃏 Questions ✨ My Sentence
Phase 1
🎪 Show & Tell
Show something from your desk — describe it
5 min
Teacher Models First
"OK, look at my desk. This is my coffee cup — it is white and it is big. I like it because it is MY cup. And this is my notebook. It is old but it is important. OK, now — show me something from YOUR desk. One thing."
Student Sentence Starters
This is my ___ (cup / notebook / pen / computer).
It is ___ (big / small / new / old).
It is ___ (color).
I like it because it is ___.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • If student is remote: "Show me your desk on camera!" If in-person: pick up an object and show it.
  • This works because it is REAL and IMMEDIATE — no abstraction, no imagination needed.
  • Common: student picks up phone. Say: "Ah, we already described phones! Pick something different — your cup? Your pen?"
  • Accept: "This is mouse" → recast "This is MY mouse, yes!"
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Oh, that is a nice cup! What color is it?" — keep it conversational and real.
  • "You are describing your office in English. Your coworkers cannot do that!"
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Every time you sit at your desk now, you can name everything in English. That is real progress."

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • MODEL pair 1: "I choose CLEAN DESK. A clean desk is important for me. I am an organized person. A messy desk is stressful!"
  • "Clean vs messy" is GOLD for manufacturing professionals — they relate immediately to 5S and workplace organization.
  • If student uses "I prefer" — accept it as a chunk, but model "I choose" for consistency.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Me too! Coffee at my desk is essential. I am a coffee person."
  • Laugh with them about messy desks. Humor builds retention.
Phase 4
🃏 Question Cards
Teacher models first — then you answer
12 min
Question 1
What is on your desk right now?
"On my desk there is a computer, a notebook, and a coffee cup. There are two pens. It is a normal desk."
Now you: "On my desk there is..."
Tip: Teach "there is" (one thing) vs "there are" (many things). Point at real objects. Count them together.
Question 2
Is your desk clean or messy?
"My desk is... messy! There are papers everywhere. It is terrible. But I am comfortable with it."
Now you: "My desk is... because..."
Tip: This gets laughs. Let them be honest. If they say "es un desastre" — model: "It is a disaster!"
Question 3
Is your chair comfortable?
"No! My chair is terrible. It is old and it is not comfortable. I want a new chair."
Now you: "My chair is..."
Tip: "I want" is acceptable as a memorized chunk. If they say "I need" — also fine as a chunk.
Question 4
Where is your office? Is it big or small?
"My office is in the third floor. It is small but it is nice. There are 4 desks. My desk is near the window."
Now you: "My office is..."
Tip: Teach "near the window" / "near the door" as location chunks. Point and gesture.
Question 5
What is the most important thing on your desk?
"The most important thing on my desk is my computer. It is essential for my work. Without it — impossible!"
Now you: "The most important thing is..."
Tip: This previews the My Sentence structure. Scaffold if needed: "Is it your computer? Your phone? Your coffee?"
Question 6
Is there a photo on your desk? Who is in the photo?
"Yes! There is a photo of my family. My wife and my two kids. They are beautiful. That photo is my motivation."
Now you: "There is a... on my desk" / "There is no photo on my desk."
Tip: If no photo: "There is no photo — but there is a [plant/cup/nothing]!" Turn it into a conversation, not a dead end.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • "There is / there are" is the grammar star of this session. Drill it naturally through desk objects.
  • Watch for: "In my desk" → "ON my desk." Preposition error — correct gently, gesture helps.
  • "My office is in the third floor" → "ON the third floor." Don't overcorrect — just recast.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "You just described your entire workspace in English. Your boss would be impressed!"
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Phone, family, desk — you can now describe three parts of your life in English. Imagine what you can do in 3 months."
  • Testimonial trigger: "If your HR manager asks — can you describe your workspace in English? Now YES."
Phase 5
✨ My Sentence
Build one perfect sentence — practice it — record it
5 min
Complete This Sentence
The most important thing on my desk is my ___ because it is ___ for my work.
📱 WhatsApp Homework: Record this sentence as a voice note (30 seconds). Say it with confidence. Send it to your teacher.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Model: "The most important thing on my desk is my computer because it is essential for my work."
  • Alternative: "...is my coffee because it is necessary for my morning!"
  • Correct pronunciation: "im-POR-tant", "be-CAUSE", "ES-sen-tial".
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Three sessions, three perfect sentences. You are building a collection of YOUR English."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Phone, family, desk — done. Next: your city, your food, your weekend. You are unstoppable."
  • "Send that voice note. Your teacher wants to hear YOUR sentence."
