Tourists in Mexico:
Love or Hate?

They take photos of everything, they speak loud, and they always ask for the check in English. What do you really think?

🪝
The Trigger
Phase 1 · Open the conversation
4 min
Mexico Tourism Board · 2023
"Mexico received 42 million international tourists last year — the 6th most visited country in the world. Tourism brings money but also problems."
Teacher script: Read the stat. Then: "42 million tourists. That's a lot of people with cameras and questions. Think about your city or a tourist place near you. Do you see tourists? What do you think when you see them?" Let 2–3 students react quickly before moving to vocab.

The Conversation Engine
Phase 4 · 10 questions · 4 blocks
23 min
Block 1 — Past Experience
Q1
Did you ever talk to a tourist in your city? What happened?
Teacher tip: This is a great English speaking motivation question. Push: "Did you speak to them in English or Spanish? Were they polite? What did they ask you?" Many students will have stories about helping lost tourists.
Q2
Did you ever see a tourist do something strange or rude in Mexico? What did they do?
Model answer: "Yes. Last year at a market, a tourist took photos of old women without asking. The women were uncomfortable. I wanted to say something to the tourist but I didn't. I just watched. I felt bad."
Q3
Did you ever go to a very tourist place in Mexico — like Cancún, Los Cabos, or Tulum? What did you think about it?
Teacher tip: Very relatable for Mexican professionals. Push: "Was it expensive? Were there many foreigners? Did you feel like a tourist in your own country?"
Block 2 — Opinion
Q4
Do you think tourism is good or bad for Mexico?
Teacher tip: Push for both sides: "Tell me one good thing and one bad thing." Model: "Tourism is good because it brings money. But it's bad because prices go up and the culture changes. It's both."
Q5
Is it good when tourists try to speak Spanish — or is it better when they speak English?
Model answer: "I think it's much better when they try Spanish. Even if it's bad Spanish — it shows respect. When tourists only speak English and expect everyone to understand, it's a little rude. We are in Mexico, not in the US."
Q6
Do you think Mexican people are friendly with tourists — or not always?
Teacher tip: Push for honesty. Most students will say yes — Mexicans are friendly. Then ask: "But is it always real — or sometimes is it because they want to sell something?" Good honest discussion.
Block 3 — Future Plans
Q7
Are you going to visit a tourist place in Mexico this year? Where?
Teacher tip: "Going to" practice. If they have no plans, push: "OK — if you can go anywhere in Mexico, free, tomorrow — where do you go?"
Q8
If a foreign tourist asks you for help in your city, will you help them? What are you going to say?
Model answer: "Yes, I will help them. I'm going to try to speak English. My English is not perfect, but I will try. I'm going to say: 'I can help you. Where do you want to go?' And then I'm going to use my phone for the directions."
Block 4 — Provocative
Q9
Be honest — when YOU travel to another country, do you do things that are rude or strange for the locals?
Teacher tip: Flip the perspective — now the student IS the tourist. Common honest answers: "I take photos of everything," "I speak Spanish loudly," "I don't know the rules." Celebrate honesty.
Q10
A tourist from the US asks you: "Is Mexico really safe to visit?" What are you going to say?
Teacher tip: Rich and personally important question. Push for a real answer with "going to": "What are you going to say? Are you going to say yes, no, or something in the middle?"

The Exit Shot
Phase 5 · One final question
5 min
💣 Exit Shot
A tourist from Canada stops you on the street. He speaks no Spanish. He is lost, he looks worried, and he needs to get to the bus station. You speak some English. What are you going to do?

Homework — WhatsApp Voice Note
