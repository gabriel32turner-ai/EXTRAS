The lazy one, the loud one, the one who takes credit. Let's talk about them.

Gallup · Workplace Survey
"1 in 3 workers says a difficult coworker is the biggest problem at their job — bigger than salary, hours, or the boss."
Teacher script: Read the stat. Then: "1 in 3. That's a lot of people. Think about your job right now — or your last job. Is there one person who is difficult? Just one?" Let two students answer in 2 sentences. Don't go deep yet — that's Phase 4. Keep it fast and fun.

The Conversation Engine
Phase 4 · 10 questions · 4 blocks
23 min
Block 1 — Past Experience
Q1
Think about a difficult coworker — past or present. What did they do that you didn't like?
Teacher tip: Help with past simple starters: "She always..." / "He never..." / "One day he..." Push for a specific moment, not just a general description. "Give me one specific day — what exactly happened?"
Q2
Did you ever fight or have a problem directly with a coworker? What happened?
Model answer: "Yes. Last year a coworker was very rude to me in a meeting. She said my work was wrong — in front of everyone. I was very embarrassed. After the meeting I talked to her. She said sorry, but it was not sincere."
Q3
What did you do the last time a coworker did something that made you angry?
Teacher tip: Common answers: "I said nothing," "I talked to my boss," "I complained to another colleague." Push: "And did that help? Or did the problem continue?"
Block 2 — Opinion
Q4
What is worse — a lazy coworker or a rude coworker? Why?
Teacher tip: Force a choice. If a student says "both," ask: "Yes, but if you have to choose one — which one is worse for YOU at work?" Model: "I think rude is worse. Lazy is a problem for the company. Rude is a problem for ME every day."
Q5
Do you think it's a good idea to talk directly to a difficult coworker — or is it better to talk to the boss?
Model answer: "I think it's better to talk to the coworker first. The boss is the last option. When you go to the boss first, the coworker gets angry and the situation is worse. But it's difficult. Not everyone can do it."
Q6
Do you think lazy people change — or do they stay lazy forever?
Teacher tip: Fun opinion question. Most will say "no, they don't change." Push for evidence: "Why do you think that? Did you see this at your job?"
Block 3 — Future Plans
Q7
Are you going to do anything different with a difficult coworker this week or next week?
Teacher tip: Practice "going to." Help: "I'm going to try to..." / "I'm going to say..." / "I'm not going to do anything — I prefer to avoid them." All answers are valid.
Q8
If a coworker takes credit for your work again, what are you going to say?
Model answer: "I'm going to speak in the meeting. I'm going to say: 'Thank you for adding to my idea.' And I'm going to look at my boss when I say it. I'm not going to be rude — just clear."
Block 4 — Provocative
Q9
Be honest — do YOU do something at work that maybe a coworker doesn't like?
Teacher tip: The best question of the session. Give 10 seconds of silence before asking for answers. Common honest answers: "I eat lunch at my desk and it smells," "I talk too much," "I'm always late to meetings." Celebrate honesty — it produces the best language.
Q10
Your best friend starts working at your office next week. Is that good or bad news?
Teacher tip: Fun closing scenario. Push students to think about the complications: "What do you think is going to happen? Good things? Bad things?" Model: "It's good news and bad news. Good — I have a friend at work. Bad — what happens if we fight? It's complicated."

The Exit Shot
Phase 5 · One final question
5 min
💣 Exit Shot
A coworker took credit for your project in a meeting today. Your boss congratulated that person — not you. Tomorrow morning you are going to see that coworker in the office. What are you going to say?

Homework — WhatsApp Voice Note
Record a 60-second voice message in English. Tell the story of a difficult coworker — past or present. What did they do? How did you feel? What did you do? Use past simple: "He/She always...", "One day...", "I was angry because..."
