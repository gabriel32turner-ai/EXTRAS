The Trigger
Phase 1 · Provocation & Opening
⏱ 4 min
BBC Money · 2023
"Studies show that most couples fight about money more than anything else — and the number one cause is not how much they earn, but how differently they think about spending it."
Teacher script: Read it. Then ask: "Are you a spender or a saver? Be honest." Wait for quick answers. Then: "Think about someone close to you — your partner, your family, your best friend. Are they the same as you or the opposite? Does that cause problems?" Keep it light and personal to start.

The Conversation Engine
Phase 4 · 10 Questions · 4 Blocks
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 · Personal Experience
Q1
Have you ever spent money on something and felt really bad about it later? What was it?
Teacher Tip Almost everyone has a story. Push for specifics: "How much was it? Did you tell anyone? Do you still have it — or did you throw it away?" The embarrassment is where the best language comes out.
Q2
Have you ever had a moment in your life when you had no money at all? What happened?
Model Answer "Yes. When I first moved to the city, I had $200 pesos for the whole week. I ate a lot of rice. I remember checking my phone to see if my salary came in, like every 10 minutes. It was horrible — but it taught me a lot."
Q3
Did your parents teach you about money when you were young? What did they say?
Teacher Tip Great for childhood memories and family language. Push: "Did they show you how to save, or did they just say 'money doesn't grow on trees'? Did you follow their advice — or do the opposite?"
Block 2 · Opinion
Q4
Is it OK to spend all your money if you enjoy it — as long as you pay your bills? Or do you always need to save?
Teacher Tip No wrong answer — push them to commit and defend it. "If you spend everything and you get sick or lose your job, what happens? Is that a personal problem or a social problem?"
Q5
Do you think spenders and savers can be happy together in a relationship? Or is money a deal-breaker?
Model Answer "I think it is very hard. My sister is a saver and her husband spends everything. They fight about money almost every week. Love is real — but so are the bills."
Q6
Do you believe rich people are rich because they are smart with money — or because they were lucky?
Teacher Tip Fun debate question. Push both sides: "Is there anyone you know personally who is rich from their own decisions? What did they do?" Keeps it real and B1.1 friendly.
Block 3 · Hypothetical
Q7
What would you do if you got $50,000 pesos right now — a bonus, a gift, anything? Be honest.
Model Answer "Honestly? I would pay my credit card. Then maybe take a trip with my family. And try to save $10,000. But the truth is I don't know — I've never had $50,000 pesos extra in my life."
Q8
What would you do if your partner spent all your shared savings on something you didn't agree with?
Teacher Tip Strong emotional scenario. Push for the real reaction: "Would you argue? Leave? Forgive? How long would you be angry?" The emotion drives authentic language at B1.1.
Block 4 · Provocative
Q9
Are you happy with how you use your money right now — or do you wish you did things differently?
Teacher Tip The honest self-reflection question. Give real space. Most people will say they wish they saved more. Push: "What stops you? Is it a habit, a personality thing, or something else?"
Q10
If your friends saw your bank account right now, would you be embarrassed — or proud?
Model Answer "Embarrassed. Definitely. Not because I spend on bad things — but because I don't have much saved. And I know why. I just don't want to admit it."

The Exit Shot
Phase 5 · Final Dilemma · No right answer
⏱ 5 min
💣 Exit Shot
You have $20,000 pesos saved. It took you one year. Your best friend calls you — they need the money for a family emergency. If you give it, you start from zero again. If you don't, they will be in serious trouble. What do you do?
