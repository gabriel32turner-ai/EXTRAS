§ 01 · The Trigger
🪝 How Mexico Pays
Read aloud · First reaction · No debate yet · 4 min max

87%

54%

3×

Sources: Banco de México Encuesta de Hábitos de Pago 2023 · CONDUSEF · Forbes México
🪝 Opening Question — Read and point at someone
"This morning — did you pay for something with cash or with a card?"
Teacher Mechanics — 4 min Very simple. Point at one student: "This morning — cash or card?" Short answers are perfect. 2–3 students. Then: "OK. Today we talk about that choice."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • The "3 times more spending with card" stat generates immediate recognition — many students have experienced this. Don't explain it — just show the number and let it land.
  • The trigger question is very simple by design — "this morning, cash or card?" requires only a one-word answer. That's perfect A2.2 activation.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "87% of payments in Mexico are cash. That means we are mostly a cash country. Is that you? Or are you different?"
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Retention: "Next lab — people who always ask for discounts. Another money topic, same level."

§ 03 · Opinion Wall
🧱 4 Real Voices. Cash vs Card.
Read each card · Pairs 60 sec · Group shares · Same question each time
03
10 min
Processing Question — Same for all 4
"Who do you agree with? Why?"
Keep it simple
🔁 Processing Question — Pairs → Group
"Who do you agree with? Why? Use 2 or 3 sentences."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • The provocateur is Don Aurelio — his "I don't trust cards" position is the most common among older Mexican adults and will generate a sympathetic or challenging reaction depending on the group's age range.
  • At A2.2, accepting "I agree with her because I also use cash" is perfect. Don't push for more complexity.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • After all 4: "And YOU — which one are you? Cash person or card person?" Point at each student for a 5-second answer.
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Testimonial trigger: "Which person sounds like someone in YOUR family?"
§ 04 · The Conversation Engine
🗣 10 Questions. 4 Blocks. 23 Minutes.
Past → Opinion → Future → Past habits · Short answers OK · Keep moving
04
23 min
⚡
A2.2 Rule: Short answers are OK. 1 or 2 sentences is great. Don't wait for perfect English — just talk.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Block 1 past simple — "When did you pay by card last?" Accept any past time expression: "yesterday," "last week," "on Friday." All correct.
  • Block 3 "going to" — "Are you going to use more cards next year?" Accept "I want to" as an equivalent at A2.2.
  • Block 4 "used to" — Model before asking: "When I was young, I USED TO pay everything in cash. Now I use my card. You?"
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • Q9 is the most self-revealing — "did you spend more money with a card?" Most students will say yes. Let the honesty be the celebration.
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • After Q10: "You talked about money in English for 23 minutes. That's not easy. That's A2.2."
§ 05 · The Exit Shot
💣 One Question. Your Answer.
5 min · No chips · Answer in 2–3 sentences · Then the loop
05
5 min
EXIT
💣 Exit Shot
Next month, are you going to try to use only cash for one week — no cards, no phone payments? Why or why not?
Answer in 2 or 3 sentences. Use "I am going to..." or "I am not going to... because..." Be honest.
📌 A2.2 Model Answer — Reveal AFTER student speaks
"I am going to try it. I think it is a good idea because I spend too much with my card. I am going to take 500 pesos on Monday and see how long it lasts."
🔁 Retention Loop — WhatsApp Homework "Before next session — record a 60-second voice note in English. Your answer to today's Exit Shot. Don't write it first. Just talk. Send it to [teacher number]."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • The Exit Shot is a personal future plan — very accessible at A2.2. "I am going to try" is the target. Accept "I want to try" as well.
  • If a student says "No, I'm not going to do it" — that's also great. Push: "Why not? One reason."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • The model answer has a specific detail: "500 pesos on Monday." Point out: "That detail — 500 pesos, Monday — makes it a REAL plan in English. Not just an idea."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Closing: "Record your answer tonight. 60 seconds. Tell me your plan in English."
