§ 01 · The Trigger
🪝 The Friendship Tax
Read aloud or project · First reaction only · No debate yet · 4 min max
01
4 min
🤝
SurveyMonkey Financial Friendship Study + Journal of Financial Therapy 2023
36%
Of friendships that involve a money loan end within 12 months — most without the debt being repaid
$800
The average amount people say they have lent to a friend and never got back — without asking for it again
67%
Of people have avoided a friend after borrowing money — because they felt too embarrassed to face them
1 in 4
Say their biggest financial regret is related to a friend — either lending, borrowing, or going into business together
Sources: SurveyMonkey Financial Friendship Report (2023) · Journal of Financial Therapy · Bankrate Personal Finance Survey
🪝 Opening Trigger — Read this, then point at someone
"Your best friend of 10 years asks to borrow money. You give it. Six months later, they still have not paid you back — but they just posted a photo of their new vacation on Instagram. What do you do?"
Teacher Mechanics — 4 min Read the scenario slowly. Let the Instagram detail land. Point at someone: "First reaction — what do you do?" Don't answer. Don't analyze. After 2–3 responses, say: "Everything we just said comes back in §4."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • The Instagram detail is intentional — it transforms a vague money story into a visual, specific betrayal. The reaction is almost always immediate and strong. Let it happen.
  • If someone asks "how much money?" — say: "Enough that it mattered to you. Does the amount change your answer?" That's actually Q4 in Block 2.
  • The 36% friendship-ending stat is the one that surprises people most. Watch who reacts — they'll lead §3 debate.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • This topic is almost universally personal. Most students have a real story about this. The session creates space to tell it — in English. That's valuable to them beyond the language.
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Retention hook: "This is the kind of topic you want to be able to discuss in English — with international colleagues, with people from other cultures. Money and friendship work differently across cultures. That's what we explore here."

🔵 10 Verbs — What Happens When Money Meets Friendship
⚡ Production Challenge — No preparation
Each person picks 3 words. One sentence each about a real situation with a friend or family member and money. Real story. No writing. Just talk.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • "Lend" vs "borrow" is the classic confusion — clarify once and move on: "I lend TO you. You borrow FROM me." Write it on the board if available.
  • Best production challenge trigger: "Which 3 words describe a real situation you've been in — or someone you know?"
  • If a student starts telling a full story during the production challenge — let them go 30 seconds max, then: "Save that. We need it in §4."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "These words describe real situations that affect real relationships. Not academic concepts — real life."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Knowing how to talk about money — respectfully, clearly, in English — is a professional skill. In international business, this conversation comes up all the time."
§ 03 · Opinion Wall
🧱 4 Real Voices. 4 Different Rules.
Read each card · Pairs discuss 90 sec · Group shares · Same question each time
03
10 min
Processing Question — Same for all 4 voices
"Who do you agree with most — and who would you argue with? Why?"
Don't vary the question
🔁 Processing Question — Pairs → Group
"Who do you agree with most — and who would you argue with? Why?"
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Read all 4 before opening. The contrast between Luis (never mix money and friends) and Patricia (always help your people) generates the strongest debate.
  • The provocateur is Carlos. His position — that if a friend doesn't pay back, they were never a real friend — sounds harsh but forces students to define what "real friend" means. Push: "Has this ever happened to you?"
  • Marisol's voice is the nuanced reframe. If students all agree with her too quickly, push: "But in practice — can you really separate love from money cleanly?"
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • Best activation if the group is quiet: read Carlos in a slightly cold, direct tone. Read Patricia warmly. The delivery creates instant contrast.
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Testimonial trigger: "One sentence — which of these four people describes how you actually behave with money and friends? Not what you believe — what you do."
§ 04 · The Conversation Engine
🗣 10 Questions. 4 Blocks. 23 Minutes.
Personal → Opinion → Hypothetical → Provocative · No round-robin · Point and push
04
23 min
⚡
Rule: Minimum 2 sentences per answer. Position first. Reason or real example second. One-word answers don't count.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Block 1 is present-perfect heavy — watch for "I lended" (irregular verb error). Correct once, quietly, then move on. The correct form is "I lent."
  • Block 3 second conditional: watch for "If I would lend" — note and recycle at the end only. Don't break the conversational flow.
  • Q10 (synthesis) — push hard if the answer is generic. This is the emotional peak of the session.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • When a student tells a real personal story about money and friendship — acknowledge the courage first: "That takes honesty to say. Now say it again — in a full English sentence."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • After Q10: "You just had a conversation about loyalty, trust, and money — in English. That is B1.2 level. That is a skill."
§ 05 · The Exit Shot
💣 One Question. No Easy Answer.
5 min · No chips · Just position + reason · Then the loop
05
5 min
EXIT
💣 Exit Shot
Your best friend of 15 years asks you to lend him 30,000 pesos to start a business. You have the money — but it is your emergency fund. You say yes. Two years later, the business failed. He has not paid you back. He has not mentioned it. Do you ask for the money — or do you let it go?
Answer in 3 sentences minimum. Be specific. What do you actually do — not what you think you should do?
📌 B1.2 Model Answer — Reveal AFTER student speaks
"I think I would let it go — but I would never lend that much money again. Not to anyone. The friendship is worth more than 30,000 pesos, but I've learned something about this person and about myself. I've always said 'don't mix money and friendship' but I've never really believed it — until now."
🔁 Retention Loop — The WhatsApp Homework "Before our next session — record a 60-second voice note in English. Your answer to today's Exit Shot. Don't write it first. Don't rehearse. Just talk. Send it to [teacher number]."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • The amount (30,000 pesos) and the detail (your emergency fund, 15-year friendship) make this impossible to answer abstractly. Push students who give clean, resolved answers: "And the friendship — is it the same after?"
  • One follow-up only after the main answer: "What would you do differently if you could go back to the moment he asked?" Second conditional activated naturally.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • The model answer ends with a shift — "I've never really believed it — until now." That kind of personal insight in English is the goal of this level. Point it out: "That last sentence — 'until now' — is what B1.2 sounds like."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Closing line: "You just talked about loyalty, betrayal, and forgiveness — in English. Not just vocabulary. Real thinking."
  • Upsell: "Next Speaking Lab: Jealousy and Success — what happens when your best friend earns twice your salary. Same honesty. Different topic."
