WhatsApp Groups I Hate
The family group. The work group. The school group. 200 unread messages and none of them are for you. Everyone knows this feeling.
🪝
Phase 1
The Trigger
One number. Everyone in the room knows this feeling.
⏱ 4 min
Statista · WhatsApp Usage Mexico · 2023
"Mexico is one of the top countries in the world for WhatsApp use. The average Mexican user is in 8 different WhatsApp groups. Most people say at least 3 of them are annoying."
WhatsApp groups are a big part of daily life in Mexico — for family, work, school, neighbors, church, and more. Some groups are useful. But many groups are full of good morning messages, chain letters, political memes, and people who write at 6am. Sound familiar?
Teacher Script Ask immediately: "How many WhatsApp groups are you in right now? Count them. Tell me the number." Let them check their phones. Compare numbers — usually 8 to 25. Then: "Which group do you hate the most? Don't say the name — just describe it." That's your opening. Huge laughter guaranteed.

Phase 4
The Conversation Engine
10 questions. Real stories. Your actual phone. Short answers are great.
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 · Personal Experience (Past Simple)
Q1
What was the most annoying message you got in a WhatsApp group this week? Describe it.
Teacher Tip Past simple description. Almost everyone has something from this week. "My aunt sent a video about politics at 7am." "Someone sent 15 voice notes in the work group." Keep it fun and specific — the more details, the better the story.
Q2
Did you ever leave a WhatsApp group? What happened after?
Teacher Tip Past simple — the drama of leaving. "Yes, I left the neighborhood group. Nobody called me." "I left a school group and the teacher was angry." "I left and my mother called me five minutes later." These stories always produce laughter and great language.
Q3
Did someone add you to a group without asking? What did you do?
Teacher Tip Past simple reaction. "Yes, my cousin added me to a cooking group. I stayed but I never write." "Someone added me to a political group. I left immediately." This is a very common and relatable experience.
Block 2 · Opinion (Simple Present)
Q4
Do you think it's okay for your boss to send work messages on Sunday? Why or why not?
Teacher Tip "I think it's not okay because Sunday is my free day." "I think it depends on the job — maybe in an emergency it's okay." Target: one clear opinion + because. Rodrigo's story connects here directly.
Q5
Is it rude to leave a WhatsApp group? Or is it just a decision?
Teacher Tip Opinion with "I think it's rude" / "I think it's just a decision." Push for the reason: "It's rude because people see you left." "It's not rude because it's your phone." In Mexico, leaving a family group is often a big social event — students know this very well.
Q6
What is more annoying — good morning messages, political memes, or voice notes? Choose one and explain.
Teacher Tip Comparative opinion — "I think [x] is more annoying because..." This is a fun debate question. Voice notes often win because of their length. Accept any answer — the goal is producing "more... than" or "because" sentences at A2.1 level.
Block 3 · Future Plans (Going to / Will)
Q7
Are you going to leave or mute any group this week? Which one — and why?
Teacher Tip Going to for concrete decision. "Yes, I'm going to mute the neighborhood group tonight." "No, I'm not going to leave — I'm afraid of the reaction." The fear of leaving is often more interesting than the decision itself. Ask: "Why are you afraid?"
Q8
Will WhatsApp groups get better or worse in the future? Why?
Teacher Tip Will for prediction. "I think they will get worse because more people use WhatsApp every year." "I think they will get better because WhatsApp added new controls." One sentence with will + reason is perfect. Don't push for complexity.
Block 4 · Provocative
Q9
Be honest — do YOU send voice notes longer than 2 minutes? How many?
Teacher Tip Simple present + honesty. Voice notes are a very polarizing topic in Mexico. People either love them or hate them — and many who hate them also send them. "Yes, I send voice notes. Sometimes 3 minutes." "No, I never send voice notes. I write." The self-awareness is the fun part.
Q10
Your uncle is going to add you to a new group for the family Christmas party. It will have 45 people. What are you going to do?
Teacher Tip Going to scenario — funny and real. "I'm going to mute it immediately." "I'm going to stay but I'm not going to write anything." "I'm going to leave after Christmas." This scenario is so specific and Mexican that it almost always produces laughter and real spontaneous language.

Phase 5
The Exit Shot
One situation. What are you going to do?
⏱ 5 min
💣 Exit Shot · A2.1 WhatsApp Groups I Hate
Your boss is going to create a new work WhatsApp group tonight. He is going to add everyone, including you. You know this group is going to have messages at 10pm, 6am, and on Sundays. You don't want to be in it. What are you going to do?
Homework · WhatsApp Voice Note (1 min)
Record your answer in English. What are you going to do — and what are you going to say to your boss?
Retention Hook This is a situation almost every Mexican professional has been in. "Going to" for the plan + real language for what to say to the boss = excellent production. The impossibility of saying no to the boss adds just the right tension. End: "Send me the voice note. And tell me: are you brave or not?"
