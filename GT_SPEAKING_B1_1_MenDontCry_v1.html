Men Don't Cry?
The Male Stereotype

Why do so many men still feel they can't show emotions — and what does that cost them?

🪝
The Trigger
Open the Conversation
⏱ 4 min
Gallup / American Psychological Association — 2023
"Men are 4 times more likely to die by suicide than women. Experts say one big reason is that many men still feel they cannot talk about their feelings or ask for help. The message 'be strong' is killing people."
🎙 Teacher Script

Read the stat slowly. Let it land. Then ask simply: "Where does this idea come from — that men should not show emotions?" Don't point at any student first. If the group is quiet, try: "Did your father cry in front of you when you were a child?" That question almost always opens the conversation immediately. Stay warm and non-judgmental throughout the session.

The Conversation Engine
23 Minutes of Real Talk
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 — Personal Experience
Q1
When you were a child, did someone tell you "don't cry" or "be strong"? Who was it? How did it make you feel?
This works for both men and women — women received this message too, sometimes. Create a safe space: "There are no wrong answers here. We are just sharing experiences." The first honest answer unlocks the whole group.
Q2
Have you ever hidden how you really felt because you were afraid of what people would think?
Model answer: "Yes, at work. I had a very hard month once — personal problems. But I came to work every day with a smile. Nobody knew. I didn't want people to see me as weak or unprofessional."
Q3
Have you ever seen a man cry in public — a film, a funeral, a sports game? What did you think or feel?
Notice the range of reactions. Some students feel empathy. Others feel uncomfortable. Neither reaction is wrong — both are honest data about how deeply this stereotype is inside us.
Block 2 — Opinion
Q4
Do you think it's OK for a man to cry at work? What about in front of his children?
The two-part question creates useful contrast. Many students who say "not at work" say "yes, in front of children." Push them: "Why is it different with children? What message does that send?"
Q5
Is the "men don't cry" idea changing in Mexico — or is it still very strong in your experience?
Generational debate — great for mixed-age groups. Younger students often say it's changing. Older students often say it's still very present. Both are right about their own experience. Let them notice that together.
Q6
Do you think women have the same problem — or is it different for them?
Opens the conversation to women's experience — that emotions are "too much" for women in professional settings. Both genders face stereotypes about emotion, just different ones. Creates natural empathy across the group.
Block 3 — Hypothetical
Q7
What would you do if your male friend called you and said "I'm really not OK. I'm very sad and I don't know what to do"?
Many men admit they wouldn't know what to say. That's an honest and important answer — not a failure. Model answer: "Honestly? I would feel nervous. I don't know if I would say the right thing. But I would listen. I think listening is the most important thing."
Q8
What would you tell your son — or a young man you know — about emotions and being strong?
The values question. What do students actually want to pass on? Many people discover in this question that their answer is different from how they were raised. That gap is the real insight.
Block 4 — Provocative
Q9
Honestly — do you judge a man differently when he cries? Even a little bit?
The most honest question in the session. Many people, including women, will admit they feel slightly uncomfortable when men cry — and then feel bad about that feeling. That self-awareness is valuable. Normalize it: "These ideas are very old and very deep. It's not your fault."
Q10
Is there something you feel but never say — because you think people will judge you? What stops you?
The deepest question in the session. Don't force it — if a student doesn't want to share, that's OK. But give the silence space. Sometimes the most powerful moment in a class is when nobody says anything for a few seconds. That silence is real.

The Exit Shot
One Question. No Easy Answer.
⏱ 5 min
💣 Final Dilemma
Your father — or a man you love very much — is very sick. He is in hospital. You visit him and he starts to cry. He says: "I'm scared. I don't want to die." You have never seen him cry before. What do you do? What do you say?
