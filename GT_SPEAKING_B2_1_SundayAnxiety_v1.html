Sunday Anxiety
The dread before Monday. Why do so many professionals feel it — and what does it say about the work we've chosen?
🪝
Phase 1
The Trigger
A real headline. A real debate.
⏱ 4 min
LinkedIn Workforce Confidence Report · 2023
"80% of professionals report experiencing 'Sunday Scaries' — a wave of anxiety before the workweek begins. Most say it's not about the work itself. It's about the environment they have to walk back into."
Teacher Script
"Sunday evening. 7 PM. What happens in your body? Let's be honest — raise your hand if Sunday night feels different from Saturday night. Now the real question: is that feeling telling you something important — or is it just anxiety that everyone feels?"

Phase 4
The Conversation Engine
10 questions. Real talk. No right answers.
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 — Personal Experience
Q1
Describe what your typical Sunday evening feels like — not what you do, but how you feel. Has this changed over the years?
Listen for the emotional vocabulary: nervous, heavy, restless, fine, empty, energized. Which word they choose tells you a lot. Follow up: "When was the last time Sunday felt actually good?"
Q2
What's the specific thing that triggers it for you? Is it a thought, an email, a sound, a time of day?
Help them find precision here. "Work stress" is too vague. Push for: a specific person, a specific meeting, a specific type of message. The more concrete, the better the conversation.
Q3
Have you ever had a job where Sunday nights felt completely fine? What was different about it?
Contrast builds vocabulary. If they've only had one job, ask about a project vs. another project. What changed? Autonomy? The boss? The team? The mission?
Block 2 — Opinion
Q4
Is Sunday anxiety a personal mental health issue, a workplace culture problem, or just a normal part of working life? Where does the responsibility lie?
Model answer: "I think it's both — but we tend to put all the responsibility on the individual because it's uncomfortable for companies to admit they're causing it. Meditation won't fix a toxic manager."
Q5
Should companies have a formal "Sunday silence" policy — no emails, no messages, no contact after Friday 6 PM? Would that even work in Mexico?
Great cultural moment. France has the "right to disconnect" law. Mexico passed something similar. Would it change anything? "My boss would send WhatsApps anyway." Ask them what would actually change their situation.
Q6
Marcos says anxiety means you care — that feeling nothing on Sundays is actually worse. Do you agree? Is there something dangerous about that argument?
This is the most intellectually rich question. The danger: it can be used to normalize exploitation. "We suffer because we care" is also how people end up burned out. Push them to name the difference between healthy investment and self-destructive devotion.
Block 3 — Hypothetical
Q7
If you could redesign your Monday mornings completely — what would they look like? And would that actually fix the Sunday problem?
Model answer: "If I could, I'd start at 9:30, no meetings until 11, and use the first hour just to plan. I think it would help — but honestly the anxiety would probably just move to Sunday morning."
Q8
If a close friend told you they've been having Sunday anxiety for two years but they're too scared to quit or change jobs — what would you tell them?
This flips the perspective. Now they have to give advice — which forces them to articulate a position. Watch: their advice to the friend is usually what they can't tell themselves.
Block 4 — Provocative
Q9
Is it possible that you've gotten so used to Sunday anxiety that you'd actually feel uncomfortable without it? Have you ever checked?
Psychologically edgy — in the best way. Some people use Sunday stress as identity: "I'm a hardworking person." Take that away and who are they? This is where the real conversation starts.
Q10
What does your relationship with Sunday evenings say about the choices you've made — professionally and personally?
The closer. Not about the company, not about the boss — about their own choices. Did they choose this path? Could they change it? Have they tried? Give them space to be honest. Don't rush to comfort.
