My Family

Talk about the people who matter most. With simple words, big feelings.

🎪 Show & Tell 💉 Vocab 🔀 Choose & Speak 🃏 Questions ✨ My Sentence
Phase 1
🎪 Show & Tell
Show a photo of your family on your phone
5 min
Teacher Models First
"OK, look at my phone. This is MY family. This is my mom — her name is Laura. She is 65 years old. She is very nice. And this is my brother. He is tall! OK, now you — show me a photo of YOUR family."
Student Sentence Starters
This is my ___ (mom / dad / brother / sister / wife / husband).
His / Her name is ___.
He / She is ___ years old.
He / She is ___ (nice / tall / funny / young / old).
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Students LOVE showing family photos. This is the highest-engagement moment of the session. Let them talk.
  • Common error: "She have 30 years" (Spanish transfer). Recast: "She IS 30 years old."
  • If student only knows "mamá" — teach "mom" right here. It sticks because the photo is in front of them.
  • Accept "He is good person" — do not correct article omission at A1.1, just recast naturally.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Your mom is beautiful!" / "Your brother is tall!" — react to the PHOTO, not just the English.
  • "You just introduced your family in English — that is a big moment."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Imagine — next month you can describe your family AND your job in English. Same structure."

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • MODEL pair 1: "I choose BIG FAMILY. My family is big — I have 3 brothers. A big family is fun but sometimes it is difficult!"
  • "More like Mom/Dad" is GOLD — students love this. They produce adjectives naturally: "I am also serious" / "I am also funny."
  • Watch for "I am the more young" → correct to "I am the youngest." Teach as a chunk.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "That is so interesting! You are like your mom — strong and nice."
  • Share your own family detail to build rapport: "Me too! I am the oldest."
Phase 4
🃏 Question Cards
Teacher models first — then you answer
12 min
Question 1
How many people are in your family?
"There are 5 people in my family. My mom, my dad, my two brothers, and me. We are a big family."
Now you: "There are ___ people in my family..."
Tip: Teach "there are" as a fixed phrase. If student says "We are five" — that is also acceptable at A1.1.
Question 2
Who is the funniest person in your family?
"The funniest person in my family is my brother Carlos. He is 28. He is always happy and he is very funny."
Now you: "The funniest person is..."
Tip: Accept "My brother is funny" if they avoid the superlative. Just model "funniest" naturally.
Question 3
Is your family in Celaya or in another city?
"My mom is in Guadalajara. My brother is here in Celaya. My family is in different cities."
Now you: "My family is in..."
Tip: This produces real, personal answers. Let the student take their time. Help with city names if needed.
Question 4
Is your mom or dad strict or relaxed?
"My dad is very strict! He is serious. But my mom is relaxed. She is always happy."
Now you: "My mom/dad is..."
Tip: Teach "relaxed" as a new word here — they know "strict" from vocab. Contrast is natural.
Question 5
Who is your favorite person in your family?
"My favorite person is my daughter. She is 4 years old. She is beautiful and funny. She is my world!"
Now you: "My favorite person is..."
Tip: Emotional questions produce longer answers. Give them space. React genuinely to what they share.
Question 6
Is your family important for you?
"Yes, my family is VERY important for me. They are my support. My wife is my best friend. Family is everything!"
Now you: "My family is... for me because..."
Tip: This is the emotional peak. Let it breathe. No grammar corrections here — just listen and react.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Family questions produce the MOST language at A1.1. Students want to talk — your job is to scaffold.
  • Watch for: "My family have 5 persons" → "There ARE 5 people in my family."
  • "Persons" → gently model "people" as the natural word.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Your daughter is 4? That is beautiful. You are a great parent — and now you can describe her in English!"
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "You talked about your entire family in English today. Next time — your desk at work. Same structure, same confidence."
  • Testimonial trigger: "Could you describe your family in English before this class? Now you can."
Phase 5
✨ My Sentence
Build one perfect sentence — practice it — record it
5 min
Complete This Sentence
The most important person in my family is ___ because he/she is ___.
📱 WhatsApp Homework: Record this sentence as a voice note (30 seconds). Say it clearly, with feeling. Send it to your teacher.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Model: "The most important person in my family is my wife because she is my best friend and she is always there."
  • Help with pronunciation of "important" (im-POR-tant) and "because" (be-CAUSE).
  • Student repeats 2-3 times. Correct only pronunciation, not grammar, at this stage.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "That is a beautiful sentence. That is YOUR sentence. It is real and it is in English."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Today: your family. Next time: your desk at work. You are building English about YOUR life."
