Hot Weather or Cold Weather?

Sun or snow — what is your perfect temperature?

🎪 Show & Tell 5 min

Show a photo from a hot day or a cold day in your life.

Your Task

Open your phone. Find a photo from a hot day (the beach, summer, your city in June) or a cold day (a jacket, rain, a cold morning). Show it and describe it.

Teacher Script — Model First

"Look — this is a photo from December. It is cold! I am in a big jacket. My nose is red. I am not happy — I like hot weather. OK — show me YOUR photo. What is the weather like?"

Sentence Starters

This is a photo from _______ .
It is hot / cold / rainy / sunny .
I am _______ in this photo.
The weather is _______ .

Teacher Tip

Drill "weather" — students say "weh-AH-ter" instead of "WEH-ther". Also drill "cold" vs "cool" — explain: cold = very (brrr), cool = a little (nice). If they have no photo, just ask: "What is the weather today? Is it hot or cold right now?"

🃏 Question Cards 12 min

Teacher models the answer first. Then student personalizes.

Question 01
What is the weather like in your city today?
Now you: describe the weather RIGHT NOW.
Teacher Model

"Today the weather is sunny. The temperature is 29 degrees. It is hot but comfortable. The sky is beautiful."

Teacher Tip

Start easy — the weather right now. If online, ask them to look out the window. This generates instant real-world language.

Question 02
What is your perfect temperature?
Now you: what number is perfect for YOU?
Teacher Model

"My perfect temperature is 24 degrees. It is not hot, not cold. It is perfect for everything — for a walk, for coffee outside."

Teacher Tip

Great number practice. Push for WHY: "24 degrees — is that comfortable? Is that for inside or outside?" Build more sentences from one number.

Question 03
Who is the cold-weather person in your family?
Now you: who in YOUR family is always cold?
Teacher Model

"My wife is the cold-weather person. She is cold in September! Her jacket is always with her. I am the opposite — I am a hot-weather person."

Teacher Tip

Family vocab review opportunity. Ask about multiple family members: "And your mom? And your dad? Are they hot or cold people?"

Question 04
How is your city in summer? And in winter?
Now you: describe YOUR city in both seasons.
Teacher Model

"My city in summer is very hot — 35 degrees! It is terrible. But in winter it is nice — 18 degrees. The winter is my favorite time."

Teacher Tip

Push for contrast: "So summer is ______ BUT winter is ______." This is a natural way to introduce "but" as connector without formal grammar teaching.

Question 05
What is the worst weather for you?
Now you: what weather is TERRIBLE for you?
Teacher Model

"The worst weather for me is humid and hot. 38 degrees with humidity — it is terrible! I am tired, I am not productive, I am angry."

Teacher Tip

Emotion + weather = great output. Push for body reactions: "Are you tired? Are you angry? Is your energy at 0%?" Recycle the energy percentage from other labs if they have done them.

Question 06
Is the weather important for your mood?
Now you: is YOUR mood different with different weather?
Teacher Model

"Yes! The weather is very important for my mood. On a sunny day I am happy and productive. On a rainy day I am lazy and tired. The sun is my energy."

Teacher Tip

Teach "mood" as a chunk: "My mood is good/bad." Don't explain it grammatically. Just model it and let them use it. If they connect weather to emotions with 3+ sentences, celebrate big.

✨ My Sentence 5 min

Build one perfect sentence. Practice it. Record it.

My perfect weather is _______ because I am _______ and my energy is _______ .

🎙️ Record this sentence as a WhatsApp voice note (30 seconds).

Teacher Tip

Push for a specific temperature number + adjective + emotion: "My perfect weather is 22 degrees because I am comfortable and my energy is at 100%." This is a complex, beautiful sentence for A1.1. Celebrate it. Three repetitions, then record.
