Weekday vs. Weekend

Compare your work days and your free days. What do you do? What changes? What stays the same?

🎪 Show & Tell 💉 Vocab 🔀 Choose & Speak 🃏 Questions ✨ My Sentence
🎪
Phase 1
Show & Tell
Show a weekend photo from your phone and describe what you do
5 MIN
Today's Object

Show a photo from a recent weekend — a meal, a place, your family, anything and describe what you usually do.

Teacher models first:
"OK, this is a photo from my weekend. I always go to this coffee place on Saturday morning. I read the news, I drink a big coffee, and I eat pan dulce. On Sundays I cook for my family. I make pasta or something easy. On weekdays? I wake up at 6, I drive to work, I eat fast food for lunch. Completely different life! OK, show me YOUR weekend."

On weekdays, I _______ . I wake up at _______ and I _______ .

Phase 4
Question Cards
The teacher answers first. Then it's your turn.
12 MIN
Question 1
What do you usually do on Saturday morning?
Teacher: "I sleep in until 9. Then I make a big breakfast — eggs, fruit, good coffee. I go to my favorite coffee place and I read the news. I never work on Saturday morning. That's MY rule."
Now you: "On Saturday morning, I usually _______ . I always / never..."
Tip: Saturday morning is sacred for most people. Push for the full sequence: "First you wake up... then what? Do you eat? Do you check your phone? Do you exercise?" Build the picture step by step.
Question 2
What is the worst day of the week for you? Why?
Teacher: "Monday. Always Monday. I wake up tired, the weekend is over, and I have a lot of work. Sunday evening is also bad because I always think about Monday. But Tuesday is OK — the week is already moving."
Now you: "The worst day is _______ because I _______ . I always feel..."
Tip: Most say Monday — push beyond the obvious: "Is it the morning? The traffic? The meetings? What EXACTLY makes Monday bad?" Specifics produce richer language.
Question 3
Do you do housework on weekends? Who cleans?
Teacher: "I clean on Saturdays. I wash the dishes, I organize my desk, and I do laundry. My wife cooks and I clean — that's our system. We always clean on Saturday so Sunday is free."
Now you: "On weekends, I _______ . I always clean on _______ because..."
Tip: Gender dynamics at home — interesting and relatable. Push: "Do you clean or does your partner clean? Do you both clean? Is that fair?" Produces opinions naturally.
Question 4
Can you relax on Sunday or do you think about work?
Teacher: "I can relax on Sunday morning. But Sunday evening? No. I always think about Monday — the meetings, the emails, the schedule. I can't stop thinking about work after 6 PM on Sunday."
Now you: "I can / can't relax on Sunday because _______ . I always think about..."
Tip: "Sunday scaries" — the universal professional feeling. Push: "At what time on Sunday do you start thinking about Monday? 4 PM? 6 PM? Or all day?" Very relatable.
Question 5
Do you sometimes work on weekends?
Teacher: "Sometimes. When I have a deadline, I work on Saturday. I check emails on Sunday sometimes — just 10 minutes. But I try to keep weekends for my family. I don't always succeed!"
Now you: "I sometimes / never work on weekends. I _______ because..."
Tip: Work-life balance is a hot topic for Mexican professionals. Push: "Does your boss send messages on Saturday? Do you answer? Is that normal at your company?"
Question 6
What do you eat differently on weekends?
Teacher: "On weekdays I eat fast — a sandwich or leftovers for lunch. On weekends I cook! I make pasta or go out for tacos. I also eat breakfast at a restaurant on Saturdays. Weekday food is fast, weekend food is good."
Now you: "On weekdays I eat _______ . On weekends I eat _______ . The difference is..."
Tip: Food contrast is always fun. Push: "Do you cook more on weekends? Do you go to restaurants? Do you eat breakfast or skip it?" Contrasting habits = contrasting sentences.
Question 7
Is there something you do EVERY weekend without exception?
Teacher: "Coffee at my place on Saturday morning. That's my ritual. I never skip it. Rain or sun, tired or not — I always go. And I always watch at least one movie on Sunday night. Those two things are non-negotiable."
Now you: "Every weekend I always _______ . I never skip it because..."
Tip: "Non-negotiable" rituals reveal personality. This is a CEFR A1.2 goldmine — "I always + verb" with real commitment behind it. Let them talk — these are the sentences they'll remember.
Question 8
Do you need longer weekends or are two days enough?
Teacher: "Two days is not enough! I need 3 days — Saturday for errands and cleaning, Sunday for family, and one more day just for ME. I think everybody needs a 3-day weekend. But maybe that's just because I'm tired today!"
Now you: "I think _______ days is enough / not enough because I need time to..."
Tip: Fun closer — everyone wants more weekend. Push: "What do you do with day 3? Sleep? Travel? Or just sit on the sofa?" The fantasy reveals real priorities.
