My Perfect Vacation

Describe your dream vacation. Say where it is and what it is like. Only "to be" and adjectives.

🎪 Show & Tell 💉 Vocab 🔀 Choose & Speak 🃏 Questions ✨ My Sentence
🎪
Phase 1
Show & Tell
Show a vacation photo from your phone and describe it
5 MIN
Today's Object

Show a vacation photo from your phone — or search for a photo of your dream destination and describe it.

Teacher models first:
"OK, look — this is a photo from my last vacation. This is the beach in Oaxaca. The water is blue and warm. The sand is white. It is beautiful and relaxing. There are palm trees and small restaurants. It is my favorite place! OK, now you — show me a vacation photo."

Phase 4
Question Cards
The teacher answers first. Then it's your turn.
12 MIN
Question 1
Where is your perfect vacation? What country or city?
Teacher: "My perfect vacation is in Oaxaca, México. The beach there is incredible. The water is warm and blue. The food is amazing — it is the best food in México for me."
Now you: "My perfect vacation is in _______ . It is..."
Tip: If student says only the place, push: "OK, but WHY? What is special about it? Is the food good? Is the weather nice? Is it beautiful?" Every adjective is a sentence.
Question 2
What is the weather like in your perfect vacation?
Teacher: "The weather is warm and sunny. Not too hot — maybe 28 or 30 degrees. The mornings are fresh and the afternoons are warm. The nights are perfect for dinner outside."
Now you: "The weather is _______ . The mornings are _______ and the afternoons are _______ ."
Tip: Great for "to be" + weather adjectives. Push for time of day: "And the nights? Are the nights warm or cold?" Every time slot is a new sentence.
Question 3
Is your vacation expensive or cheap? Is that important?
Teacher: "My perfect vacation is not very expensive. Oaxaca is cheaper than Cancún. The hotels are nice but not luxury. For me, the food and the beach are more important than the hotel."
Now you: "My vacation is _______ . For me, the most important thing is..."
Tip: Money talk is always engaging for professionals. Ask: "Is an expensive vacation always better? Or is a cheap vacation sometimes more fun?" Forces opinion.
Question 4
Who is with you on your perfect vacation?
Teacher: "My wife is with me. She is my best travel partner. We are relaxed together. No kids, no work, no phone. Just us and the beach. That is my perfect vacation."
Now you: "On my perfect vacation, _______ is with me. He/She is..."
Tip: Some students will say "alone!" — that is valid and interesting. Push: "Why alone? Is it more relaxing? Is it more fun?" Both answers produce good language.
Question 5
What is the worst thing about vacations?
Teacher: "The worst thing is the last day. The last day is always sad. You are in paradise and then — back to work on Monday. That moment is terrible!"
Now you: "The worst thing about vacations is _______ because..."
Tip: This question always gets a reaction. Students relate to "the last day" pain. Push: "Is Monday after vacation the worst Monday?" Gets laughs and language.
Question 6
How many days is a perfect vacation? Three days? One week? Two weeks?
Teacher: "For me, one week is perfect. Three days is too short — you are not relaxed yet. Two weeks is too long — I am bored after 10 days. One week is the perfect number."
Now you: "For me, _______ days is perfect because..."
Tip: Good for "too + adjective" structure: "too short, too long, too expensive." Model it and let student copy the pattern.
