Morning or Night Person?

Are you happy in the morning — or is night your best time?

🎪 Show & Tell 5 min

Show a photo from your phone — your morning or your night.

Your Task

Open your phone. Find a photo from your morning (coffee, your street, your breakfast) or from your night (your sofa, a movie, your dinner). Show it and describe it.

Teacher Script — Model First

"Look — this is a photo from MY morning. This is my coffee. It is black. It is very big! My morning is quiet. I am happy in the morning. OK — now you. Show me YOUR photo. What is it?"

Sentence Starters

This is my _______ .
It is _______ .
My morning / my night is _______ .
I am happy / tired / relaxed in the _______ .

Teacher Tip

If they have no photo, ask: "OK, no photo? Describe your morning RIGHT NOW. What is on your desk? What is your coffee like?" Push them to produce 4-5 sentences minimum. Correct pronunciation of "morning" (students often say "mornin") and "night" (the silent 'gh').

🃏 Question Cards 12 min

Teacher models the answer first. Then student personalizes.

Question 01
What is your morning like?
Now you: describe YOUR morning.
Teacher Model

"My morning is early. My alarm is at 5:30. I am tired but my coffee is good. My morning is quiet — it is my favorite time."

Teacher Tip

If student answers with one sentence, prompt: "Good! And what is your coffee like? And how is the morning — quiet or noisy?" Push for 3+ sentences.

Question 02
Is your night relaxed or busy?
Now you: what is YOUR night like?
Teacher Model

"My night is relaxed. My house is quiet at 9 PM. I am on my sofa. It is my time for me."

Teacher Tip

Drill "relaxed" vs "relaxing" — at A1.1, only use "relaxed" (I am relaxed). "Relaxing" is too complex to explain as a concept now.

Question 03
Who is a morning person in your family?
Now you: who is the early bird in YOUR family?
Teacher Model

"My mom is the morning person. She is awake at 5 AM! She is incredible. My dad is the opposite — he is a night person."

Teacher Tip

This is a great moment for family vocab review. Ask: "And your brother? Your wife? Your kids?" Keep them talking about different family members.

Question 04
How is your energy at 7 AM? And at 10 PM?
Now you: what is YOUR energy like at those times?
Teacher Model

"At 7 AM, my energy is at 100%! I am productive. But at 10 PM — my energy is at 0. I am tired and I am on my pillow."

Teacher Tip

The "energy at percentage" format is fun — students enjoy rating themselves. Try: "So your energy at 2 PM — what percentage?" It generates spontaneous sentences.

Question 05
What is the best thing about your morning?
Now you: what is the best part of YOUR morning?
Teacher Model

"The best thing is silence. My house is quiet. My coffee is hot. It is perfect. That is my moment."

Teacher Tip

If they struggle, scaffold: "Is it coffee? Is it silence? Is it your family?" Let them pick one and build from there.

Question 06
Is your weekend morning different?
Now you: what is YOUR Saturday morning like?
Teacher Model

"Oh yes! My weekend morning is very different. There is no alarm. I am lazy. My coffee is at 10 AM, not 6 AM. It is beautiful."

Teacher Tip

Great closer. Compare weekday vs weekend. Ask: "So — your weekday morning is ______ but your weekend morning is ______?" This generates contrastive sentences naturally.

✨ My Sentence 5 min

Build one perfect sentence. Practice it. Record it.

The best part of my morning / night is _______ because it is _______ .

🎙️ Record this sentence as a WhatsApp voice note (30 seconds).

Teacher Tip

Have them say it 3 times. First time: just build it. Second time: correct pronunciation. Third time: say it with confidence and emotion. THEN record. This is the "trophy sentence" they take home.
