The Trigger
Phase 1 · Open the conversation
4 min
Forbes · Careers · 2023
"1 in 3 candidates say they have walked out of a job interview — or seriously considered it — because of how the interviewer treated them."
Teacher script: Read the stat. Then ask: "OK — has anyone here ever had a terrible job interview? Like, actually terrible?" Wait for hands or reactions. Then: "Tell me what happened in 30 seconds." Get two quick stories before moving to vocab.

The Conversation Engine
Phase 4 · 10 questions · 4 blocks
23 min
Block 1 — Personal Experience
Q1
Have you ever had a really bad job interview? What made it so bad?
Teacher tip: Get the story out in detail. Push: "What did the interviewer say? How did you feel? What did you do after — did you go home and cry, call a friend, eat a lot?" The post-interview reaction is always funny and real.
Q2
Have you ever gone completely blank during an interview — and didn't know what to say?
Model answer: "Yes. They asked me 'Why do you want to work here?' and I forgot everything I prepared. I said 'Because it's a good company' and then nothing. Ten seconds of silence. It was horrible. I wanted to disappear."
Q3
Have you ever lied in a job interview? What did you say?
Teacher tip: The room will laugh. Common lies: "I speak English fluently," "I know Excel very well," "I left my last job because I wanted a new challenge." Don't judge — collect the stories.
Block 2 — Opinion
Q4
Is it OK to walk out of a bad interview like Carolina did?
Teacher tip: Strong debate. Some say yes — respect yourself. Others say no — you never know who that person knows. Let both sides argue. Ask: "Would you actually do it, or just think about it?"
Q5
Do you think "What is your biggest weakness?" is a good interview question?
Model answer: "No, I think it's a terrible question. Nobody tells the truth. Everyone says something like 'I'm a perfectionist' or 'I work too hard.' It doesn't tell you anything real about the person."
Q6
Do you think companies should tell you why they didn't hire you after an interview?
Teacher tip: Almost everyone says yes. Push further: "Has a company ever actually given you feedback? What did they say?" Most will say they never received any — that's the real conversation.
Block 3 — Hypothetical
Q7
What would you do if an interviewer asked you a question that felt personal or inappropriate — like your age, relationship status, or plans for children?
Model answer: "I would probably answer it even if I didn't want to — because I'd be afraid of losing the opportunity. But I would also think: if they ask this now, what is this company going to be like when I actually work there?"
Q8
What would you do if you arrived at a job interview and the company looked nothing like what they described online?
Teacher tip: Keep it concrete: "The office is dirty. There are no people around. The receptionist looks unhappy. What do you do — stay or leave?"
Block 4 — Provocative
Q9
Have you ever judged someone else's interview performance when you were in a group interview — and felt secretly relieved they did worse than you?
Teacher tip: Very honest question. Most people will laugh first — that's them saying yes. Ask: "Do you think that's normal? Or does it say something bad about us?"
Q10
Be honest: have you ever accepted a job offer even though you knew during the interview that the job was wrong for you?
Teacher tip: Almost everyone has done this. Common reasons: needed the money, didn't have other options, hoped it would get better. The follow-up question is the best one: "Were you right to accept — or do you regret it?"

The Exit Shot
Phase 5 · One final question
5 min
💣 Exit Shot
You really need this job. The interview is going terribly — you forgot things, you went blank twice, and the interviewer looks bored. You have 5 minutes left. What do you do to save it?
