Foods I Like and Foods I Hate

Talk about your favorite and least favorite foods. Use simple present to describe what you eat, cook, and buy.

Show & Tell
Show a photo of your favorite food and describe it
5 MIN
Today's Object

Search on your phone for a photo of your favorite food or a meal you eat every week and describe it.

Phase 4
Question Cards
The teacher answers first. Then it's your turn.
12 MIN
Question 1
What do you usually eat for breakfast?
Teacher: "I usually eat eggs and toast. I always drink coffee — black, no sugar. Sometimes I eat fruit, but I never eat cereal. Cereal is for kids!"
Now you: "I usually eat _______ for breakfast. I always drink..."
Tip: Push for frequency adverbs: "ALWAYS eggs? Or SOMETIMES? Do you NEVER eat something sweet?" Each adverb is a new sentence. Stack them.
Question 2
What food do you hate? Why?
Teacher: "I hate liver. I can't eat it. The smell is terrible and the flavor is disgusting. My mom cooks liver sometimes and I always say no. I also don't like sushi — I know it's popular, but I avoid it."
Now you: "I hate _______ . I can't eat it because..."
Tip: "Hate" produces energy — students get animated. Push: "Is it the smell? The flavor? The texture? Why EXACTLY do you hate it?" Forces specific vocabulary.
Question 3
Can you cook? What do you usually cook?
Teacher: "I can cook simple things — pasta, eggs, rice. I cook on weekends when I have time. But I can't cook fish or anything complicated. My wife cooks better than me."
Now you: "I can cook _______ . I usually cook on _______ . I can't cook..."
Tip: Perfect "can/can't" practice. Push the contrast: "OK, you CAN cook pasta — but CAN you cook sushi? CAN you cook a cake?" Every "can't" is an honest sentence.
Question 4
Do you eat the same food every day or do you like variety?
Teacher: "I eat the same breakfast every day — eggs and coffee. But for lunch and dinner, I like variety. I don't like the same food two days in a row. I get bored."
Now you: "I usually eat the same _______ every day, but for _______ I like..."
Tip: Contrast "every day" vs. "sometimes" — do they have a routine or do they change? Either answer produces good simple present output.
Question 5
Is there a good restaurant near your house?
Teacher: "Yes! There is a taco place two blocks from my house. It's cheap and the food is amazing. There are also some pizza places, but they are expensive. I usually go to the taco place on Fridays."
Now you: "There is _______ near my house. I go there on..."
Tip: Natural "there is/there are" practice. Push: "How many restaurants are there near your house? What kind? Is there a market too?" Each "there is" builds fluency.
Question 6
What food do you eat when you feel sad or stressed?
Teacher: "When I feel stressed, I eat chocolate. Always chocolate. I buy a big bar and I eat it all in one night. I know it's not healthy, but it makes me happy for 20 minutes!"
Now you: "When I feel _______ , I eat _______ . I usually buy..."
Tip: Emotional eating — everyone relates. This question gets honest, personal answers. Don't judge — just push for detail: "How much do you eat? Where do you buy it? Do you share it?"
Question 7
Do you eat breakfast, lunch, AND dinner every day?
Teacher: "No! I sometimes skip breakfast because I wake up late. I always eat lunch — it's the most important meal for me. And I usually eat a light dinner, maybe just fruit or a sandwich."
Now you: "I always eat _______ . I sometimes skip _______ because..."
Tip: Great for "always/sometimes/never" stacking. Ask about each meal separately: "Breakfast — always, sometimes, or never? Lunch? Dinner?" Three structures in one question.
Question 8
What is a food you want to try but you never eat?
Teacher: "I want to try Korean food. There is a Korean restaurant in Querétaro but I never go. I don't know what to order! I can eat spicy food, so I think I can like Korean food."
Now you: "I want to try _______ . I never eat it because..."
Tip: "Want to try" is a fixed chunk at A1.2 — model it and let them copy. Push: "Why don't you try it? Is there a place near you? Can you cook it at home?" Opens new paths.
