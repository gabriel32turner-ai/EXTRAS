🪝 Opening Trigger — Read this, then point at someone
"A fitness influencer posts a 12-week transformation — 15 kg lost, visible abs, completely different body. In the comments, someone finds photos from 3 years ago showing the same body. The 'transformation' took 3 years, not 12 weeks. The influencer never corrects the post. It has 2 million views."
Teacher Mechanics — 4 min Read the influencer story slowly. Let the "2 million views" land. Point: "First reaction — is this person lying? Is it a big deal?" No judgment. Wait for reactions. After 2–3 responses: "Hold that. Everything comes back in §4."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • The 67% stat (most gym members quit in 3 months and gyms count on it) is the most provocative for students who have a gym membership. It makes the business model feel cynical. That's the activation.
  • The 83% fake transformation photo stat is the most surprising. Use it as the pivot: "So when you see a before-and-after photo online — how do you know if it's real?"
  • If nobody reacts: "Has anyone here ever bought a fitness product or plan because of a transformation photo? Raise your hand."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • This topic is personally relatable for almost every adult — gym memberships, New Year's resolutions, fitness apps, Instagram accounts they follow. The emotional activation is immediate.
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Retention: "Next Speaking Lab goes into anxiety and mental health — a topic that connects directly to how we think about our bodies and our performance. Same format, same depth."

⚡ Production Challenge — No preparation
Each person picks 3 words. One sentence each about your real relationship with exercise — honest. No writing. Just talk.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • "Supplement" — clarify if needed: protein powders, vitamins, creatine. Not medication. The word is widely used in gym culture but students sometimes confuse it with medicine.
  • Best production challenge trigger: "Which 3 words describe your gym history? Be honest — include the part where you stopped going." Usually gets a laugh and immediate engagement.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "These words appear in every health and wellness conversation with international colleagues — HR wellness programs, team challenges, corporate gym memberships. You'll use them."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention

🔁 Processing Question — Pairs → Group
"Who do you agree with most — and who do you think is missing the point? Why?"
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Read all 4 before opening. The contrast between Claudia (gym is her therapy) and Héctor (gym culture is toxic) is the main tension. Both are sympathetic — that's intentional.
  • The provocateur is Tomás. His position — that fit bodies are a career advantage and should be cultivated strategically — will make some students very uncomfortable. Push: "Do you think that's true in your company? Does being fit give you an advantage?"
  • Isabel's position (she quit the gym for a walk in the park) will resonate with students who feel guilt about not going to the gym. Let that resonate.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • Best activation: ask before reading — "How many of you have a gym membership right now? How many of you went last week? Keep your hand up if you went more than 3 times." The drop-off is always funny and immediately relevant.
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Testimonial trigger: "One sentence — which of these four people sounds most like you at your most honest?"
§ 04 · The Conversation Engine
🗣 10 Questions. 4 Blocks. 23 Minutes.
Personal → Opinion → Hypothetical → Provocative · No round-robin · Point and push
04
23 min
⚡
Rule: Minimum 2 sentences per answer. Position first. Real example second. No motivational poster answers.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Block 1 present perfect — watch for "I have go" or "I have went." Correct once gently: "So you have been to the gym — tell me about it."
  • Q9 (body image) is the most personal question in this session. Give it the most silence — 6 seconds before redirecting. The honest answer is worth waiting for.
  • Q10 asks for a clear position — "inspiration or pressure." Push anyone who says "both" to pick one as the stronger of the two.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • When "quit," "routine," or "transform" appear naturally in answers — point it out immediately: "You just used a word from today's list without thinking. That's B1.2 output."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • After Q10: "You just discussed body image, social media, and personal motivation — in English — with honesty. That is not easy. That is B1.2."
§ 05 · The Exit Shot
💣 One Question. No Easy Answer.
5 min · No chips · Just position + reason · Then the loop
05
5 min
EXIT
💣 Exit Shot
You have been going to the gym for 6 months. You lost 4 kg and feel better. A colleague at work notices and asks: "What's your secret?" The honest answer is: gym 4 days a week, no alcohol, early bedtime, and some luck with genetics. But they want a simple, inspiring answer. What do you say?
Answer in 3 sentences. Be specific. Do you tell the whole truth — or give them the simple version?
📌 B1.2 Model Answer — Reveal AFTER student speaks
"I would tell the truth — all of it — because I've seen how much damage oversimplified fitness advice does to people. I'd say: 'It took 6 months, 4 days a week in the gym, no drinking, and honestly, some genetics that helped.' If they want the simple version, they can take the parts that apply to them. I don't want to be another person selling a fake shortcut."
🔁 Retention Loop — The WhatsApp Homework "Before our next session — record a 60-second voice note in English. Your answer to today's Exit Shot. Don't write it first. Don't rehearse. Just talk. Send it to [teacher number]."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • The scenario is deliberately ambiguous — there is no objectively "right" answer. Some students will value honesty; others will prioritize inspiration or simplicity. Both are valid. Push: "What would you want someone to tell YOU in that situation?"
  • Follow-up: "What would you do differently if the person asking was struggling with their weight and felt frustrated?" Second conditional naturally activated.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • The model answer introduces "fake shortcut" — a natural collocation worth pointing out: "Shortcut — a faster but often less reliable route. 'Fake shortcut' — something that promises speed but doesn't work. Remember that."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Closing: "Next Speaking Lab: Anxiety — Real Problem or Excuse? Same honesty. Different topic." Direct tease to Lab 3 of this lote.
