My Neighborhood

The streets, the shops, the people — tell us about the place you live.

🎪 Show & Tell 5 min

Show a photo of your street or your neighborhood and describe it.

Your Task

Open Google Maps or your phone gallery. Find a photo of your neighborhood — your street, a nearby store, a park, your building. Show it and describe what is there.

Teacher Script — Model First

"Look — this is my street. There is a small park and there are many trees. I walk there every morning. There is a coffee shop on the corner — I go there on Saturdays. I like my neighborhood because it is quiet. OK — show me YOUR neighborhood. What is there?"

Sentence Starters

There is a _______ near my house.
There are many _______ .
I go to _______ every _______ .
I like my neighborhood because _______ .

Teacher Tip

Google Maps is an incredible tool here — if the student opens Street View, you can literally point at things: "What is THAT? Is there a pharmacy? Is there a bank?" Push for "there is" and "there are" — this is the target structure. Correct: "there are a stores" → "there are STORES" / "there is a store."

Teacher Tip

"Neighborhood" is the hardest word here — NAY-bor-hood. Drill it 3 times. Students want to say "NEI-bor-jud". Also "sidewalk" — students know "banqueta" but not this word. "Around the corner" is incredibly useful — model it in 3 contexts: "The pharmacy is around the corner. The park is around the corner. My favorite restaurant is around the corner."

Choose one and explain with 3-4 sentences.

Teacher — Model the First One

"I choose quiet neighborhood. My street is very quiet. There are not many cars. I can walk at night and I feel safe. I don't like noise — I never go to noisy areas. OK, you choose!"

Teacher Tip

The "neighbors" pair is culturally loaded in Mexico — many people know their neighbors well, or deliberately avoid them. Both answers generate great language. Push: "What do you do with your neighbors? Do you talk? Do you eat together?" The last pair — love vs. move — often surprises. Many students suddenly open up about wanting to live somewhere else. Let them talk.

🃏 Question Cards 12 min

Teacher models first. Then you personalize.

Question 01
What is there near your house?
Now you: list everything near YOUR house.
Teacher Model

"There is a park near my house. There are two restaurants and a pharmacy. There is also a small supermarket on the corner. I buy groceries there every week."

Teacher Tip

This is a "there is/are" machine. Push for quantity: "How many restaurants are there? Are there banks? Is there a gym?" Every answer = one target structure sentence.

Question 02
Do you like your neighborhood?
Now you: what do YOU like and dislike?
Teacher Model

"Yes, I like my neighborhood. It is quiet and safe. I like the park and the coffee shop. But I don't like the traffic on Monday mornings — it is terrible."

Teacher Tip

Push for both positive AND negative: "What DON'T you like?" This generates the "I don't like..." structure naturally.

Question 03
Is there a good restaurant near your house?
Now you: where do YOU eat near home?
Teacher Model

"Yes! There is a taco place around the corner. The tacos are incredible. I eat there every Friday. There is also a pizza place but I don't go there — it is expensive."

Teacher Tip

Food + location = high engagement. Push: "How often do you eat there? Do you walk or drive? What do you eat?" Each follow-up = another sentence.

Question 04
Can you walk to the supermarket from your house?
Now you: what can you walk to? What do you need a car for?
Teacher Model

"I can walk to the pharmacy and the park. They are very close. But I can't walk to the supermarket — it is 3 kilometers. I always drive there on Saturdays."

Teacher Tip

Great can/can't + location combo. Push for distances: "How far is the gym? Can you walk there in 10 minutes?" Mixes can/can't with there is/are naturally.

Question 05
Do you know your neighbors?
Now you: describe YOUR relationship with your neighbors.
Teacher Model

"I know my neighbors on the left side. They are very nice — we sometimes talk in the morning. But I don't know the neighbors on the right side. They are very private."

Teacher Tip

Culturally rich in Mexico. Push for descriptions: "Are they nice? Are they noisy? Do they have kids? Do they have a dog?" This generates third person + adjectives.

Question 06
Is there a lot of traffic in your area?
Now you: how is traffic near YOUR house?
Teacher Model

"Yes, there is a lot of traffic at 8 AM. My street is small but many cars use it. I usually leave my house early because the traffic is terrible. On weekends there is no traffic — it is perfect."

Teacher Tip

Traffic is universal frustration in Mexican cities. Push for time specifics: "What time is bad? What time is good?" Generates natural use of "at" + time.

Question 07
What do you usually do in your neighborhood on weekends?
Now you: describe YOUR weekend in your area.
Teacher Model

"On weekends I walk in the park. I sometimes buy coffee at the cafe around the corner. I also go to the supermarket on Saturdays. I like my neighborhood on weekends because it is quiet."

Teacher Tip

Cross-references "My Weekend Routine" if they did that lab. Great for recycling vocab. Push for frequency: "Do you ALWAYS walk? Or just sometimes?" Layers adverbs on top of location language.

Question 08
What is missing in your neighborhood?
Now you: what do YOU need that isn't there?
Teacher Model

"There is no gym near my house — I always drive 20 minutes to the gym. I also want a good coffee shop. There are restaurants but there is no cafe with good coffee. That is a problem!"

Teacher Tip

"There is no..." and "There are no..." are key negatives for this level. Push: "Is there a gym? No? Is there a cinema? No?" Every "no" generates "There is no..." Great closer because it ends with an opinion/desire.

✨ My Sentence 5 min

Build one perfect sentence. Practice it. Record it.

I like my neighborhood because there is _______ and I always _______ on _______ .

🎙️ Record this sentence as a WhatsApp voice note (30 seconds).

Teacher Tip

Target: "I like my neighborhood because there is a park near my house and I always walk there on Saturdays." This stacks: like + because + there is + frequency adverb + day. Full A1.2 arsenal in one sentence. Three repetitions, then record. If they don't like their neighborhood, alternative: "I don't like my neighborhood because there is no _______ and I always have to drive to _______."
