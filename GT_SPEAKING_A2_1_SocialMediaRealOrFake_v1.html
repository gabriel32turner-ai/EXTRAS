A2.1 Technology & Media 🔥 Medium 50 min
Social Media: Real or Fake?
People post beautiful photos, perfect food, and happy faces. But is that their real life — or just a show?
🪝
Phase 1
The Trigger
One number. One reaction. Go.
⏱ 4 min
Forbes · Social Media Report · 2023
"70% of people say they only post photos when they look good. Most people delete a photo if they don't get enough likes in the first hour."
Social media shows the best version of people — the nice food, the clean house, the happy family. But behind the phone, real life is different. Many people feel bad when they see other people's perfect posts. They think: "My life is not like that."
Teacher Script Read the stat. Then ask: "Quick — did you post something this week? Was it a good photo or a normal photo?" Let 2–3 students answer fast. Then: "And did you delete a photo because you didn't like it?" That immediate recognition is your opening. Keep it light and fun.

Phase 4
The Conversation Engine
10 questions. Short answers are great. Use past simple and simple present.
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 · Personal Experience (Past Simple)
Q1
What was the last photo you posted on social media? Describe it.
Teacher Tip Past simple description. "I posted a photo of my food / my dog / my trip." Help them add one detail: "It was a picture of my lunch. The food was really good." If they say "I don't use social media," ask: "What was the last photo you took on your phone?"
Q2
Did you ever feel bad after looking at social media? What did you see?
Teacher Tip Past simple + emotion. "Yes, I saw a photo of my friend's vacation and I felt jealous." Or: "I saw a person with a perfect body and I felt bad." These are common, honest experiences. No judgment — just conversation.
Q3
Did you ever delete a post because you didn't like it? Why?
Teacher Tip Past simple + reason. "Yes, I deleted a photo because I didn't look good." "I deleted a comment because I was angry when I wrote it." Most people have done this. Short answers with "because" are the target structure.
Block 2 · Opinion (Simple Present)
Q4
Do you think people are honest on social media? Why or why not?
Teacher Tip "I think people are not honest because they only post good things." "I think some people are honest but many people are not." Push for a reason after "because." Accept short answers — this is A2.1, not a debate.
Q5
Is it okay to use filters on photos? Or is it a lie?
Teacher Tip Simple opinion with "It's okay" / "It's not okay" as scaffolding. "I think it's okay because it's just a photo." "I think it's a little bit of a lie because you don't look like that in real life." Both positions are valid. No wrong answer.
Q6
Do you think social media is good for people — or bad? What do you think?
Teacher Tip Simple present opinion. "I think social media is good because you can connect with family." "I think it's bad because people compare themselves." Target: one good thing OR one bad thing + "because." Don't push for both sides at A2.1.
Block 3 · Future Plans (Going to / Will)
Q7
Are you going to post something on social media this week? What?
Teacher Tip Going to for concrete plans. "Yes, I'm going to post a photo of my birthday dinner on Saturday." "No, I'm not going to post anything this week." Simple and personal. Ask: "Are you going to use a filter?"
Q8
Will social media be more fake or more honest in the future? What do you think?
Teacher Tip Will for prediction. "I think it will be more fake because of AI." "I think it will be the same." Short prediction + simple reason. Don't push for complex arguments — one sentence is perfect at A2.1.
Block 4 · Provocative
Q9
Be honest — do YOU use filters on your photos? How many?
Teacher Tip Simple present + honesty. Most people use at least one. "Yes, I always use one filter — just a little." "No, I never use filters but I choose the best photo." The number question makes it fun and specific. Laugh with them — no judgment.
Q10
Your friend posts a terrible photo of you on social media. You look bad. What do you do?
Teacher Tip Simple scenario — past simple or present for response. "I call her and say: delete this photo!" "I comment: please delete this." "I'm going to ask her to take it down." This produces real language they'll actually use. Fun to act out.

Phase 5
The Exit Shot
One situation. What are you going to do?
⏱ 5 min
💣 Exit Shot · A2.1 Social Media: Real or Fake?
You are going to a party tonight. You want to post a photo. But in every photo, you look tired and bad. Your friend looks great in all of them. Are you going to post a photo anyway — or not? What are you going to do?
Homework · WhatsApp Voice Note (1 min)
Record your answer in English. Tell me: are you going to post the photo? And why?
Retention Hook This is a specific, relatable situation everyone has been in. "Going to" for the decision + a real feeling = easy to record. The contrast with the friend who looks great adds just enough tension. End: "Send me the voice note. I want to know — post or no post?"
