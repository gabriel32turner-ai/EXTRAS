My Family
Drama

Every family has a story. A fight that nobody talks about. A person who is always the problem. Let's talk about it — in English.

🪝
The Trigger
Phase 1 · Let's Talk Starter
⏱ 4 min
A simple question to start
"In a survey, 78% of adults said they have a difficult person in their family. Not a bad person — just difficult. Someone who always starts problems, always argues, or always talks too much. Do you have one of those in your family?"
Teacher Script: Read the stat with a smile. Ask: "78% — do you think that number is correct? Or is it higher?" Then laugh a little and ask: "Do you have a difficult person in your family? Who?" At A2.2 the goal is to get the student talking about real people and real situations with simple past verbs. Keep questions short and listen generously.

The Conversation Engine
Phase 4 · 10 Questions · 4 Blocks
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 · Personal Experience — Past Simple
Q1
Did your family have a big fight or problem this year — or last year? What happened?
Let the student tell the story. Keep your follow-up questions short: "Who started it?" / "What did you do?" / "How did it end?" Target: past simple verbs — argued, said, left, called, felt. Give plenty of wait time. A2.2 students need a moment to find the words.
Q2
When was the last time you went to a family dinner or party? Was it fun or difficult?
Good for activating: fun, boring, nice, loud, difficult, long. Push: "Who was there? What did you eat? Was everyone happy?" Target: past simple + simple adjectives. Model: "Last Christmas. It was fun but also a little loud. My uncle always argues about politics."
Q3
Did you ever stop talking to someone in your family for a period of time? What happened?
Connects to Ricardo's bubble. If the student has no personal experience, they can talk about someone they know. Push: "How long? Did you talk again?" Target: past simple + time expressions (for 2 months, after that, then). Key: no judgment — just curiosity.
Block 2 · Opinion
Q4
Do you think family is more important than friends — or can friends be your real family?
Simple opinion question. Push: "Do you have a friend who feels like family?" Target: present simple + I think / I believe. Model: "I think family is important, but some of my friends are more family than my real family. You choose your friends — you don't choose your family."
Q5
Is it OK to say no to your family sometimes — or do you always need to be there for them?
Good for: I think it's OK / I think you should. Target: simple modals (should, can, need to). Model: "I think it's OK to say no sometimes. You need time for yourself too. Family can ask too much sometimes." Keep it conversational and simple.
Q6
Do you think it is better to talk about problems in the family — or is it better to just forget and continue?
Two clear positions. Push: "What do people in YOUR family usually do?" Target: present simple + it depends on. Model: "In my family we usually just forget. We don't like to talk about problems. Maybe it's better to talk — but it's difficult." Simple and real.
Block 3 · Future Plans — Going to / Will
Q7
Are you going to see your family this month? Are you happy about that — or a little nervous?
Target: going to + simple emotions. Push: "Who are you going to see? What are you going to do?" Model: "Yes, I'm going to see my parents next Sunday. I'm happy — but my aunt is going to be there and she always asks about my life. It's a little difficult." Light, personal, real.
Q8
Is there a family problem you want to fix this year? What will you do about it?
Target: will + infinitive for intentions. Push: "Will you talk to that person? Will you call them?" Model: "Yes — I want to talk to my sister. We had a small problem last month. I will call her this week. I think." The "I think" is natural and realistic for A2.2. Validate it.
Block 4 · Past Habits + Reflection — Used to
Q9
Did you use to fight with your brothers or sisters when you were young? What did you fight about?
Target: used to + past simple. Very accessible and fun topic. Push: "What did you fight about most — TV, food, the bathroom?" Model: "Yes! My brother and I used to fight about the TV every weekend. He wanted cartoons, I wanted movies. We always argued." Gets students laughing.
Q10
Is your relationship with your family different now than when you were young? What changed?
Reflection question. Target: past simple + present simple for contrast. Model: "Yes, it's different. When I was young, I used to argue with my mom a lot. Now we are good friends. I understand her better now." Push: "What changed — you or them?" Simple but meaningful.

The Exit Shot
Phase 5 · One Final Question
⏱ 5 min
Final Question
Is there someone in your family that you want to call this week — but you didn't call? Who is it? And are you going to call them?
Homework Loop: "Record a 60-second WhatsApp voice message. Tell me who it is and what you want to say to them — in English. No script. Simple words are fine."
