Are You a
Good Liar?

Everyone lies a little. "I'm almost there." "I love the food." "I'm fine." The question is: are you good at it — or does your face tell the truth?

🪝
The Trigger
Phase 1 · Let's Talk Starter
⏱ 4 min
Simple fact to start
"Researchers say that people lie about 2 times every 10 minutes in conversation. Most lies are small: 'I'm on my way', 'I'm fine', 'I love it'. Are you a good liar — or does everyone know when you lie?"
Teacher Script: Smile and ask directly: "Be honest — are you a good liar? Give me a 1 to 10: 1 is terrible liar, 10 is excellent." Let them pick a number. Then: "What is the last small lie you told — today or yesterday?" The goal is to get students laughing and admitting something real. Keep the energy light and fun.

The Conversation Engine
Phase 4 · 10 Questions · 4 Blocks
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 · Personal Experience — Past Simple
Q1
What was the last small lie you told? To who — your boss, your partner, a friend?
Target: past simple. Deliver with a smile — the word "small" gives permission to be honest. Push: "Why did you lie? Was it a good decision?" Model: "Yesterday. My boss asked 'Are you almost finished?' I said 'yes, 10 more minutes.' It was 1 hour. I didn't want problems." Short, real, past simple.
Q2
Did someone lie to you last month? What did they say — and what was the truth?
Target: past simple + contrast (they said X, but the truth was Y). Push: "How did you feel when you found out?" Model: "Yes. My friend said he was sick and couldn't come to my party. The next day I saw photos of him at a different party. I was angry." Real and relatable.
Q3
Did you ever lie and then feel very bad after?
Target: past simple + emotion. Push: "Did you tell the truth later — or did you keep the lie?" Model: "Yes. I told my mom I was at work. But I was at a party. She called me at 11pm. I felt terrible. The next day I told her the truth. She was angry. But I felt better." Simple guilt narrative.
Block 2 · Opinion — Simple Present
Q4
Is a small lie OK — or is every lie bad?
Simple binary. Push: "Give me an example of a good lie and a bad lie." Target: I think + because. Model: "I think small lies are OK sometimes. 'Your food is delicious' — that's kind. But 'I was at work' when you weren't — that's a problem. It depends on why." Good for clear A2.1 opinion statements.
Q5
Do you think people can see on your face when you lie?
Fun, personal. Push: "What happens to your face?" Target: I think / I don't think + present simple body language description. Model: "Yes. I laugh. I don't know why — I want to lie and I start to laugh. Everyone knows. It's terrible." Gets students describing themselves in English — rich activity.
Q6
Is it OK to lie to protect a person — for example, "No, your presentation was great" when it wasn't?
The "kind lie" question. Push: "Is it better to say the truth — or to protect their feelings?" Target: I think it's OK / I think it's better to + infinitive. Model: "I think it depends. Before the presentation — tell the truth. They can change it. After the presentation — maybe say something kind. It's too late to fix it."
Block 3 · Future Plans — Going to / Will
Q7
This weekend, someone asks you to do something you don't want to do. Are you going to say the truth — or invent an excuse?
Target: going to + real scenario. Push: "What excuse are you going to use?" Model: "I'm going to say I have plans with my family. It's a good excuse. Nobody checks. And it's not a big lie." Gets students producing natural future-tense language about real social situations.
Q8
Your partner asks you: "Do you like my new car?" — and you don't like it. What are you going to say?
Fun scenario. Target: going to + direct speech. Push: "Say the exact words." Model: "I'm going to say 'It's very practical. Good color.' I won't say 'I hate it.' That's not kind. But I also won't say 'beautiful' because that's too much." Get the student to produce the actual sentence — this is speaking practice at its most real.
Block 4 · Provocative
Q9
Be honest — do YOU lie at work sometimes? What do you say?
The self-reflection question. Most students will laugh and admit something. Push: "What is your most common work lie?" Target: present simple + specific examples. Model: "Yes. 'I already sent the email' — when I didn't. 'The computer was slow' — when I was on my phone. Small lies. Normal lies." Gets real, personal English.
Q10
A friend says "You can tell me the truth — I can take it." Do you believe them?
Simple but sharp scenario. Push: "Do you tell the truth — or do you still protect them?" Target: present simple + direct speech. Model: "No, I don't believe them. Nobody really wants the complete truth. They want the truth — but with some kind words. So I say something honest but not too direct." Good closer for the session.
💣
The Exit Shot
Phase 5 · One Final Scenario
⏱ 5 min
Final Question
Your best friend is going to marry someone — and you think it is a big mistake. Your friend asks you: "Are you happy for me?" Tomorrow is the wedding. What are you going to say?
Homework Loop: "Record a 60-second WhatsApp voice message. Tell me what you are going to say to your friend — the exact words, in English. Are you going to lie or tell the truth? Simple words are perfect."
