A2.1 Money & Finance 🟡 Medium 50 min
Do You Give Tips?
How Much?
10%? 15%? Nothing? Every Mexican has a different answer — and a reason for it.
🪝
The Trigger
Phase 1 · Warm-Up
⏱ 4 min
A real number to discuss
"In Mexico, waiters earn about $100–200 pesos a day in salary. Tips are not extra — they ARE the salary. Did you know that?"
Teacher script: Read the stat. Then ask simply: "When was the last time you went to a restaurant? Did you leave a tip? How much?" Wait for the answer. Then: "Is that normal for you — or did you think about it?" This stat usually surprises students and opens the conversation immediately.

The Conversation Engine
Phase 4 · 10 Questions · 4 Blocks
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 · Past Experience
Q1
When was the last time you went to a restaurant? Did you leave a tip? How much?
Teacher Tip Very concrete and easy. Push: "Was the service good? How many people were at the table? Who paid — you or someone else?" All simple past, natural and accessible.
Q2
Did you ever have a waiter or delivery person who was really good? What did they do? Did you give a big tip?
Model Answer "Yes. Last month. A waiter at a pizza place. He was fast, friendly, and he remembered I don't like onions without me saying it. I gave 20%. He was happy."
Q3
Did someone in your family teach you about tips when you were young? What did they say?
Teacher Tip Many Mexicans learned tip habits from parents. "Did your dad always tip? Your mom? Did you think it was normal or strange? What did they say about it?" Activates childhood memories — great for fluency at this level.
Block 2 · Opinion
Q4
Do you think tips are obligatory in Mexico — or is it optional? What do you think?
Teacher Tip Simple opinion question. "Is it a rule or a choice? Do you feel social pressure? What happens if you don't leave a tip — do you feel bad?" Easy yes/no with reasons.
Q5
Is it OK to NOT give a tip when the service is really bad? Or do you tip anyway?
Model Answer "I think it's OK to give less when the service is bad. But I always give something. Maybe 5% instead of 10%. I can't give nothing. I feel too bad about it."
Q6
Do you give tips to other people — not only waiters? Delivery people, parking attendants, gas station workers?
Teacher Tip Great for expanding the conversation beyond restaurants. "What about the person who takes your bags at the supermarket? The person who washes your car? The delivery person at 9pm in the rain?" Real Mexican daily life vocabulary.
Block 3 · Future Plans
Q7
Are you going to a restaurant soon? Are you going to tip? How much are you going to give?
Model Answer "Yes, I'm going to lunch with my family on Sunday. I'm going to give 15%. My mom always gives 10%. We always talk about this. Same conversation, every Sunday."
Q8
Are you going to change how much you tip after today's class? Or will you do the same as always?
Teacher Tip Fun self-reflection with "going to." "Did anything we talked about today change your mind? The stat at the beginning about the $100 peso salary — did that change anything?" Direct connection back to the trigger.
Block 4 · Honest Reflection
Q9
Be honest — do you tip differently when you are with your boss or an important client than when you are alone?
Teacher Tip The social performance question. Very honest and generates good language. "Do you tip more in public? Do you worry what people think? Or do you always tip the same?" Most people laugh because the answer is yes.
Q10
A friend ate with you. The bill was $400 pesos. Your friend left $10 as a tip. What did you do?
Model Answer "I added more money when he wasn't looking. I left $50. I didn't say anything. I didn't want to fight. But I was a little embarrassed."

The Exit Shot
Phase 5 · Final Situation
⏱ 5 min
💣 Exit Shot
You go to a restaurant. The food is very good. But the waiter is slow, unfriendly, and he got your order wrong. At the end, he brings the bill and stands next to the table — waiting for the tip. What are you going to do?
📱 WhatsApp Homework Record 60 seconds in English: What are you going to give — a big tip, a small tip, or nothing? And what are you going to say to the waiter?
