§ 01 · The Trigger
🪝 Mexico and Fast Food
Read aloud · Immediate reaction · No debate yet · 4 min
01
4 min
🍔
INEGI + Euromonitor Fast Food Report 2023
2×
Fast food consumption in Mexico doubled in the last 10 years. Mexico is now the second largest fast food market in Latin America. The average Mexican eats fast food at least once a week.
Source: INEGI Encuesta Nacional de Salud 2023 · Euromonitor Fast Food Mexico
🪝 Opening question — Point at one student and ask:
"When was the last time you ate fast food? What did you order? Did you feel guilty after?"
Teacher Script — 4 min Three short questions in one. Point at one student: "Last fast food — what? When? Guilty?" Accept short answers. 2–3 students. The guilt question is the one that opens the real conversation. Say: "We go deeper in §4."
Pedagogical Fast food is the perfect "guilty pleasure" topic — everyone eats it, most people feel some degree of guilt, and the emotional complexity (pleasure + shame + defensiveness) generates natural language production. The "doubled in 10 years" stat normalizes the behavior while introducing scale. The guilt question is the session engine — note who says yes and who says no. Retention "This is our final Health lab for this series. But your habits — coffee, fast food, sleep — these topics are always relevant. We come back to health topics next season."

§ 03 · Opinion Wall
🧱 4 People. 4 Relationships with Food.
Read all 4 · Pairs 90 sec · "I agree with [name] because..." · Group share
03
10 min
🔁 Processing Question — Ask every student
"Who do you agree with most? Use: I agree with [name] because..."
Provocateur: Álvaro Ríos Álvaro's "I never eat fast food — it's garbage" position is the most challenging because it implicitly judges everyone who does. For a group where everyone probably ate fast food this week, his position creates mild indignation. Push: "Is Álvaro right — or is he just pretending to be perfect?" The defensiveness that generates is productive language.

💣 Exit Shot
It's Friday at 7pm. You are tired. You didn't eat lunch. There is a McDonald's right there. Your diet starts Monday. What are you going to do?
Answer in 2–3 sentences. Be honest. Not the healthy answer — the real one. What are you actually going to do?
📌 A2.1 Model Answer — Show AFTER student speaks
"I am going to go in. I know I am going to order a Big Mac and large fries. I am going to feel bad after — but not very bad. And yes — my diet still starts Monday."
