Things I Do Every Day

Your morning, your routine, your habits. Describe a normal day in your life — action by action.

🎪 Show & Tell💉 Vocab🔀 Choose & Speak🃏 Questions✨ My Sentence
Phase 1
🎪 Show & Tell
Show your alarm clock or your morning app — describe your first hour
5 min
Teacher Models First
"OK, look at my phone — this is my alarm. I wake up at 6:30 every day. First I check my phone. Then I drink coffee — always black, no sugar. I eat breakfast with my family. I leave my house at 7:45. That is my morning. OK, now you — what do YOU do every morning? Show me your alarm!"
Student Sentence Starters
I wake up at ___ every day.
First I ___. Then I ___.
I always / never ___ in the morning.
I leave my house at ___.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • THIS is the quintessential A1.2 topic. Simple present + adverbs of frequency + time expressions = full routine description.
  • Teach sequencers: "first", "then", "after that", "finally". These transform choppy sentences into a narrative.
  • Common error: "I am wake up at 6" → "I WAKE UP at 6." No auxiliary needed in simple present affirmative.
  • Time telling: "at six thirty" not "at six and half." Quick correction, big impact.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "6:30? That is early! I wake up at 7 — you are more disciplined than me!"
  • "Black coffee, no sugar? Same here! Coffee people understand each other."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "You just narrated your entire morning in English. That is not vocabulary — that is communication."

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • MODEL: "I am a MORNING PERSON. I wake up at 6 and I always exercise before work. I never sleep late — never! I can't function at night."
  • 5 pairs instead of 4 — more production at A1.2. Keep ~2 min per pair.
  • "I can" fits naturally in Drive vs Work From Home: "I can save time" / "I can wake up late."
  • Watch for: "I am work from home" → "I WORK from home." Simple present, no auxiliary in affirmative.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Skip breakfast? Really? I can't do that — I need my eggs!"
  • "Night person AND you exercise? You are superhuman!"
Phase 4
🃏 Question Cards
Teacher models first — then you answer
12 min
Question 1
What time do you wake up every day?
"I wake up at 6:30 every day. On weekends I sometimes sleep until 8. But I always wake up early — I can't sleep late."
Now you: "I wake up at... every day."
Tip: Time telling practice. Also: "on weekends" vs "every day" — frequency contrast.
Question 2
What do you do in the morning before work?
"First I take a shower. Then I drink coffee and I eat breakfast. I check my phone and I read the news. I leave at 7:45."
Now you: "First I... Then I..."
Tip: Sequencers are KEY: first, then, after that. Push for at least 3 actions in sequence.
Question 3
Do you drive to work or can you walk?
"I drive to work. It is 25 minutes. The traffic is sometimes terrible in the morning. I can't walk because it is too far."
Now you: "I drive / walk because..."
Tip: "Can/can't" in real context. "Too far" — teach as a chunk if needed.
Question 4
Do you eat lunch at work or at home?
"I eat lunch at work. There is a cafeteria. The food is OK — not great but not bad. I usually eat at 1:30."
Now you: "I eat lunch at... I usually eat at..."
Tip: Two "at" meanings: "at work" (place) and "at 1:30" (time). Good to notice and model both.
Question 5
Do you exercise? When?
"Sometimes. I go to the gym on Monday and Wednesday. I can't go every day because I don't have time. I want to exercise more."
Now you: "I exercise on... / I don't exercise because..."
Tip: "I want to" — accept as a chunk. Negatives are natural here: "I don't have time" is REAL language.
Question 6
What do you do after work?
"After work I go home. I play with my kids. We eat dinner together. Then I watch TV or I read. I go to sleep at 10:30."
Now you: "After work I..."
Tip: "After work" is the transition phrase. This produces the LONGEST answers — evening routines are personal and rich.
Question 7
Is there something you do EVERY day without exception?
"Yes — I drink coffee. Every single day. I always drink coffee in the morning and sometimes in the afternoon. I can't live without coffee!"
Now you: "I always... every day."
Tip: "Without exception" prompts "always" + emphasis. Fun answers guaranteed — coffee, phone, music.
Question 8
Do you like your routine or do you want to change it?
"I like my routine but I want to change some things. I want to exercise more and I want to sleep earlier. But in general my routine is good — it works for me."
Now you: "I like my routine because... but I want to..."
Tip: "But" is the connector star here. Like + but + want = complex A1.2 sentence. Celebrate every "but" they use.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Grammar stars: simple present routines, adverbs of frequency, can/can't, sequencers, time expressions, "but" connector.
  • This is the MOST verb-dense Micro Lab. By Q8, student should be producing 4-5 verbs per answer naturally.
  • Watch for: "I don't can" → "I CAN'T." Modal negative form — critical correction.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "You just narrated an entire day — morning to night — in English. That is not a lesson. That is YOUR life in a new language."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Three A1.2 Micro Labs done: your office people, your favorite place, your daily routine. You can describe your ENTIRE world now."
  • Testimonial trigger: "If someone asks you in English — What do you do every day? — you have a complete answer. Right now."
Phase 5
✨ My Sentence
Build one perfect sentence — practice it — record it
5 min
Complete This Sentence
Every day I ___, I ___, and I ___ because that is my routine and I like / don't like it.
📱 WhatsApp Homework: Record this sentence as a voice note (30 seconds). Say it naturally, like you are telling a friend about your day. Send it to your teacher.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Model: "Every day I wake up early, I drink coffee, and I drive to work because that is my routine and I like it."
  • Three verbs + because + opinion. This is the LONGEST My Sentence in the Micro Lab series so far.
  • Pronunciation: "EV-ry", "rou-TINE", serial comma pause before "and I".
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Three actions, one connector, one opinion — all in ONE sentence. That is English fluency happening."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "You can now describe people, places, and routines in English. In A2 you start talking about the PAST. But today — you own the present."
