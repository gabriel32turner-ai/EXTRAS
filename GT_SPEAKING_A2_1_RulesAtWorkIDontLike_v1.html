§ 01 · The Trigger
🪝 Rules Nobody Asked For

01
4 min
📋
Gallup Workplace Survey 2023
67%
Of workers say there is at least one rule at their job that they do not understand or do not agree with. Many follow the rule anyway — but they are not happy about it.
Source: Gallup State of the Global Workplace 2023
🪝 Opening question — Point at one student and ask:
"Name one rule at your job right now. Do you like it — or not? Why?"
Teacher Script — 4 min Read the stat. Point at one student: "One rule — at your job. Do you like it?" Accept any answer — "we can't use our phones," "we have to arrive at 8am exactly." 2–3 students. Say: "We go much deeper in §4."
Pedagogical This topic is high-energy for corporate professionals. The 67% stat validates their frustration — they are not alone. Expect students to immediately name rules about phones, tardiness, dress code, or absurd bureaucracy. Accept everything — these real-life examples will fuel the entire session. Retention "Next session — People Who Never Pay Back. Different world, same frustration."

🔁 Processing Question — Ask every student
"Who do you agree with most? Use: I agree with [name] because..."
Provocateur: Arturo Jiménez Arturo's "rules exist for a reason — follow them" position is the most challenging for this group of adult professionals who feel controlled. Push: "Is Arturo right? Why do you think he says that?" Don't let students dismiss him — make them argue against a specific point. That friction is the vocabulary practice.

EXIT
💣 Exit Shot
Your company has a new rule starting Monday. You don't like it. Your boss asks you in a meeting: "Do you have any problems with this rule?" What are you going to say?
Answer in 2–3 sentences. Start with yes or no. Be honest — not the polite answer, the real one.
📌 A2.1 Model Answer — Show AFTER student speaks
"Yes, I have a problem with this rule. I think it is not fair because it doesn't help my work — it only makes it more difficult. But I am going to follow it because I don't want problems with my boss."
