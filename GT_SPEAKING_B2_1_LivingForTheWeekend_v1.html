Living for the Weekend

If you're mentally checking out every Monday morning, something is wrong. But is it your job — or you?

🪝
The Trigger
Open the Conversation
⏱ 4 min
Gallup State of the Global Workplace Report 2023
"Only 23% of workers worldwide are engaged at work. That means 77% of employees — nearly 4 in 5 — are either not engaged or actively disengaged. Most people are spending 5 days waiting for 2."
🎙 Teacher Script

Read the stat, then pause. Ask: "Is that you? When you wake up on Monday, what's your first thought?" Let students answer honestly. Follow with: "And when you're finally on Friday afternoon, what does that feel like?" The contrast between those two feelings is the whole conversation.

The Conversation Engine
23 Minutes of Real Talk
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 — Personal Experience
Q1
On a scale from 1 to 10, how much do you look forward to Monday morning? What's actually holding back that number?
This opens vulnerably fast. Let the numbers speak — if most say 3-5, celebrate the honesty. Don't moralize. Ask: "What would need to change for it to be a 7?"
Q2
Have you ever had a job — or a period in your current job — where you actually didn't dread going in? What was different about that time?
Model answer: "Yes, when I first started. Everything was new, I had something to learn every day. Now I could do my job in my sleep. The boredom is the problem, not the work itself."
Q3
What do you actually do on weekends? Is it real rest — or are you just recovering from the week so you can survive the next one?
This distinction hits hard. Recovery ≠ living. If weekends are just "recharging for work," the question becomes: who is the weekend actually for?
Block 2 — Opinion
Q4
Do you think it's realistic — or even healthy — to expect your job to be meaningful and fulfilling? Or is that an unrealistic standard most people can't meet?
No right answer. Push both sides: "If you agree it's unrealistic, how do you feel about spending 35 years at something that just... pays?" And: "If you expect meaning, what are you doing to create it?"
Q5
Is there a difference between "I work to live" as a healthy mindset — and "I work to live" as an excuse not to try to improve your situation?
This is the nuance question. Both can be true at the same time. The honest test: are you at peace with your situation — or are you using acceptance to avoid the fear of change?
Q6
If you had kids, would you want them to see your relationship with work — and think it's normal? Why or why not?
Deeply personal and usually the most honest answer of the session. Don't rush it. Let the silence do the work if someone pauses.
Block 3 — Hypothetical
Q7
If you won 5 million pesos tomorrow and didn't need to work, would you still work? What would you do — and why?
Classic engagement test. Follow up: "If what you'd do with 5 million is completely different from what you do now — what does that tell you?" Don't answer it for them.
Q8
If you could redesign your current job — same salary, same company — but change 3 things about it, what would you change first?
This reveals what's actually wrong. Most people name: the commute, their boss, or the meetings. Rarely the actual work. That's the insight.
Block 4 — Provocative
Q9
Beatriz said that if you're mentally checking out every Monday, it's your problem to solve. Is she right — or is that victim-blaming people who are stuck in bad jobs?
Push them to hold both: "Can Beatriz be right AND can the system be unfair at the same time?" The most mature answer is yes — and it's uncomfortable.
Q10
If you knew you'd be doing exactly this job for the next 20 years — no promotion, no change — would you be okay with that? Be honest.
The hardest question. It forces students to confront whether they're investing in their current role or just enduring it. There's no right answer — but the honest answer always matters.

The Exit Shot
One Question. No Easy Answer.
⏱ 5 min
💣 Final Dilemma
You're offered a job that pays 15% less than your current salary — but it's something you'd actually enjoy going to every day. You have a mortgage, a car payment, and two kids in private school. The numbers barely work. Do you take it — or do you stay where you are and keep counting down to Fridays?
