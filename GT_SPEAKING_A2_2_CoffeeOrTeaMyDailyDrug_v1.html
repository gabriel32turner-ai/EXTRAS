🪝 Opening Question — Read and point at someone
"This morning — did you drink coffee, tea, or something else first? How many cups today?"
Teacher Mechanics — 4 min Binary + number. Point at one student: "You — coffee or tea this morning? How many?" Very simple. 2–3 answers. Then: "OK — let's talk about our daily drink habit."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Coffee/tea is the most universally low-stakes personal topic — everyone has a habit, everyone has an opinion. Zero anxiety to activate.
  • The "tea = only when sick" stat is very relatable in Mexico. Use it: "Is that you? Do you only drink tea when you're sick?"
  • Accept any answer: "I drink 3 coffees" / "I only drink water" / "I don't like coffee." All perfect A2.2 openers.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "68% of Mexicans drink coffee before breakfast. Raise your hand if that's you."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Retention: "This is our last Health lab for this lote. Great topic to keep talking about — we all have a coffee story."

⚡ Production Challenge
Pick 3 words. One sentence each about your coffee or tea habit. Short and real. Just talk.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • "Mug" vs "cup" — quick clarification: a mug is bigger and usually ceramic; a cup is smaller. Both are valid in conversation. "I drink 3 mugs of coffee" is more common than 3 cups for heavy drinkers.
  • "Caffeine" — most students know this word. Confirm pronunciation: KAF-een. It's often mispronounced.
  • Best trigger: "Three words that describe YOUR coffee or tea life. One sentence each."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "These are words you can use at any café, any office kitchen, any breakfast conversation in English."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "If you travel or work with English speakers, knowing how to order and talk about drinks is the most basic daily skill."

🔁 Processing Question — Pairs → Group
"Who do you agree with? Are you a coffee or tea person? Say 2 or 3 sentences."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • The provocateur is Lucía — she quit coffee and says she feels great. For heavy coffee drinkers, this is a friendly challenge. "No way! I can't stop!" is a perfect A2.2 reaction.
  • Read all 4, then ask: "Coffee or tea? Raise your hand for coffee." Quick visual poll before opening to pairs.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • After the poll: "OK — now tell me WHY. One sentence. Why coffee? Why tea?"
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Testimonial trigger: "Which person drinks most like you? Tell me in one sentence."
§ 04 · The Conversation Engine
🗣 10 Questions. 4 Blocks. 23 Minutes.
Past → Opinion → Future → Past habits · Short answers OK · Keep moving
04
23 min
⚡
A2.2 Rule: Short answers are OK. 1 or 2 sentences is great. Don't wait for perfect English — just talk.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • This is the most socially light-weight session in the lote — no career, no money, no stress. Let it be fun. Laughing while talking English is still talking English.
  • Block 1: "When was the last time you had coffee?" — easy past simple. Most students will say "this morning." Push: "What kind? How did you take it — black, with milk, with sugar?"
  • Block 4 "used to": "Did you use to drink coffee as a child?" — many Mexicans did! Café de olla at grandmother's house is a very common childhood memory.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • Q5 — "Is coffee bad for your health?" — generates confident simple opinions. "Yes, but I don't care!" is also a valid answer.
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • After Q10: "You talked about coffee, tea, habits, and health — in English. All of it. That's A2.2."
§ 05 · The Exit Shot
💣 One Question. Your Answer.
5 min · No chips · Answer in 2–3 sentences · Then the loop
05
5 min
EXIT
💣 Exit Shot
Are you going to drink less coffee — or try tea — this week? What is your plan?
Answer in 2 or 3 sentences. Use "I am going to..." Say yes or no — and tell me why.
📌 A2.2 Model Answer — Reveal AFTER student speaks
"No, I am not going to drink less coffee. I love it too much and I need it to work. But I am going to try to not drink coffee after 3pm. Maybe that will help my sleep."
🔁 Retention Loop — WhatsApp Homework "Before next session — record a 60-second voice note in English. Your answer to today's Exit Shot. Don't write it first. Just talk. Send it to [teacher number]."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • The Exit Shot gives students permission to say NO — "I am NOT going to drink less." That's also a valid "going to" construction. Don't push for a positive behavioral change; push for honest English production.
  • The "no coffee after 3pm" detail in the model answer also bridges back to the Sleep lab — make the connection explicit if students noticed it.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • The model answer is honest and funny — "I love it too much." Point out: "That's real English. Honest, simple, and clear. That's the goal."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Closing: "Record tonight. Tell me your coffee plan in English. 60 seconds. Go."
