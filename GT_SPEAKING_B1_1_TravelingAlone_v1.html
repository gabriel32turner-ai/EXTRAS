Traveling Alone:
Brave or Sad?
One ticket, one suitcase, no plans to share. Is solo travel freedom — or is it just loneliness with better photos?
🪝
The Trigger
Phase 1 · Provocation & Opening
⏱ 4 min
BBC Travel · 2023
"Solo travel has grown 42% since 2019 — and the biggest group doing it is not young backpackers. It's adults aged 35–55 who say they travel alone because nobody in their life wants to go where they want to go."
Teacher script: Read it. Then ask: "Have you ever traveled alone — even just for work? How did it feel?" Wait. Then: "Is there a place you really want to go that nobody in your life wants to visit with you?" That second question usually surprises people. It's personal and it lands fast.

The Conversation Engine
Phase 4 · 10 Questions · 4 Blocks
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 · Personal Experience
Q1
Have you ever traveled alone — even for just one or two days? Where did you go, and how did it feel?
Teacher Tip Work trips count. Push: "Was it different from traveling with other people? What did you do that you wouldn't have done with someone else? Did you feel lonely at any moment?"
Q2
Have you ever wanted to go somewhere but nobody wanted to go with you? What did you do?
Model Answer "Yes — I wanted to go to Oaxaca two years ago. My husband didn't want to and my friends were busy. I almost went alone. Then I didn't. I still think about it. I think I should have just gone."
Q3
Have you ever had a bad travel experience because of the person you were traveling with?
Teacher Tip Almost everyone has this story. Keep it fun: "What happened? Did you argue? Did you stop speaking? Did the trip survive — or did the friendship not survive the trip?" Great for storytelling vocabulary.
Block 2 · Opinion
Q4
Is it brave to travel alone — or is "brave" the wrong word?
Teacher Tip Push them to find the right word. "If not brave — then what? Independent? Lonely? Normal? Boring? Which word do you really think describes it?" The vocabulary search is the exercise.
Q5
Do you think people who travel alone are happier than people who always travel in groups? Why?
Model Answer "I don't think it's about happy or not happy. I think solo travelers know themselves better. They know what they want. People who always travel in groups sometimes don't know what they want — they just go where everyone else goes."
Q6
Is it OK for a married person to take a trip alone — without their partner? Or is that strange?
Teacher Tip Great cultural debate for Mexican professionals. "Would you be OK with your partner going on a trip alone for 5 days? Would they be OK with you doing it? Why or why not?" The double standard often appears here.
Block 3 · Hypothetical
Q7
What would you do if you had 5 free days and $10,000 pesos — and nobody could go with you?
Model Answer "Honestly? I think I would go. Maybe somewhere close at first — Oaxaca or San Cristóbal. I would feel nervous. But I think that nervous feeling is exactly why I should do it."
Q8
What would you do if your best friend called you and said: "I booked a solo trip to Europe for 3 weeks. I'm going alone." Would you think it was great — or would you worry?
Teacher Tip Interesting because the answer often reveals the student's own fears. "What specifically would you worry about? Safety? Loneliness? Or something else?" Push gently.
Block 4 · Provocative
Q9
Do you think you could spend 7 days completely alone — no friends, no family, no partner — and enjoy it? Or would you go crazy?
Teacher Tip This is really a question about who they are when nobody is watching. "What would be the hardest part? Eating alone? Sleeping alone? Not having anyone to talk to? Or something deeper?"
Q10
Is there a trip you have been dreaming about for years that you still haven't taken? What is stopping you — really?
Model Answer "Yes. I want to go to Japan. I've wanted to go for 8 years. The real reason I haven't gone is not money. It's that I keep waiting for the right person to go with. And that person hasn't appeared yet. Maybe I'm the right person."

The Exit Shot
Phase 5 · Final Dilemma · No right answer
⏱ 5 min
💣 Exit Shot
You find a cheap flight — $3,500 pesos round trip to a city you have always wanted to visit. The trip is in 3 weeks. Nobody can go with you. Your family thinks it's strange to travel alone. Your partner is nervous about it. But the ticket is there, the time is there, and part of you really wants to go. Do you buy the ticket?
📱 WhatsApp Homework Record 60 seconds in English: Do you buy the ticket — yes or no? And where are you going?
