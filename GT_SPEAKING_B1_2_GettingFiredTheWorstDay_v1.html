§ 01 · The Trigger
🪝 The Number Nobody Talks About
Read aloud or project · First reaction only · No debate yet · 4 min max
01
4 min
📋
Gallup State of the Global Workplace + LinkedIn Workforce Report 2023
1 in 5
Workers have been fired or laid off at least once in their career — most with no warning
67%
Of people who were fired say they never received clear feedback before the termination
3× faster
People who were fired at least once find their next best job faster than those who quit voluntarily
78%
Say being fired was one of the most stressful experiences of their life — worse than divorce, worse than moving
Sources: Gallup State of the Global Workplace (2023) · LinkedIn Workforce Confidence Report · Harvard Business Review "The Productive Failure" (2022)
🪝 Opening Trigger — Read this slowly, then point at someone
"In 2022, Elon Musk fired 50% of Twitter's workforce in one day. Some people found out via email. Others lost access to their computers before the email arrived. Is there a right way to fire someone — or is it always just bad?"
Teacher Mechanics — 4 min Read the Twitter/Musk story once, slowly. Then point at one student: "First reaction — is there a right way to fire someone?" Don't judge the answer. Wait 5 seconds for silence. After 2–3 reactions, say: "Hold that thought. Everything comes back in §4."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • This topic is almost universal in Mexican corporate settings — most students know someone who was fired, or fear it themselves. Use that. The emotional activation is instant.
  • If a student has personally been fired and starts to share immediately — let them speak for 30 seconds max, then say: "We're going to come back to that story in §4. Save it."
  • The 67% stat (no feedback before termination) is the one that generates the most reaction. Mark who responds to it — they'll lead Q3 and Q6.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • The Musk/Twitter reference works in Mexico — it was everywhere in the news. If students don't know it: "The guy who bought Twitter fired half the company by email. Before the email, people lost access to their computers. No conversation, no explanation."
  • Best activation question if the group is quiet: "How many of you have a colleague who was fired without warning? Raise your hand."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Retention line: "This session is about something that nobody teaches you in school — how to talk about failure, loss, and starting over. In English. That's a professional skill."
  • Referral trigger: the Musk stat is WhatsApp-shareable. After class: send it in English.
§ 02 · Vocab Injection
💉 20 Words That Matter
10 nouns + 10 verbs · Click to hear · Pairs simultaneous · Production challenge after
02
8 min
Pairs Simultaneous
Person A reads definition — Person B produces the word. Switch. Then production challenge.
Challenge Mode Available
Click any word to hear pronunciation
💡 10 Nouns — The Job Loss World
🔵 10 Verbs — What Happens Before, During & After
⚡ Production Challenge — No preparation
Each person picks 3 words. One sentence each about a real situation at your job — past or present. No writing. Just talk.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • "Laid off" vs "fired" is a key distinction — clarify if it comes up: laid off = company decision (no wrongdoing), fired = performance or behavior issue. Many students use them interchangeably.
  • Best production challenge trigger: "Which 3 words describe what happened to someone you know at work? Go."
  • If a student uses "I was fired" in the production challenge — don't react emotionally. Treat it as language. Celebrate the grammar, not the story.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • These are real Monday-morning words. Say it: "If something happens at work today and you need to tell someone in English — these are the words."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "These words appear in resignation letters, performance reviews, and LinkedIn posts. You'll need them."
§ 03 · Opinion Wall
🧱 4 Real Voices. 4 Different Reactions.
Read each card · Pairs discuss 90 sec · Group shares · Same question each time
03
10 min
Processing Question — Same for all 4 voices
"Who do you agree with most — and who surprises you most? Why?"
Don't vary the question
🔁 Processing Question — Pairs → Group
"Who do you agree with most — and who surprises you most? Why?"
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Read all 4 cards before opening debate. The contrast between Valentina (relief) and Marco (shame) is the tension that drives the conversation.
  • The provocateur is Ing. Torres. His opinion will create immediate resistance — especially from younger students. Let the resistance be the fuel. Push: "He's your boss. Is he wrong?"
  • Don't let discussion become personal storytelling. Redirect with: "We'll get to your story in §4. Right now — which of these four people is closest to how you think?"
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • Best activation: read Ing. Torres in a slightly authoritative voice. Read Valentina with surprise. The delivery creates contrast that the group reacts to.
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Testimonial trigger: "In one sentence — which of these four sounds most like someone you actually know?"
§ 04 · The Conversation Engine
🗣 10 Questions. 4 Blocks. 23 Minutes.
Personal → Opinion → Hypothetical → Provocative · No round-robin · Point and push
04
23 min
⚡
Rule: Minimum 2 sentences per answer. Position first. Reason second. One finger up = more. Two fingers = next question.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Block 1 uses present perfect — monitor "Have you ever been..." responses. If students answer in simple past only ("I was fired"), gently recycle: "So you have been fired — tell me more."
  • Block 3 (second conditional) — watch for "If I would be fired" (Spanish interference). Recycle at the end: "If I were fired, I would..." Don't interrupt the flow.
  • Q9 (self-reflection) is the most honest question of the session. Give it full time — don't rush to Q10.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • Celebrate when someone uses vocab from §2 in their answer: "You just used 'severance' in a real sentence. That's B1.2 output."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • After Q10: "You just talked about one of the hardest things that can happen at work — in English. Not translating in your head. Talking."
  • Upsell: "Next Speaking Lab goes into money — salary, debt, savings. Same depth. Same format."
§ 05 · The Exit Shot
💣 One Question. No Easy Answer.
5 min · No chips · Just position + reason · Then the loop
05
5 min
EXIT
💣 Exit Shot
You've been at your job for 6 years. Your boss calls you in on a Friday at 4pm. He says: "This is your last day." No explanation. No warning. You have a mortgage and one kid in school. What is the first thing you do — and the first thing you say?
Answer in 3 sentences minimum. Be honest — not brave. What would you actually do?
📌 B1.2 Model Answer — Reveal AFTER student speaks
"Honestly, I think I would go quiet. I wouldn't know what to say. The first thing I would do is ask for something in writing — because I've heard that you should always have documentation. Then I would call my partner from the parking lot. I wouldn't be brave. I would be scared. And I think that's normal."
🔁 Retention Loop — The WhatsApp Homework "Before our next session — record a 60-second voice note in English. Your answer to today's Exit Shot. Don't write it first. Don't rehearse it. Just talk. Send it to [teacher number]."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • The scenario is concrete: 6 years, Friday 4pm, mortgage, one kid. The specificity forces a real decision. If a student gives a generic answer ("I would look for another job"), push: "Before that — right now, in that room, what do you say to your boss?"
  • After the student answers: one follow-up only — "What would you do differently if you had seen it coming?" This activates second conditional naturally.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • The model answer uses honest, non-heroic language — "I would be scared. And I think that's normal." This gives students permission to be vulnerable in English. That vulnerability is the most powerful learning state.
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Closing line: "You just described a moment of real human fear — in English. That's not a textbook exercise. That's language that works when it counts."
  • Referral trigger: "If someone you know is taking a basic English class doing grammar exercises, they've never had a conversation like this one. That's the difference."
