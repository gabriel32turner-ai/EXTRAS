My Weekend Routine

What do you do when you don't work? Tell us everything.

🎪 Show & Tell 5 min

Show a photo from a recent weekend and describe what you do.

Your Task

Open your phone. Find a photo from your last weekend — breakfast, a park, your family, your sofa. Show it and describe what you usually do on weekends.

Teacher Script — Model First

"Look — this is my Saturday morning. I wake up late — usually at 9. I drink coffee and I read the news on my phone. I don't work on Saturdays. I sometimes go to the park with my dog. OK — show me YOUR photo. What do you do on weekends?"

Sentence Starters

On weekends, I usually _______ .
I always _______ in the morning.
I sometimes _______ with my family.
I never _______ on Sundays.

Teacher Tip

Push for adverbs of frequency: "Do you ALWAYS do that? Or just SOMETIMES?" This is a natural way to drill always/usually/sometimes/never. If they produce 3+ sentences with different adverbs, celebrate. Correct third person if they slip: "My wife go..." → "My wife GOES."

Drill "errands" — students don't know this word and it's GOLD for weekend conversations. Model: "I do errands on Saturday — I buy groceries, I go to the bank, I go to the pharmacy." Also "sleep in" is a phrasal verb they love — it feels informal and cool. Drill: "SLEEP in", not "sleep IN".

🃏 Question Cards 12 min

Teacher models first. Then you personalize.

Question 01
What do you do on Saturday mornings?
Now you: describe YOUR Saturday morning step by step.
Teacher Model

"On Saturday mornings I wake up late. I don't use an alarm. I make coffee and I read the news. Sometimes I cook breakfast — eggs and toast. I never check work emails on Saturdays."

Teacher Tip

Push for sequence: "First I... then I... after that I..." This builds natural discourse flow at A1.2.

Question 02
Do you like Sundays or Saturdays more?
Now you: which day do YOU like more and why?
Teacher Model

"I like Saturdays more because I have the full day free. I do errands in the morning and I relax in the afternoon. Sundays are good but I always think about Monday — and I don't like that."

Teacher Tip

The "Sunday anxiety" is universal — everyone relates. Push: "Do you think about Monday on Sunday night?" Generates great spontaneous language.

Question 03
Can you cook? What do you cook on weekends?
Now you: do YOU cook? What?
Teacher Model

"Yes, I can cook! I cook pasta on Saturdays. I can make a good salad too. But I can't cook fish — it's difficult for me. My wife cooks fish."

Teacher Tip

Great can/can't practice. Push for specifics: "Can you cook tacos? Can you cook Italian food? What CAN'T you cook?" Every answer is a sentence.

Question 04
Do you exercise on weekends?
Now you: what do YOU do to move your body?
Teacher Model

"Sometimes I exercise on Saturdays. I go to the gym or I walk in the park. I never exercise on Sundays — Sundays are for rest. I can run 5 kilometers but I don't like it."

Teacher Tip

Mix frequency adverbs + can: "Can you run? Do you ALWAYS go to the gym?" This combines two grammar targets naturally.

Question 05
What do you usually eat on weekends?
Now you: describe YOUR weekend meals.
Teacher Model

"On Saturdays I eat a big breakfast — eggs, coffee, fruit. For lunch, I sometimes eat tacos or pizza. I don't eat dinner late on weekends. I like simple food."

Teacher Tip

Food is always engaging. Push for all three meals: "What do you eat for breakfast? And lunch? And dinner?" Three meals = minimum 6 sentences.

Question 06
Do you spend time with your family on weekends?
Now you: who do YOU spend time with?
Teacher Model

"Yes, I always spend time with my family on Sundays. We eat together. We watch a movie sometimes. I visit my parents every two weeks. Family time is important for me."

Teacher Tip

Push for specifics: "What do you DO with your family? Do you eat together? Do you watch TV?" Each activity = one more sentence.

Question 07
Is there a park or a nice place near your house?
Now you: what is near YOUR house?
Teacher Model

"Yes, there is a park near my house. It is big and there are many trees. I go there on Saturdays. There is also a coffee shop and I sometimes buy coffee there."

Teacher Tip

Great "there is/are" practice. Push: "Is there a gym near your house? Is there a restaurant? Are there stores?" Each answer uses the target structure.

Question 08
What don't you like about weekends?
Now you: what is the WORST part of YOUR weekend?
Teacher Model

"I don't like chores. I clean the house on Saturdays and I don't enjoy it. I also don't like Sunday nights because Monday is coming. Weekends are too short!"

Teacher Tip

Negative sentences are gold at A1.2. Push for 2-3 "I don't like..." sentences. End on "Weekends are too short!" — everyone agrees and it closes the section with energy.

✨ My Sentence 5 min

Build one perfect sentence. Practice it. Record it.

I like my weekends because I _______ and I spend time with _______ .

🎙️ Record this sentence as a WhatsApp voice note (30 seconds).

Teacher Tip

Target: "I like my weekends because I sleep in and I spend time with my family." Push for natural rhythm — no pauses between words. Three repetitions, then record. This sentence has 2 verbs + because + connector "and" — that's A1.2 gold.
