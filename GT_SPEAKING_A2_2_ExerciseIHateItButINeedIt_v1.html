Exercise: I Hate It
But I Need It
You know you should exercise. But the sofa is so comfortable. The gym is so far. Sound familiar?
A2.2Health & Wellness🟡 Medium50 min
🪝 Trigger 💉 Vocab 🧱 Opinions 🗣 Conversation 💣 Exit Shot
🪝 Phase 1 — The Trigger
The Question
Read it. Then ask the student directly.
4 min
01
A simple question to start
"When was the last time you exercised? Not walked to the car — really exercised."
— This question usually gets a long pause. That pause says everything.
A simple fact
"Most people know that exercise is good for them. But more than 70% of adults don't exercise enough. Knowing is easy. Doing is hard."
— Today we talk about that gap — between knowing and doing.
Launch line: "Quick question — on a scale of 1 to 5: how much do you like exercise? 1 is 'I hate it' and 5 is 'I love it.'" [Let them answer.] "Perfect. Today we talk about exercise — why we need it, why we avoid it, and what we actually do." Keep the energy light. This topic always generates honest, funny answers at A2.2.

🗣 Phase 4 — The Conversation Engine
10 Questions · 4 Blocks
Short questions. Honest answers. Use simple sentences.
23 min
04
Block 1 — Personal Experience (Past Simple)
Q1
When was the last time you exercised? What did you do?
Past simple — recent exercise story. The honest answer is usually funny. Model: "Last week I ran for 10 minutes. Then I stopped. I was very tired." Or: "Last year. I walked to the store. That was my exercise." Accept any answer — the grammar is the goal, not the achievement.
Q2
Did you play a sport when you were a child? What sport? Did you like it?
Past simple — childhood sports memory. Very natural and warm. Model: "Yes, I played football every afternoon after school. I was not very good but I loved it. I played with my neighbors." Three short sentences is excellent A2.2 production.
Q3
Did you ever try to start an exercise routine and stop? What happened?
Past simple — the classic "I tried and stopped" story. Diego's story. Most students have this. Model: "Yes! Two years ago I went to the gym every day for three weeks. Then I stopped. I don't know why. I was tired. The gym was far." Activate "Tell me about it!" as a reaction.
Block 2 — Opinion (Simple Present)
Q4
Do you think exercise is important? Or is it okay to not exercise?
Simple opinion. Most say "yes, it's important" — even Rodrigo types. The interesting follow-up: "Do you think that — and do you exercise?" The gap between belief and action is the fun part. Model: "I think it's very important. And I don't do it. I know. It's a problem."
Q5
What do you think is easier — going to the gym or exercising at home?
Simple comparison opinion. Model: "I think home is easier. You don't need to travel. You don't need to pay. But at home I always stop after 5 minutes because the sofa is there." Both options are valid — the grammar is the same. Activate: sofa, gym, excuse.
Q6
Is walking enough exercise — or do you need to do more?
Lucía's position. Most students feel relieved when walking is accepted as exercise. Model: "I think walking is good! It's not a lot, but it's something. Better than nothing." Or: "I think you need more. Walking is nice but it's not exercise." Both are honest and grammatically on target.
Block 3 — Future Plans (Going to / Will)
Q7
Are you going to start exercising — or exercise more — this year? What are you going to do?
Going to — future plan. The classic New Year resolution question but for any time of year. Model: "I'm going to start walking every morning. 20 minutes. I'm going to do it on Monday." Or: "I'm not going to lie — probably not. But I want to try." Both are valid and funny. Help with "I'm going to + verb."
Q8
Will you feel different — more energy, less tired — if you exercise every week?
Will — future prediction. Model: "Yes, I think I will feel better. More energy in the morning. Maybe I will sleep better too." Or: "Maybe. I don't know. I never did it long enough to know!" Both answers are honest. Short sentences are perfect here.
Block 4 — Past Habits + Reflection (Used to)
Q9
Did you use to exercise more when you were younger? What did you do?
Used to — past habit comparison with present. Almost everyone exercised more as a child or young adult. Model: "Yes! I used to play football every weekend. And I walked a lot. Now I drive everywhere. I don't walk as much." Natural and personal. Help with "I used to + verb."
Q10
Did you use to think exercise was fun — or was it always difficult for you?
Used to + attitude/feeling about exercise. Light closing question. Model: "I used to love running! When I was 20. Now I hate it. My body is different. Or maybe I'm just lazy." Or: "I never liked exercise. I used to hate PE class at school. Nothing has changed!" Both are funny and honest.
A2.2 grammar targets: Past simple (Q1-3), simple present opinions (Q4-6), going to / will (Q7-8), used to (Q9-10).

Tone note: Keep this session fun and non-judgmental. The humor works because everyone relates. Never praise or shame exercise habits — just accept all answers equally. The student who says "I haven't exercised since 2019" should feel as comfortable as the one who runs every day.
💣 Phase 5 — The Exit Shot
The Final Question
One question. Simple and personal. Answer in 2-3 sentences.
5 min
05
💣 Exit Shot
This week — just this week — what is ONE thing you are going to do for your health? It can be very small. Walk 10 minutes. Drink more water. Sleep earlier. What is your plan?
🎙 WhatsApp Homework: Record a 60-second voice message in English: "Tell me about your relationship with exercise. Do you like it or hate it? What did you do last week for your health? And what are you going to do next week?" Send before our next session.
Why it works: "Going to" embedded naturally. The "just this week" and "very small" qualifiers remove pressure — this is not a life plan, just one action. Students feel capable of answering. The specificity ("walk 10 minutes, drink more water") gives language scaffolding without making it feel like a test.

Homework note: Three natural questions that activate past simple + going to + simple present in a personal, non-academic context. Very A2.2 appropriate. High completion expected because the topic is personal and low-stakes.

Upsell moment: "Next session we talk about fast food — another health topic but completely different energy. More fun, a little more guilty."
