Office Friendships:
Real or Fake?

The people you see every day at work — are they really your friends?

🪝
The Trigger
Phase 1 · Open the conversation
4 min
A simple question
"Think about your 3 best friends. How many of them are from work — and how many are from outside work?"
Teacher script: Ask students to think for 5 seconds. Then: "How many of your best friends are from work? Tell me — one, two, three? Or zero?" Take a quick count. Most will say zero or one. Use that to open: "Interesting. So are work friends real friends — or just nice people you see every day?"

The Conversation Engine
Phase 4 · 10 questions · 4 blocks
23 min
Block 1 — Past Experience
Q1
Did you ever make a real friend at work? Tell me about that person.
Teacher tip: Help with: "I met [name] at work. We used to..." / "We ate lunch together and..." Push for 3–4 sentences. What did they do together? Do they still talk?
Q2
Did you ever have a problem with a colleague at work? What happened?
Model answer: "Yes. One time, a colleague took my lunch from the fridge. I was very angry. I didn't say anything. I just bought food from outside. It was a stupid problem, but I was really hungry and mad."
Q3
When was the last time you talked with an old colleague — someone from a past job?
Teacher tip: Great for practicing simple past time expressions: "Last month...", "Two years ago...", "I don't remember..." Reference Fernando's story — did anyone keep in touch after leaving?
Block 2 — Opinion
Q4
Do you think it's good to be friends with your boss outside of work?
Teacher tip: Simple yes/no with a reason. Help with: "I think it's good because..." / "I think it's a problem because..." Most students have a clear opinion — let them state it simply.
Q5
Is it OK to talk about personal problems with a colleague at work?
Model answer: "I think it's OK with one person — someone you really trust. But not with everyone. At work, some people like to talk about other people's problems. So I am careful."
Q6
Do you think people who work from home are more lonely than people who go to the office?
Teacher tip: Reference Ricardo's opinion. Very relatable topic. Push: "Did you ever work from home? How did you feel?" Simple comparison: office vs. home.
Block 3 — Future Plans
Q7
Are you going to have lunch or coffee with a colleague this week?
Teacher tip: Simple "going to" practice. Help with: "Yes, I'm going to..." / "No, I usually eat alone because..." Follow up: "Who do you usually eat with at work?"
Q8
If you change jobs in the future, will you stay in contact with your current colleagues?
Model answer: "I think I will stay in contact with two or three people. Not everyone. There is one colleague I really like. I will send her messages. The others — probably not. That's just how it is."
Block 4 — Past Habits & Reflection
Q9
Did you use to have lunch with your colleagues at a past job — or did you usually eat alone?
Teacher tip: "Used to" practice. Help with: "At my old job, I used to eat with..." / "I used to go to a restaurant near the office..." Compare to current job naturally.
Q10
Think about your current colleagues. Which one are you closest to — and why?
Teacher tip: Nice closing question. Simple descriptive answers. Help with: "I am closest to [name] because she/he is..." + adjective. Students often discover they haven't thought about this before — that moment of reflection is the goal.
💣
The Exit Shot
Phase 5 · One final question
5 min
💣 Exit Shot
Think of one person at your job right now. Are they a real friend — or just a nice colleague? How do you know the difference?

Homework — WhatsApp Voice Note
Record a 60-second voice message in English. Describe one colleague — their name, what they do, and why they are (or are not) a real friend. Use: "I met [name] at work...", "We usually...", "I think they are / are not a real friend because..."
