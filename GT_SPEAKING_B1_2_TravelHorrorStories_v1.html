Travel Horror Stories

Lost luggage, missed flights, scams, food poisoning — the trips you'll never forget for the wrong reasons.

🪝
The Trigger
Phase 1 · Open the conversation
4 min
The Guardian · Travel Section
"Airlines lost or mishandled over 26 million bags last year. And yet, travel experts agree: the stories people tell most passionately are never about the perfect trips."
Teacher script: Read the stat, then say: "Think about a trip that went wrong — even a little. A missed bus, a terrible hotel, food that destroyed you. Give me 10 seconds to think... Now, who has the worst story in this room?" The competition for worst story is the best warm-up in this session.

The Conversation Engine
Phase 4 · 10 questions · 4 blocks
23 min
Block 1 — Personal Experience
Q1
Have you ever missed a flight or a bus? What happened, and how did you handle it?
Teacher tip: Almost everyone has a version of this. Push for the emotional detail: "What was going through your head in that moment? Who did you call first?"
Q2
Have you ever been scammed or ripped off while travelling? How much did it cost you?
Model answer: "Yes, in a taxi in Cancún. The driver said the meter was broken and charged me three times the normal price. I paid because I didn't know the city. I've been checking prices on my phone before getting in a taxi ever since."
Q3
Have you ever stayed in a hotel or Airbnb that was nothing like the photos? What did you do?
Teacher tip: Follow up: "Did you complain? Did you leave a bad review? Or did you say nothing?" The willingness to complain in a foreign country is a rich topic.
Block 2 — Opinion
Q4
Do you think travel problems make a trip more memorable, like Valeria says — or do they just ruin it?
Teacher tip: Split the room. Ask who agrees with Valeria and who disagrees. Make each side explain with a specific example from their own experience.
Q5
In your opinion, who is responsible when a trip goes wrong — the traveller, the airline, the hotel, or the country?
Model answer: "I think it depends on the situation. If my bag is lost, that's the airline. If I get scammed, that's partly my fault for not being more careful. If a destination is dangerous and nobody warned me — that's a government problem."
Q6
What do you think about people who travel to dangerous places on purpose — war zones, politically unstable countries, extreme environments? Is that brave or irresponsible?
Teacher tip: Natural debate question. Strong opinions on both sides. Push students to justify: "Where do you draw the line between adventure and recklessness?"
Block 3 — Hypothetical
Q7
What would you do if you arrived at your destination and your hotel said they had no record of your reservation?
Model answer: "I would show them my confirmation email immediately. If they still couldn't help me, I would call the booking platform on the spot and ask them to find me something nearby at their expense. I would stay calm but very clear."
Q8
What would you do if you got seriously sick in a foreign country and your travel insurance didn't cover the treatment?
Teacher tip: This question reveals how prepared students actually are. Good follow-up: "Do you always buy travel insurance? Why or why not?"
Block 4 — Provocative
Q9
Have you ever made a travel decision that put you or someone else at risk — and you knew it at the time?
Teacher tip: This surfaces honest stories. Common answers: taking unsafe transport to save money, going out alone at night in an unfamiliar city, ignoring local warnings. Don't judge — let the story come out.
Q10
Be honest: have you ever exaggerated a travel horror story to make it sound worse than it was?
Teacher tip: The best closing question of this session. Almost everyone says yes with a laugh. Follow up: "Why do we do that? What does it say about how we use travel stories socially?"

The Exit Shot
Phase 5 · One final question
5 min
💣 Exit Shot
You are at the airport. Your flight is delayed 11 hours. You have no lounge access, $30 in cash, your phone is at 8% battery, and the charging stations are all occupied. There is one other traveller next to you who also looks completely lost. What do you actually do for the next 11 hours?
