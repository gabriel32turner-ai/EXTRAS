First Dates: Funny or Awkward?

Nervous, excited, and hoping nothing goes wrong. First dates are always a good story — even when they go badly.

🪝
Phase 1
The Trigger
⏱ 4 min
"In a survey of 5,000 people, 72% said their first date was awkward. The most common problems: long silences, spilling food, talking too much about an ex, and arriving very late."
— Match.com Dating Survey
Teacher script: "72% of people say their first date was awkward. So — was your first date normal or a little bit crazy?" [Pause and smile.] "Today we talk about first dates — the good, the bad, and the very embarrassing."

Phase 4
The Conversation Engine
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 — Personal Experience (Past Simple)
Q1
What was your best first date like? Where did you go and what did you do?
Past simple storytelling. "We went to... We talked about... We laughed a lot." Keep the tone warm and fun. If they are shy, offer options: "Was it dinner? Coffee? A movie?" Scaffolding helps A2.2 students get started.
Q2
Did something embarrassing or funny happen on a date? What was it?
Best question for stories. Spilling food, awkward silence, saying the wrong name, forgetting their wallet — all common. Model: "One time I spilled coffee on my white shirt. I was so embarrassed." Past simple + emotions.
Q3
Did you ever go on a date and want to go home after 10 minutes? What happened?
The "bad date" angle. Very relatable. Model: "Yes! The person was very boring. They only talked about their job. I ate my food quickly and said I was tired." Past simple + sequence of events. Light and funny tone.
Block 2 — Opinion
Q4
What do you think is the best place for a first date — a restaurant, coffee, or something different?
Simple opinion with "I think... is better because..." Rodrigo's coffee argument is useful here. Prompts practical reasoning: cost, duration, exit strategy. Very accessible for A2.2 — just comparing two options.
Q5
Do you think the man should always pay on a first date — or should both people pay?
Classic debate, simple language. "I think the man should pay because..." / "I think both people should pay because..." Generates strong, quick opinions. Can/should modal is natural here. Very energetic discussion in Mexican professional groups.
Q6
Is it okay to look at your phone on a first date?
Simple yes/no with explanation. "I think it is okay if..." / "I think it is never okay because..." Almost everyone says no — but most have done it. Ask: "Did you ever look at your phone on a date?" Honest laughter usually follows.
Block 3 — Future Plans (Going To / Will)
Q7
Are you going to go on a date soon? Where do you want to go?
Going to for personal plans. Very open — married or partnered students can answer about a date night with their partner. "I'm going to go to dinner with my partner next week." Completely inclusive. Fun and future-focused.
Q8
If a first date goes very well, will you call the person the next day — or will you wait?
Will for future decision. "I will call the next day because..." / "I will wait 2 days because..." The "rules" of dating — how soon is too soon? Good for opinions + will. Light, fun topic with no wrong answer.
Block 4 — Past Habits & Reflection (Used To)
Q9
Did you use to feel more nervous on first dates when you were younger?
Used to for past habits. "I used to feel very nervous. Now I am more relaxed." Contrast past and present confidence. Good for "used to + verb" structure. Most adults feel less nervous now — a sign of growth they can describe simply.
Q10
Did you use to think first dates were very important — or not important at all?
Used to + past belief. "I used to think first dates were very important. Now I think they are just the beginning." Or "I used to not care about first dates. Now I think they tell you a lot." Elena's opinion connects naturally here.
