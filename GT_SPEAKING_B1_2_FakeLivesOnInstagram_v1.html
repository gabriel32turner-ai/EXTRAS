Fake Lives
on Instagram

The perfect meal. The perfect trip. The perfect body. The perfect life. Everyone is performing — including you.

🪝
The Trigger
Phase 1 · Provocative Headline
⏱ 4 min
The Wall Street Journal · Facebook Internal Research Leak · 2021
"Facebook's own research showed that Instagram makes body image worse for 1 in 3 teenage girls. Internal documents said: 'We make body image issues worse for one in three teen girls.' The company knew. They continued anyway."
Teacher Script: Read the quote slowly. Then: "The company knew it was hurting people — and kept going. Does that surprise you? Or does it sound exactly like what you expected?" Let students react. Then shift closer to home: "Have you ever felt worse about your life after spending time on Instagram? Be honest." The goal is immediate personal connection, not abstract debate.

Push: "Is Sebastián right — does complaining about Instagram while still using it mean you secretly like it?" Then use Patricia to bring the stakes up: "Is it still just a personal choice when it's affecting 14-year-olds?"

The Conversation Engine
Phase 4 · 10 Questions · 4 Blocks
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 · Personal Experience
Q1
Have you ever seen someone's Instagram and felt that your life was less interesting, less beautiful, or less successful than theirs?
Most students will say yes. The important follow-up: "And did you know — even while feeling it — that their profile was probably not their real life?" The gap between knowing and feeling is the interesting territory. Target: present perfect + past simple for the specific memory.
Q2
Have you ever posted a photo that showed a better version of a moment than it really was — a nicer meal, a happier face, a more exciting trip?
The honest self-implication question. Most students have done this. Push gently: "Why did you choose that photo instead of a real one?" Target: present perfect + honest reflection. Key vocab: filter, edit, choose, select, impression. No judgment — just curiosity.
Q3
Have you ever felt happier or more confident after posting a photo and getting a lot of likes? Be honest — why do you think that happens?
This is about dopamine and validation — in simple B1.2 terms. Most students will admit this. Push: "Is that a problem — or is it just how humans work?" Target: present perfect + present simple for the explanation. Key vocab: likes, validation, confidence, approval.
Block 2 · Opinion
Q4
Do you think most people know — deep down — that Instagram is not real? Or do some people actually believe it?
The interesting answer: adults know, but still feel the comparison. Children and teenagers often don't know. Push: "Is it worse to feel bad about something you know is fake — or is that even more worrying?" Target: present simple + modals for speculation (might, could, probably).
Q5
In your opinion, is it dishonest to show only the best parts of your life on social media — or is that just normal human behavior?
Good philosophical question for B1.2. Push: "Do you show strangers the worst version of yourself? Then why should Instagram be different?" Target: present simple for opinion + modals (should, have to). Model: "I think it's normal to show your best — but there's a difference between choosing a nice photo and pretending your life is perfect."
Q6
Should Instagram be required to show when a photo has been filtered or edited — like a warning label? Would that actually change anything?
Some countries (Norway, UK) have already passed laws requiring influencers to disclose edited photos. Push: "Would a small label change how you feel when you see a perfect body?" Target: should + passive voice (photos should be labeled). Key: even if students say "yes," push: "Would you trust the label?"
Block 3 · Hypothetical
Q7
What would you do if you found out that a close friend's "perfect life" on Instagram was completely fake — financial problems, relationship problems, unhappy at work?
Target: second conditional. Push: "Would you say something — or pretend you didn't know?" Model: "I would probably say something privately. Something like: 'Hey, I know things aren't easy right now. You don't have to pretend with me.'" Role-play this sentence. Key vocab: pretend, fake, honest, admit.
Q8
What would you do if your teenage child came to you crying because they felt ugly compared to influencers they follow? What would you actually say?
Target: second conditional + direct language production. Push: "Say it out loud — in English — what are the first 2 sentences you would say?" This is the kinesthetic moment. Key: the student has to produce real, warm, human language. Not a speech — a real response to a real child. Patricia's bubble gives them a model to work with.
Block 4 · Provocative
Q9
Is social media making people less happy — or were people always unhappy, and social media just makes it more visible?
Big question, accessible for B1.2. Push both directions: "Were people happier before Instagram — or did they just compare themselves to their neighbors instead of the whole world?" Target: past simple + present simple. Key vocab: compare, happiness, before/after, visible. This question often produces the most interesting personal reflection of the session.
Q10
Uncomfortable question: what part of your life do you NEVER post about on social media — and why? What are you protecting people from seeing?
The self-reflection closer. Every student has an answer. The question reveals what they're protecting: finances, relationship problems, insecurities, failures at work. Push: "Is that dishonest — or is it just private?" Let the student sit with the distinction. The goal is honest English, not a confession. Target: present simple + honest vocabulary.

The Exit Shot
Phase 5 · One Final Dilemma
⏱ 5 min
Final Question
You follow someone on Instagram who posts about their perfect relationship, perfect home, and perfect body. You've always felt a little jealous. Then you find out — through a mutual friend — that they lost their job 6 months ago, have serious debt, and are in therapy for depression. Their last post was yesterday: a smiling selfie with the caption "Grateful for this beautiful life." Do you unfollow them — or do you feel something completely different now?
Homework Loop: "Record a 60-second WhatsApp voice message. Tell me how you feel now — and what you're going to do the next time you feel jealous of someone on social media. In English. No script."
