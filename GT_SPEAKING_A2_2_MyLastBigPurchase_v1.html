My Last Big Purchase
What was the last big thing you bought? Was it a good idea — or do you regret it a little?
🪝
The Trigger
Phase 1 · Opening Question
⏱ 4 min
Simple question to start
"Think about the last big thing you bought — something expensive or important. Was it for you, or for someone else? Are you happy with it?"
Teacher script: Read the question out loud. Then say: "I'll go first — I bought [something simple]. It was good / not so good because..." Then ask the student: "What about you? What was the last big thing you bought?" Keep it light and simple. This is just a warm-up.

The Conversation Engine
Phase 4 · 10 Questions · 4 Blocks
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 · Past Experience
Q1
What was your last big purchase? How much did it cost, and why did you buy it?
Teacher Tip Let the student describe freely. Help with: "When did you buy it? Where — a store or online? Did you plan it or was it fast?" Accept any answer, even "I don't remember."
Q2
Did you feel happy after you bought it — or did you feel a little bad about the money?
Model Answer "I felt happy at first. But then I looked at my bank account and I felt... not so happy. But I still use it, so it's OK."
Q3
Did you talk to someone before you bought it — or did you just buy it alone?
Teacher Tip Simple past practice. Push: "Did your partner / friend / family know? Did they say anything?" Great for personal stories with very accessible grammar.
Block 2 · Opinion
Q4
Is it OK to buy something expensive when you want it — even if you don't need it?
Teacher Tip Simple yes/no with explanation. "Do you think 'want' is enough reason? Or do you need a reason?" Keep it light — this is not a debate, just an opinion exchange.
Q5
Do you like to save money before you buy something big — or do you prefer to buy now and pay later?
Model Answer "I like to save first. I don't like debt. But sometimes I see something and I want it NOW. So I use my credit card. Then I regret it a little."
Q6
What do you think is a "big" purchase? How much money is a lot for you?
Teacher Tip Very personal and easy to answer. Students often have different numbers. "Is $500 pesos big? $2,000? $10,000?" Encourage them to give their real number without judgment.
Block 3 · Future Plans
Q7
Are you going to buy something big in the next 6 months? What is it?
Model Answer "Yes, I'm going to buy a new bed. My old one is terrible. I'm going to save $3,000 pesos and then buy it. Maybe next month." Simple, clear, and uses "going to" naturally.
Q8
If you had $10,000 pesos right now, what will you buy first?
Teacher Tip Simple "will" practice. "I will buy..." No complex conditional needed at this level. Let them dream a little — it generates vocabulary naturally.
Block 4 · Past Habits & Reflection
Q9
Did you use to buy more things when you were younger — or less? What was different?
Teacher Tip "Used to" practice at A2.2 level. Help with: "When you were 20 / 25 / before you had kids / when you lived alone — what did you use to buy?" Simple and personal.
Q10
What did you use to think about money when you were young? Was your family careful with money or not?
Model Answer "When I was a kid, my family didn't have a lot of money. My mom used to say 'we don't need that.' I didn't like it then. Now I understand. She was right."

The Exit Shot
Phase 5 · Final Question · Personal & Simple
⏱ 5 min
💣 Exit Shot
You have $5,000 pesos and you can only choose one: a new phone, a weekend trip with your family, or new clothes for work. What are you going to choose — and why?
