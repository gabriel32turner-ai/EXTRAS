§ 01 · The Trigger
🪝 The Money That Never Comes Back
Read aloud · Immediate reaction · No debate yet · 4 min
01
4 min
💸
Creditkarma / Forbes Money Survey 2023
1 in 3
People say they lost a friendship because of money — specifically because one person borrowed money and never paid it back. The average amount: around $3,000–5,000 pesos.
Source: CreditKarma Friendship & Money Survey 2023 · Forbes Personal Finance
🪝 Opening question — Point at one student and ask:
"Right now — do you have someone who owes you money? Yes or no?"
Teacher Script — 4 min Binary question — yes or no first. Then: "How much? And do you think they are going to pay you back?" 2–3 students. Don't go deep yet — just activate the memory. Say: "We talk about the full story in §4."
Pedagogical Almost every adult in the group will raise their hand or nod. This is one of the most universally relatable frustrations in adult Mexican social life. The "1 in 3 lost a friendship" stat is the emotional activator — don't rush past it. Let it land. Retention "This is our last lab in this work/money series. But honestly — this topic could last 3 hours. There is always more to say about people who don't pay back."

§ 03 · Opinion Wall
🧱 4 People. 4 Views on Lending Money.

03
10 min
🔁 Processing Question — Ask every student
"Who do you agree with most? Use: I agree with [name] because..."
Provocateur: Rodrigo Peña Rodrigo's "I never lend money — I just give it" position is the most provocative because it neutralizes the whole problem by reframing it. Students who are angry about unpaid loans will find this either liberating or infuriating. Both reactions produce language. Push: "Is Rodrigo right? Or is he just rich?" Creates immediate energy.

§ 05 · Exit Shot
💣 One Scenario. Your Real Answer.
5 min · No chips · Real answer only · Then the homework loop
05

EXIT
💣 Exit Shot
A good friend calls you. He needs 2,000 pesos — urgently. You have the money. But he still owes you 500 pesos from last year. He didn't mention it. What are you going to do?
Answer in 2–3 sentences. Start with yes or no — do you lend the money? Then tell me the real reason — not the polite answer.
📌 A2.1 Model Answer — Show AFTER student speaks
"I am going to lend him the money because he is my good friend and it is an emergency. But I am going to say: 'I can help you, but you still owe me 500 pesos from last year. Can we talk about that too?' I don't want to lose the friendship, but I also don't want to lose 2,500 pesos."
