Is Money the Most

Everyone says "no." But everyone works very hard for it. Let's talk honestly.

🪝 Phase 1 — The Trigger
The Question
Read it aloud. Then ask the student directly.
4 min
01
A simple survey question
"In a global survey, 72% of people said money is NOT the most important thing in life. But 89% of those same people said they want to earn more money."
— We say one thing. We do another. Today we talk about that.
Quick question to start
"Be honest — if someone offered you 3 times your salary to do a job you hate, do you take it?"
— No wrong answer. Just the honest one.
Launch line: "Quick question — yes or no: is money the most important thing in your life?" [Let them answer.] "Okay. Now tell me — why do you go to work every day?" [Pause.] "Today we talk about money. Not saving strategies or investments — just the honest conversation about what money means to us." This topic always generates immediate, strong opinions at A2.1.

🗣 Phase 4 — The Conversation Engine
10 Questions · 4 Blocks
Short answers are fine. Real stories are better. Show tips after the student answers.
23 min
04
Block 1 — Personal Experience (Past Simple)
Q1
When was the last time you didn't have enough money for something you needed? What happened?
Past simple — a real money moment. Model: "Last month I needed to fix my car. I didn't have enough money. I borrowed from my brother. It was uncomfortable." Short sentences, real vocabulary. Activate: afford, borrow, stress.
Q2
Did you ever lend money to a friend or family member? Did they pay you back?
Past simple — very personal story. Most people have one. Model: "Yes. My friend asked for 2,000 pesos. I lent it. He didn't pay me back. I was angry. But I didn't say anything because he's my friend." Activate: lend, owe, angry. This question generates strong reactions.
Q3
What was the best thing you bought with your own money? Why was it special?
Past simple — positive money memory. Contrasts with Q1 and Q2. Model: "I bought my first car. I saved for two years. When I drove it for the first time, I felt very proud. It was my money. My car." Activate: save, earn, buy.
Block 2 — Opinion (Simple Present)
Q4
Do you think money buys happiness? Why or why not?
Classic opinion question, perfectly A2.1. Model: "I think money buys some happiness. If you have no money, you have stress. But very rich people have problems too. So — a little yes, a little no." Simple present + because = target structure.
Q5
Is it okay to talk about money with friends and family? Or is it private?
Cultural opinion — very relevant in Mexico. Model: "In my family, money is private. We don't talk about salaries. But with friends? Sometimes yes. It depends on the friend." Activate: private, choice. Good for "I think it's okay because..." structures.
Q6
What is more important — a job with a lot of money or a job you love?
Classic comparison opinion at A2.1. The Trigger question comes back here. Model: "I think both! But if I need to choose — I want money first. Then I can look for a job I love later. Right now I need to pay my bills." Practical and honest.
Block 3 — Future Plans (Going to / Will)
Q7
Are you going to save more money this year? What are you going to do differently?
Going to — personal plan. Model: "I'm going to try to save 500 pesos every month. I'm going to stop buying things I don't need. I'm going to cook at home more." Three sentences, three "going to" — excellent A2.1 production. Celebrate it.
Q8
If you get a raise next year, what will you do with the extra money?
Will — imagined future action. Model: "I will save half and spend half. I will pay my debt first. Then I will take a vacation with my family. My family is the reason I work." Short and personal. Activate: save, pay, debt, dream.
Block 4 — Provocative
Q9
Be honest — do you spend money on things you don't really need? What do you buy?
Gentle self-reflection — everyone spends on something unnecessary. Model: "Yes. Coffee every day. Clothes I don't wear. Food I don't eat. It's a problem. But I like it in the moment." Honest and funny. Activate: waste, afford, spend.
Q10
Do you think your relationship with money is good? Or do you want to change something?
Simple self-reflection close. Model: "My relationship with money is... complicated. I earn okay. But I spend too much. I don't save enough. I want to change that. But it's not easy." Three simple sentences. Very A2.1 appropriate as a closing question.
A2.1 grammar check: Past simple (Q1-3), simple present (Q4-6), going to / will (Q7-8), simple present provocative (Q9-10). Correct tense errors gently — accept any honest answer. The vocabulary activation is the main goal.

Cultural note: Money is a sensitive topic in Mexico. Students may be reluctant to share real numbers. Redirect: "You don't need to say the amount — just tell me the feeling." This usually unlocks the story.
💣 Phase 5 — The Exit Shot
The Final Question
One scenario. Real situation. What are you going to do?
5 min
05
💣 Exit Shot
A company offers you a new job. The salary is 40% more than now. But the job is boring, far from home, and you don't know anyone there. Your current job is okay — not perfect, but you like your coworkers. Are you going to accept the new job? What are you going to tell your current boss?
🎙 WhatsApp Homework: Record a 60-second voice message in English: "Tell me about your relationship with money. Is money very important to you? What do you spend too much on? And what are you going to change this year?" Send before our next session.
Why it works: The scenario is concrete, realistic, and emotionally loaded. 40% raise is enough to tempt — but the human costs are real. "Are you going to accept?" forces a decision. "What are you going to tell your boss?" forces a second decision. Two "going to" questions = double grammar practice in a natural context.

Upsell moment: "Next session we talk about morning people vs. night people — something completely different but very personal. I think you'll like it."
