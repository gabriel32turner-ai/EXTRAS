Old Movies vs. New Movies
Old movies had great stories. New movies have incredible special effects. But which ones do you actually remember? Which ones made you feel something?
🪝
Phase 1
The Trigger
One question. Think of a movie. Go.
⏱ 4 min
Rotten Tomatoes · Annual Report · 2023
"9 of the top 10 most-watched movies on Netflix last year were sequels, remakes, or superhero movies. Only 1 was an original story."
Today, Hollywood makes many sequels and remakes. Movies cost hundreds of millions of dollars and have incredible special effects. But many people say: "New movies are boring. Old movies were better." Other people say: "Old movies were slow and the quality was terrible." Who is right?
Teacher Script Ask directly: "Quick — what is your favorite movie of all time? Tell me the name and one sentence about it." Go around the room fast. Just names and one sentence. Then ask: "Is it an old movie or a new movie?" The answers will show you the room — some love classics, some love new films. That tension is your opening.

Phase 4
The Conversation Engine
10 questions. Your real opinions about real movies you watched.
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 · Personal Experience (Past Simple)
Q1
What was the last movie you watched? Was it good or bad? Why?
Teacher Tip Past simple description. "I watched ______ last weekend. It was good because the story was interesting." "It was bad because it was too long." Encourage 2–3 sentences minimum. Any movie is fine — Netflix, cinema, TV. The goal is past simple + adjective + because.
Q2
Did you ever cry at a movie? Which movie — and what happened in that scene?
Teacher Tip Past simple + description. "Yes, I cried at Coco / Titanic / The Lion King. The scene when ______ happened was very sad." If they say "No, I never cry at movies," ask: "Really? Not even Coco?" — in Mexico, Coco almost always gets someone.
Q3
Did you ever stop watching a movie because it was too boring or too bad? What movie was it?
Teacher Tip Past simple + reason. "Yes, I stopped watching ______ because it was very slow." "I stopped after 30 minutes because the story was terrible." This is a fun confession question — everyone has a movie they abandoned. Encourage the name and a specific reason.
Block 2 · Opinion (Simple Present)
Q4
Do you prefer old movies or new movies? Why?
Teacher Tip Simple present + preference. "I prefer old movies because the stories are better." "I prefer new movies because the special effects are amazing." "I like both." All three are valid. The target is "I prefer... because..." — a simple structure that produces clean output.
Q5
Is it a good idea to make sequels and remakes of old movies? Why or why not?
Teacher Tip Opinion with "I think it's a good idea" / "I don't think it's a good idea." "I think sequels are good when the story is interesting. But many sequels are bad because they only want money." Simple present opinion + because. Accept one-sentence answers.
Q6
What is more important in a movie — a great story or great special effects? Why?
Teacher Tip Comparative opinion. "I think a great story is more important because you remember it." "Special effects are more important because movies look boring without them." Target: "I think [X] is more important because..." — one clear choice, one reason.
Block 3 · Future Plans (Going to / Will)
Q7
Are you going to watch a movie this week? What movie — and where?
Teacher Tip Going to for concrete plans. "Yes, I'm going to watch ______ on Netflix on Saturday." "I'm going to go to the cinema with my family on Sunday." If they say "I don't know," ask: "Is there a movie you want to watch? Tell me the name." Simple and personal.
Q8
Will movies be better or worse in 10 years? What do you think?
Teacher Tip Will for prediction. "I think movies will be better because of AI and new technology." "I think they will be worse because they will only make sequels." One sentence with will + reason is perfect. Can also mention: "I think people will watch movies on their phone, not in the cinema."
Block 4 · Provocative
Q9
Be honest — do you check your phone during a movie at the cinema? How many times?
Teacher Tip Simple present + honesty. "Yes, I check my phone maybe 3 or 4 times." "No, I never check my phone — it bothers me when other people do it." "Yes, but only if the movie is boring." The number makes it funny and specific. No judgment.
Q10
A movie you love is going to have a sequel. The trailer looks bad. Are you going to watch it anyway?
Teacher Tip Going to scenario — specific and fun. "Yes, I'm going to watch it because I love the first movie." "No, I'm not going to watch it. I don't want to ruin the original." This is a real dilemma many movie fans have. The word "anyway" is great to introduce here.

Phase 5
The Exit Shot
One situation. What are you going to do?
⏱ 5 min
💣 Exit Shot · A2.1 Old Movies vs. New Movies
Your favorite movie from your childhood is going to come out as a new remake next month. They changed everything — the actors, the story, the ending. Are you going to watch it? And are you going to tell your family to watch it too?
Homework · WhatsApp Voice Note (1 min)
Record your answer in English. Tell me: are you going to watch it — and why? Name your favorite childhood movie too!
Retention Hook The "childhood favorite" detail creates emotional investment immediately. Every student has one. The remake question is a real situation that happens constantly — Disney remakes, Mexican telenovela remakes, etc. The "family" element adds one more layer of decision. End: "Send me the voice note. And tell me the movie name — I want to know which one."
