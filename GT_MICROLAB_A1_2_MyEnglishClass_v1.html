Phase 4
Question Cards
The teacher answers first. Then it's your turn.
12 MIN
Question 1
Why do you study English?
Teacher: "I study Portuguese because I like Brazilian culture. But most of my students study English for WORK. Their company needs English. They need English for meetings, emails, and calls with international teams."
Now you: "I study English because _______ . I need English for..."
Tip: This is THE retention question. Whatever they say — write it down. This is their "why." Come back to it in future sessions: "Remember — you said you need English for meetings. Today we practice meeting vocabulary."
Question 2
What is the hardest thing about English for you?
Teacher: "For me with Portuguese, the hardest thing is listening. People speak very fast! I can read Portuguese, I can write it, but I can't understand fast conversations. I think for my students, speaking is the hardest — they know the words but they can't say them fast."
Now you: "The hardest thing for me is _______ . I can _______ but I can't..."
Tip: Classic can/can't contrast. Push: "Can you READ English? Can you WRITE English? Can you UNDERSTAND songs? Can you SPEAK fast?" Mapping abilities builds awareness.
Question 3
Do you practice English outside of class? How?
Teacher: "I practice Portuguese with Netflix — I watch series in Portuguese with subtitles. I also listen to music in Portuguese. But I never read books in Portuguese — that's too difficult for me right now."
Now you: "I practice English with _______ . I sometimes / never _______ in English."
Tip: This reveals what they do and DON'T do. If they say "nothing outside class," don't judge — suggest one easy thing: "Can you change your phone to English? That's practice every day!" Practical micro-wins.
Question 4
Do you like your English class? What is the best part?
Teacher: "I hope my students like their class! I think the best part is when you can say something new — a new word, a new sentence — and the other person understands you. That moment is magic."
Now you: "I like my English class because _______ . The best part is when I..."
Tip: This is your testimonial moment. Whatever positive thing they say — remember it. You can quote it later: "You said the best part is speaking — and today you spoke for 35 minutes. That's progress."
Question 5
Can you understand English songs or movies without subtitles?
Teacher: "I can understand slow songs in Portuguese but I can't understand fast rap. For movies, I always need subtitles — actors speak too fast! I can understand maybe 40% without subtitles."
Now you: "I can understand _______ in English. I can't understand _______ because..."
Tip: Relatable and safe topic. Push for percentages: "What percentage do you understand? 20%? 50%? 80%?" Numbers make it concrete and trackable over time.
Question 6
Do you speak English at work? When?
Teacher: "My students sometimes need English for emails or calls with the US or Europe. Some of them speak English every day at work. Others never use it — but their company wants them to learn for the future."
Now you: "I speak / don't speak English at work. I need English for..."
Tip: Connects class to real work — the reason they're here. If they use English at work, ask: "In meetings? In emails? On the phone? With who?" If they don't: "Do your colleagues speak English? Does your boss?"
Question 7
What English word do you always forget?
Teacher: "In Portuguese, I always forget the word for 'ceiling'! I know it, I learn it, and then I forget it again. I think everybody has that one word that never stays in the brain."
Now you: "I always forget the word _______ . I also struggle with..."
Tip: Light and fun. Students usually laugh and share frustrating words. Use the moment to teach: "OK, let's fix that word RIGHT NOW. Say it 5 times." Instant micro-win.
Question 8
What is your English goal? What do you want to do in English?
Teacher: "My Portuguese goal is to have a full conversation with a Brazilian person. No Spanish, no English — only Portuguese. For 10 minutes. That's my goal. I think most of my students want to speak English in a meeting without being nervous."
Now you: "My English goal is to _______ . I want to _______ in English without..."
Tip: This is the closer — their WHY and their DESTINATION. Write it down. Repeat it back to them: "Your goal is to [X]. We work on that every class. You're closer than you think." Retention gold.
