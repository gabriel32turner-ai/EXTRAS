§ 01 · The Trigger
🪝 The Numbers Behind the Booking
Read aloud or project · First reaction only · No debate yet · 4 min max

Sources: Airbnb Annual Report 2023 · J.D. Power North America Hotel Guest Satisfaction Index · Consumer Reports Travel Survey
🪝 Opening Trigger — Read this, then point at someone
"A family books an Airbnb in Cancún. The photos show a beautiful apartment with a pool view. They arrive: the pool is under construction, the Wi-Fi password doesn't work, and the host doesn't answer messages. The hotel next door has rooms available — but costs 40% more. What do they do?"
Teacher Mechanics — 4 min Read the Cancún story slowly. Point at someone: "What do they do — stay or move?" Don't judge the answer. Wait 5 seconds if silence. After 2–3 reactions, say: "Hold that. Everything comes back in §4."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • The Cancún scenario is locally relatable — Mexican professionals travel domestically. If students have been to Cancún or similar destinations, the scenario is visceral, not abstract.
  • The $180 hidden-fee stat is the most surprising. Mark who reacts — they often lead Q4 and Q5.
  • If nobody reacts immediately: "Have you ever booked something online and it looked nothing like the photos? Raise your hand."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • This topic has universal personal experience — almost every adult has stayed in both. The debate is real, not theoretical. That's the activation.
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Retention hook: "In English, knowing how to complain, compare, and negotiate travel situations is a real professional skill — especially for business trips with international colleagues."

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • "Host" vs "guest" — clarify once: in Airbnb, the host owns the property; the guest stays in it. This role distinction matters for several §4 questions.
  • Best production challenge trigger: "Which 3 words describe your last travel experience — honestly?"
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "These words are what you need to write a review, complain at a front desk, or tell a colleague about a business trip — all in English."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Business travel vocabulary is one of the most requested topics by HR managers who hire bilingual professionals. You're building that vocabulary today."

"Who do you agree with most — and who do you think is wrong? Why?"
Don't vary the question
🔁 Processing Question — Pairs → Group
"Who do you agree with most — and who do you think is wrong? Why?"
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Read all 4 before opening. The contrast between Fernanda (Airbnb convert) and Roberto (hotel loyalist) drives the first wave of debate. Diego (Airbnb host) adds a dimension most students haven't considered.
  • The provocateur is Lic. Méndez. Her position — that Airbnb is destroying neighborhoods and should be regulated — is the most uncomfortable for students who love Airbnb. Push: "She lives in a building where 60% of apartments are now Airbnbs. Is she wrong to be angry?"
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • If anyone in the class has used Airbnb as a host — that's gold. Point them out: "You're Diego in this conversation. What do you think of Lic. Méndez's opinion?"
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Testimonial trigger: "One sentence — have you personally had an experience that proves one of these four people right?"
§ 04 · The Conversation Engine
🗣 10 Questions. 4 Blocks. 23 Minutes.
Personal → Opinion → Hypothetical → Provocative · No round-robin · Point and push
04
23 min
⚡
Rule: Minimum 2 sentences per answer. Position first. Reason or real example second. No yes/no answers.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Block 1 present perfect: watch for "I have stay" (missing -ed). Recycle gently: "So you have stayed in an Airbnb — tell me more."
  • Block 3 second conditional: watch for "If I would travel" — note and recycle at end only.
  • Q9 and Q10 require concrete position-taking — push students who answer vaguely: "Give me a number. Give me a specific situation."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • Celebrate vocab activation from §2: when "host," "amenities," or "check in" appear naturally — point it out immediately.
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • After Q10: "You can now have this conversation with an American colleague on a business trip. That is a real professional skill."
  • Upsell: "Next Speaking Lab: Gym Culture and Fake Transformations. Different topic — same depth."
§ 05 · The Exit Shot
💣 One Question. No Easy Answer.
5 min · No chips · Just position + reason · Then the loop
05
5 min
EXIT
💣 Exit Shot
Your company sends you on a 5-day business trip to Mexico City. They give you a budget of $1,200 USD for accommodation. A hotel costs $190/night — clean, reliable, breakfast included. An Airbnb costs $95/night — great reviews, but no front desk, no room service, and you've had a bad Airbnb experience before. Which do you book — and why?
Answer in 3 sentences. Be specific — mention at least one thing from today's vocabulary. What do you actually choose?
📌 B1.2 Model Answer — Reveal AFTER student speaks
"I would book the hotel. For a business trip, I need reliability — I can't risk arriving at an Airbnb and finding a problem the night before an important meeting. The extra cost is worth the peace of mind. I've stayed in both and the difference on a work trip is not about price — it's about having someone at the front desk when things go wrong."
🔁 Retention Loop — The WhatsApp Homework "Before our next session — record a 60-second voice note in English. Your answer to today's Exit Shot. Don't write it first. Don't rehearse. Just talk. Send it to [teacher number]."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • The corporate framing (company budget, business trip) makes the decision concrete and professionally relevant. It's not about personal preference — it's about professional judgment.
  • Follow-up only: "What would you choose if it were a personal vacation instead of a work trip?" This naturally surfaces the leisure vs business distinction and activates second conditional.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • The model answer uses "peace of mind" — a natural English collocation worth pointing out: "Peace of mind — remember that phrase. It comes up in professional English all the time."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Closing: "You just compared, evaluated, and decided — about a real professional situation — in English. That's exactly what B1.2 sounds like."
