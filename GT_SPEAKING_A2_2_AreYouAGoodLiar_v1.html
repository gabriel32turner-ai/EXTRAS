Are You a Good Liar?

Everyone lies a little. But are you good at it? And when is a small lie actually okay?

🪝
Phase 1
The Trigger
⏱ 4 min
"Researchers say that the average person tells 1 to 2 lies every day. Most of them are small — 'I'm fine', 'I'm on my way', 'I already ate'. Only a few are big lies."
— University of Massachusetts Study
Teacher script: "One to two lies every day. That is a lot! So — what did you lie about this week?" [Smile and pause.] "Don't worry. Small lies are normal. Today we talk about lying — when it is okay, when it is not, and if you are good at it."

Phase 4
The Conversation Engine
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 — Personal Experience (Past Simple)
Q1
When was the last time you said a small lie? What did you say?
Very recent, very personal. Students often say "I'm fine" when they are not, or "I'm on my way" when they are still at home. Keep it light and funny. Model: "Yesterday I said 'I'm five minutes away' but I was still in my house."
Q2
Did someone catch you in a lie before? What happened?
Past simple storytelling. "Yes, one time I... and then..." Encourage humor. If they say "no" — ask: "Really? Nobody ever caught you? Are you a very good liar?" This usually gets a laugh and a real answer.
Q3
Did you ever tell a lie to your boss or a teacher? What was it?
Classic: "I was sick" when they were not, or "I forgot" when they did not try. Universal experience. Tone: fun and non-judgmental. Model: "Yes, I said I was sick but I just did not want to go to work."
Block 2 — Opinion
Q4
Do you think small lies are okay? Why or why not?
Core opinion question at A2.2. "I think small lies are okay because..." / "I think all lies are bad because..." Simple present + "because." No need for complex argumentation — just a clear personal opinion.
Q5
Is it okay to lie to protect someone's feelings? For example — when a friend asks "Do I look good?"
The "kind lie" dilemma — at A2.2 level, very accessible. Model: "I think it is okay. If my friend looks bad, I don't say that. I say 'you look nice.'" Very relatable, generates strong personal opinions.
Q6
Do you think you are a good liar? What happens in your face or body when you lie?
Fun self-assessment. "I think I am a good / bad liar because..." Body language clues: "I laugh," "my face gets red," "I can not look at the person." Very animated answers typical here — students often act it out.
Block 3 — Future Plans (Going To / Will)
Q7
Are you going to try to lie less in the future — or is it too difficult?
Going to for personal plans. "I'm going to try to..." / "No, I'm not going to change because..." Keeps grammar clean and simple. Light tone — not a serious moral promise, just an honest reflection on habits.
Q8
If a friend asks "Do you like my new haircut?" and you don't like it — what will you say?
Will for decision in the moment. "I will say... because..." Concrete, funny, and personal. Many students will admit they will lie to protect feelings. Others will say they tell the truth always — good debate starter.
Block 4 — Past Habits & Reflection (Used To)
Q9
Did you use to lie more when you were a child? What did you lie about?
Used to + past habits. Children lie about homework, eating vegetables, breaking things. Very funny and nostalgic. Model: "I used to lie to my mom about my homework. I said 'I finished it' but I didn't do anything."
Q10
Did you use to think lying was always bad — or did you always think some lies are okay?
Simple self-reflection using used to. "I used to think... but now I think..." Traces personal growth on this topic. Keep it light. No need for philosophical depth at A2.2 — personal honesty is enough.
