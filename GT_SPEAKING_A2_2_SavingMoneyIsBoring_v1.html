Saving Money

Do you save money every month? Or do you spend it and think about saving... next month?
🪝
The Trigger
Phase 1 · Opening Question
⏱ 4 min
A simple true or false to start
"True or false: I save money every month." — How do you answer that question? And how do you feel about your answer?
Teacher script: Read it out loud. Then answer yourself: "For me — true / false! I save... / I don't save because..." Then ask the student: "What about you? True or false?" Keep it easy and no-judgment. This topic can feel personal — make it feel safe and fun.

The Conversation Engine
Phase 4 · 10 Questions · 4 Blocks
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 · Past Experience
Q1
Did you save money last month — or did you spend everything?
Teacher Tip Simple and direct. No judgment. If they say "I spent everything," say "Same here sometimes! What did you spend it on?" Make it feel normal and conversational.
Q2
Did you have a piggy bank or a savings box when you were a child? What did you save money for?
Model Answer "Yes! I had a piggy bank. A plastic one, pink. I saved coins for... candy. I was very serious about my candy budget. I was 7."
Q3
Did you ever save money for something specific and then feel really happy when you finally bought it?
Teacher Tip Great for personal memory storytelling. "What was it? How long did you save? Do you still have it?" Pure past simple practice in a fun, personal context.
Block 2 · Opinion
Q4
Do you think saving money is boring — or is it actually exciting when you see the number go up?
Teacher Tip Two clear positions — easy to discuss at A2.2. "Do you check your savings? Does it make you happy to see the number? Or do you prefer not to look?"
Q5
Is it better to save money in a bank — or keep cash at home? What do you think?
Model Answer "I keep cash at home. In a box. Under my bed. Very old-fashioned. But I like to see it. When it's in the bank, it doesn't feel real."
Q6
Do you like to save money for a specific goal — like a trip or a phone — or just save without a plan?
Teacher Tip Simple preference question — easy to answer at this level. "Do you need a goal to feel motivated? Or is saving for the future enough?" No wrong answer.
Block 3 · Future Plans
Q7
Are you going to save more money this year? What is your plan?
Model Answer "Yes, I'm going to save $500 pesos a week. I'm going to stop buying coffee every day. That's my plan. I made this same plan last year. We'll see."
Q8
If you save $10,000 pesos this year, what will you do with the money?
Teacher Tip Simple "will" for plans. Let them dream: "A trip? New furniture? Leave it in the bank? Give some to your family?" All options are valid and generate easy, natural language.
Block 4 · Past Habits & Reflection
Q9
Did you use to save more when you were younger — or less? What was different about your life then?
Teacher Tip "Used to" in a natural context. "When you lived with your parents / before you had kids / when you were a student — did you spend more or less? What changed?" Simple and personal.
Q10
What did you use to think about money when you were young? Did your family talk about money at home?
Model Answer "In my family, we didn't talk about money. It was a private topic. I think that was bad. I didn't learn anything about money until I was 25 and had problems."

The Exit Shot
Phase 5 · Final Question · Personal & Simple
⏱ 5 min
💣 Exit Shot
Starting tomorrow, you are going to save $200 pesos every week. No exceptions. At the end of the year, you will have $10,400 pesos. What are you going to do with that money — and do you think you can really do it?
📱 WhatsApp Homework Record 60 seconds in English: What will you do with the money — and are you going to start tomorrow? Be honest!
