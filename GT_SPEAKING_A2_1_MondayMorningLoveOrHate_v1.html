MONDAY MORNING:
LOVE OR HATE?
The alarm goes off. You open your eyes. It's Monday. What do you feel — really?

§ 01 · The Trigger
🪝 The Worst Morning of the Week
Read aloud · Immediate reaction · No debate yet · 4 min
01
4 min
😩
YouGov / LinkedIn Workplace Survey 2023
78%
Of workers say Monday morning is the most difficult moment of their work week. The average person feels tired, negative, and unmotivated on Monday before 10am.
Source: YouGov Workplace Wellbeing Survey 2023 · LinkedIn Work Trends Report
🪝 Opening question — Point at one student and ask:
"It's 7am on Monday. Your alarm goes off. What is the first thing you think?"
Teacher Script — 4 min Read the stat. Point at one student: "7am Monday — what do you think?" One sentence is enough. 2–3 students. Don't debate yet — just collect first reactions. Say: "We talk about all of this in §4."
Pedagogical 78% is universally relatable — almost everyone in the room will nod. Don't explain the stat — let the number hit first. If a student says "I like Monday," celebrate it: "Interesting! Tell me why — we'll need your opinion in §3." That student will be the natural counterpoint to the group. Retention After §1: "Next session we go deeper — rules at work you don't like. Today is the warm-up: how do you feel about work in general?"

§ 03 · Opinion Wall
🧱 4 Voices. 4 Monday Mornings.
Read all 4 · Pairs 90 sec · "I agree with [name] because..." · Group share
03
10 min
🔁 Processing Question — Ask every student
"Who do you agree with most? Use: I agree with [name] because..."
Provocateur: Elena Ruiz Elena's "I love Monday — it's a fresh start" position is the gentle provocateur. Most students will instinctively disagree or feel it's impossible. Push: "Is Elena lying? Or is she different from us?" Don't let it die at "I agree with Sofía." Push: "Why? Give me one specific reason." Even a short sentence counts.

§ 05 · Exit Shot
💣 One Scenario. Your Real Answer.
5 min · No chips · Real answer only · Then the homework loop
05
5 min
EXIT
💣 Exit Shot
It's Sunday at 9pm. You think about tomorrow — Monday at work. You feel bad. What are you going to do? Are you going to change something — or is everything going to be the same?
Answer in 2–3 sentences. Use "I am going to..." Be honest — not brave, not polite. What do you really plan to do?
📌 A2.1 Model Answer — Show AFTER student speaks
"I am going to go to bed early. I am not going to look at my phone. But I know the real problem is my job. I am going to start looking for a new one this week."
