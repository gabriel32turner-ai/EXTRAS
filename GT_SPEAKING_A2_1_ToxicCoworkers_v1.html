Toxic Coworkers

The people who destroy your office — and what to do about them.

🪝
Phase 1 · The Trigger

Read this and react

4 min
"A toxic coworker can destroy a team in 3 weeks. A good boss does nothing about it."
— Harvard Business Review
Teacher script: "Look at this headline. Read it. What do you think? Is it true? Do you know a toxic coworker? I know one. Today we are going to talk about these people — the ones who destroy the office." Student speaks 1-2 sentences. Don't correct grammar here. Activate emotion and memory.
💉
Phase 2 · Vocab Injection

Install the words

8 min
How to run it: Click each card to hear pronunciation. Student repeats. Give 1 quick office example per word. Don't translate — show gesture or context.

Phase 4 · The Conversation Engine

Now you talk

23 min
Golden rule: Student speaks 80% of the time. Correct with RECAST only — repeat the correct form without interrupting. Example: student says "She no listen to me" → you say "Oh really? She doesn't listen to you? Why?" Student hears the correct form without feeling cut off.
Block 1 · Personal experience
1Do you have a toxic coworker right now? What does he or she do?
Teacher tip: Let student describe freely. Listen for vocab from Phase 2. If they stop, prompt with: "What does he do when..." — give them a scaffold, not the answer.
2Think about your worst coworker ever. What happened? How did you feel?
Model answer (A2.1): "My worst coworker was Juan. He lied all the time. He blamed me for his mistakes. I felt angry and tired. I didn't trust him."
3Did you ever argue with a coworker? What was the problem?
Teacher tip: Past simple focus. Correct "argue → argued" if needed via recast. Push for details: "Why? What happened next?"
Block 2 · Opinion
4What is the #1 thing a toxic coworker does? Lies, gossip, drama, or laziness?
Model answer: "For me, the #1 problem is lies. A person who lies destroys the team. Gossip is also bad, but lies are worse."
5Who has more toxic people — men or women? Why do you think so?
Teacher tip: Spicy question. Let the student take a position — don't judge. Push back gently: "Really? Interesting. Why?" Opinion-building is the goal, not consensus.
6Is it the boss's job to fix toxic coworkers? Or is it your job?
Model answer: "I think it's the boss's job. The boss needs to see the problem and talk to the person. But sometimes the boss doesn't care. Then it's my job to protect myself."
Block 3 · Future & plans
7Are you going to say something to your toxic coworker next week? Why or why not?
Grammar focus: "going to" for plans. Model: "Yes, I'm going to talk to her on Monday. I'm tired of the problem." OR "No, I'm not going to say anything. It's not my job."
8Will you change jobs because of a toxic coworker? Or will you stay?
Grammar focus: "will" for decisions. Model: "I will stay because I like my job. But if the problem is very big, I will leave."
Block 4 · Provocative
9Be honest — are YOU a toxic coworker sometimes? When? Why?
Teacher tip: THE key question. Vulnerability moment. If the student laughs and says "no, never", push gently: "Really? Never? Even when you are tired or stressed?" This builds trust with the teacher. Magic happens here.
10Your boss protects a toxic coworker. What are you going to do?
Model answer: "If my boss protects him, I will talk to HR. If HR does nothing, I'm going to look for a new job. I don't work in a toxic place."
💣
Phase 5 · The Exit Shot

Take this home

5 min
Think about this tonight
"You work with a toxic coworker every day. He is your boss's best friend. Your boss will never fire him. Are you going to stay in this job, or are you going to leave? Why?"
📱 Homework: Send me a 1-minute voice message on WhatsApp with your answer. In English.
Retention loop: The Exit Shot becomes WhatsApp audio homework. Student processes the question for 24-48 hours. Next session opens with their recorded answer as warm-up. Zero friction, maximum continuity between sessions.
