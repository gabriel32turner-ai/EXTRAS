Every day has a moment you love and a moment you don't. What are yours?

🪝 Phase 1 — The Trigger
The Question
Read it. Then ask the student directly.
4 min
01
Two questions to start
"What was the best moment of your day yesterday? And what was the worst?"
— Two simple questions. Two honest answers. That's how we start today.
A simple fact about daily life
"Most people can name their worst moment of the day in 3 seconds. But the best moment takes longer — because we forget to notice it."
— Today we notice both.
Launch line: "Let's start simple. Yesterday — what was the best part? Just one thing." [Student answers.] "And the worst part?" [Student answers.] "Perfect. Today we talk about these moments — the small things that make our day good or bad." Keep it casual and warm. At A2.2, the goal is to get the student talking in past simple with confidence.

🗣 Phase 4 — The Conversation Engine
10 Questions · 4 Blocks
Short questions. Short answers are fine. Talk about your real day.
23 min
04
Block 1 — Personal Experience (Past Simple)
Q1
What was the best part of yesterday? Describe it in 2-3 sentences.
Past simple — describing a recent positive moment. Model: "Yesterday, the best part was lunch. I ate tacos with my colleague. We talked about the weekend. It was nice." Help student use past simple verbs: was, ate, talked, felt. Two to three sentences is plenty.
Q2
What was the worst part of yesterday? What happened?
Past simple — negative experience. Students often have a clear answer immediately. Model: "The worst part was the traffic. I waited 40 minutes to go home. I was very frustrated." Activate "That's the worst!" and "Tell me about it!" as reactions naturally.
Q3
When was the last time you had a really good day? What made it good?
Past simple — a more general positive memory. Model: "Last week I had a really good day. I finished an important project. My boss said 'good job.' I felt great. I went home early." Simple sentences, natural past simple flow. Celebrate good answers with "Lucky you!" reaction.
Block 2 — Opinion (Simple Present)
Q4
What do you think is the worst part of a typical work day? For most people.
Simple present opinion — about daily life in general. Most common answers: traffic, Monday mornings, difficult colleagues, boring meetings. Model: "I think the worst part is traffic. Most people wait a long time every day. It's very frustrating." Short and direct.
Q5
Do you think the morning or the evening is better? Why?
Simple binary opinion — easy to answer. Model: "I think evening is better. In the morning I am tired and I don't like to wake up early. In the evening I feel free — work is finished." The student can agree or disagree. Both answers require the same grammar: "I think... because..."
Q6
Is it important to have at least one good moment in every day? Or is it okay to have a bad day sometimes?
Simple opinion — two clear options. Model: "I think it's important to have one good moment. Even a small one — good coffee, a nice conversation. It helps. But some days are just bad. That's normal too." Students at A2.2 enjoy this type of reflective but simple question.
Block 3 — Future Plans (Going to / Will)
Q7
What are you going to do tonight to make it a good end to your day?
Going to — concrete future plan for tonight. Model: "I'm going to eat dinner with my family. Then I'm going to watch one episode of my series. Then sleep. That's my plan." Simple and personal. Help the student with the structure: "I'm going to + verb."
Q8
Will you change something about your day to have more good moments? What will you do differently?
Will — future intention or prediction. Model: "Yes — I will try to take a real break at lunch. Not eat at my desk. I will go outside for 15 minutes. I think that will help." Or: "No, I don't think I will change anything. I like my day." Both answers are valid.
Block 4 — Past Habits + Reflection (Used to)
Q9
Did you use to have a different "best part of the day" when you were younger? What was it?
Used to — nostalgic comparison. Model: "When I was a student, the best part of the day was after school. I used to play with my friends outside. Now I don't have that. Now my best part is dinner." Gentle and warm — students enjoy comparing past and present at this level.
Q10
Did you use to complain more about your day before — or are you different now?
Used to + self-reflection. Simple and personal. Model: "I used to complain a lot. I was younger and everything was a problem. Now I'm calmer. I don't complain so much. I notice good things more." Or the opposite — both are interesting. Light closing question.
A2.2 grammar targets: Past simple (Q1-3), simple present opinions (Q4-6), going to / will (Q7-8), used to (Q9-10).

Pacing note: This topic flows naturally because every student has a "best" and "worst" moment from their real day. Don't prepare — just ask about real yesterday. The content writes itself. Keep answers short and celebrate every complete sentence with positive feedback.
💣 Phase 5 — The Exit Shot
The Final Question
One question. Simple and personal. Answer in 2-3 sentences.
5 min
05
💣 Exit Shot
Today, right now — what is the best moment you are going to have when this class is finished? What are you looking forward to?
🎙 WhatsApp Homework: Record a 60-second voice message in English: "Tell me about yesterday. What was the best part? What was the worst part? And what did you do to feel better after the worst part?" Send before our next session.
Why it works: The Exit Shot at A2.2 connects directly to right now — not abstract. "Going to" is the target grammar, naturally embedded. "Looking forward to" introduces a useful phrase without forcing it. Students leave class thinking about something positive, which is a retention moment.

Homework note: Three guided questions structure exactly one minute of speech. Past simple throughout. Very doable for A2.2. The "what did you do to feel better?" question generates a problem-solving narrative that is more complex than it seems.

Upsell moment: "Next session we talk about pets and children — a very personal topic, a lot of fun. Very different energy from today."
