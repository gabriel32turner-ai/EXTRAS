The Trigger
Phase 1 · Provocation & Opening
⏱ 4 min
Forbes · 2024
"Online shopping app usage in Latin America grew 78% after the pandemic. Psychologists say shopping has replaced social connection for millions of adults — and most of them don't realize it."
Teacher script: Read it. Then ask: "When was the last time you bought something online that you didn't really need? What was it?" Let them answer fast. Then: "Did you feel good after — or a little bad? Or both at the same time?" That last question opens everything.

The Conversation Engine
Phase 4 · 10 Questions · 4 Blocks
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 · Personal Experience
Q1
Have you ever bought something online that was still in the bag or box weeks later — never used?
Teacher Tip Universal experience. Push for the specific item: "What was it? Where is it now? Did you throw it away, give it to someone, or is it still somewhere in your house right now?"
Q2
Have you ever bought something because you were sad, bored, or stressed — not because you actually needed it?
Model Answer "Yes — many times. When I had a bad week at work I would open Shein or Amazon at night and buy things. It felt good for about 20 minutes. Then I felt the same. Plus poorer."
Q3
Did you ever hide a purchase from your partner or family? Why?
Teacher Tip The confession question — delivered with lightness. "Where did you hide it? Did they find out? What happened?" This generates great storytelling at B1.1 level.
Block 2 · Opinion
Q4
Is shopping addiction a real addiction — like alcohol or cigarettes? Or is it just a bad habit?
Teacher Tip Good debate. "What's the difference between a habit and an addiction? Do you need to lose control to call it an addiction? Have you ever tried to stop buying things and found it difficult?"
Q5
Do you think apps like Shein, Amazon, and TikTok Shop are responsible for people spending too much money?
Model Answer "I think they make it very easy to spend money without thinking. The design is not an accident — it's a trap. But we also have a choice. The question is: how easy should companies be allowed to make it?"
Q6
Is it OK to spend money on things that make you happy — even if other people think you don't need them?
Teacher Tip No judgment zone question. Push: "Where is the line between enjoying your money and using shopping to avoid something? Is there a difference?"
Block 3 · Hypothetical
Q7
What would you do if your partner spent $5,000 pesos a month on things they don't need — and you were trying to save money together?
Model Answer "I would talk about it — but carefully. I wouldn't say 'you spend too much.' I would say 'I want us to save for something together — can we make a plan?' Attack the problem, not the person."
Q8
What would you do if you had no credit card and no online shopping apps for 30 days? Do you think you could do it?
Teacher Tip Fun and honest. "What would be the hardest part? What would you miss most? What would you do with that time and money instead?" This usually generates surprising self-awareness.
Block 4 · Provocative
Q9
When you open a shopping app, are you looking for something you need — or are you looking for a feeling?
Teacher Tip The most honest question of the session. Give real space. Most people, if they're honest, are looking for a feeling — excitement, comfort, control. "What feeling is it, specifically? And does the purchase actually give it to you?"
Q10
Is there something in your house right now that you bought and wish you hadn't? What is it — and what would you do with that money today?
Model Answer "An exercise bike. $4,500 pesos. I used it four times. Now it has clothes on it. I would give that money to my savings — or honestly, take a trip with my family. The bike gave me guilt. A trip would give me memories."

The Exit Shot
Phase 5 · Final Dilemma · No right answer
⏱ 5 min
💣 Exit Shot
Your doctor tells you that your stress is very high and you need to find a way to relax. Your way of relaxing is shopping — a new outfit, a new gadget, something small that makes you feel good. Your partner says it's a problem and wants you to stop. Your doctor says the stress is more dangerous than the spending. What do you do?
📱 WhatsApp Homework Record 60 seconds in English: Do you keep shopping to manage your stress — or do you find another way? And what would that other way be?
