Dogs or Cats?

Describe your pet or your dream pet. Say what animals are like. Only "to be" and adjectives.

Show a photo of your pet — or your favorite animal — and describe it

Phase 4
Question Cards
The teacher answers first. Then it's your turn.
12 MIN
Question 1
Is there a pet in your house right now? What is its name?
Teacher: "Yes! My dog Pancho is at home right now. He is small and brown. He is a mix — not a special breed. He is 4 years old and he is very loud!"
Now you: "Yes, my _______ is... / No, but I like..."
Tip: If student has no pet, redirect: "OK, no pet now — but what is your DREAM pet? A dog? A cat? Something exotic?" Both paths work.
Question 2
What is the personality of dogs? And cats?
Teacher: "Dogs are loyal, friendly, and loud. They are always happy. Cats are independent, quiet, and mysterious. They are elegant but cold. For me, dogs are more fun."
Now you: "Dogs are _______ . Cats are _______ . For me, _______ are more _______ ."
Tip: This is adjective gold — push for 3+ adjectives per animal. If student says "dogs are nice," push: "Nice AND? Loyal? Friendly? Loud? What else?" Stack the adjectives.
Question 3
Is a pet expensive? What is the most expensive thing?
Teacher: "Yes, a pet is expensive! The vet is very expensive. Food is expensive too. But for me, it is not a problem — Pancho is important for my family."
Now you: "A pet is _______ . The most expensive thing is..."
Tip: Money talk engages professionals. Ask: "Is a dog more expensive than a cat? Why?" Forces comparison with "to be" — natural and productive.
Question 4
What is the best thing about having a pet?
Teacher: "The best thing is the company. When I am alone at home, Pancho is there. He is always happy to see me. That is beautiful — no person is ALWAYS happy to see you, but a dog is!"
Now you: "The best thing is _______ . My pet is _______ when I am _______ ."
Tip: Emotional territory — let the student connect. If they get enthusiastic about their pet, let them talk. This is where "to be" becomes real communication, not grammar exercise.
Question 5
What is the worst thing about having a pet?
Teacher: "The worst thing? Hair! There is hair everywhere — on my clothes, on my sofa, on my bed. And the early morning walks — sometimes it is cold and dark and I am tired. That is terrible!"
Now you: "The worst thing is _______ . It is _______ because..."
Tip: Always gets honest answers. Common: "the hair," "the noise," "the vet." Natural "there is" opportunity: "There is hair on my sofa." Model it if it appears.
Question 6
What kind of person are you — a dog person or a cat person?
Teacher: "I am a dog person, 100%! Dog people are active, social, and loud — like dogs. Cat people are calm, independent, and quiet — like cats. I think your pet is a mirror of your personality!"
Now you: "I am a _______ person because I am _______ and _______ ."
Tip: The "mirror" idea is powerful — it makes the student describe THEMSELVES through their pet preference. Ask: "So you are a [dog/cat] person — is your personality similar to a [dog/cat]?" Generates self-description with "to be."
