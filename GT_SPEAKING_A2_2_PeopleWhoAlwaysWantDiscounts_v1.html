§ 01 · The Trigger
🪝 The Art of Asking for Less
Read aloud · First reaction · No debate yet · 4 min max
01
4 min
🏷️
Nielsen Consumer Behavior Report + Deloitte Mexico Shopping Study 2023
78%
Of Mexican shoppers say they always look for a discount or offer before buying something
3 in 4
Vendors at markets and small shops say they expect customers to negotiate — they already have a higher starting price
15%
The average discount you can get just by asking — in many Mexican markets and small shops
1 in 5
People feel embarrassed to ask for a discount — even when they know they can get one
Sources: Nielsen Consumer Behavior Report Mexico 2023 · Deloitte Mexico Retail Study · CONDUSEF Shopping Habits Survey
🪝 Opening Question — Read and point at someone
"Do you ask for discounts when you shop? Yes or no — and where?"
Teacher Mechanics — 4 min Very simple. Point at one student: "You — do you ask for discounts? Yes or no?" 2–3 quick answers. Then: "OK. Today we talk about this."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • The 15% stat (average discount just from asking) is the most actionable — it reframes asking as smart, not cheap. Use it: "You can get 15% just by asking. Do you do that?"
  • The trigger question is deliberately binary: yes or no. At A2.2, starting with yes/no then expanding is the correct sequence.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "78% of Mexicans look for discounts. That means almost everyone in this room. Let's talk about it."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Retention: "This is our last Money lab for this lote. Great conversation topic for any shopping situation in English."

⚡ Production Challenge
Pick 3 words. One sentence each about the last time you bought something. Short and simple. Just talk.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • "Negotiate" might be new at A2.2 — model it: "To negotiate = to talk about the price until both people agree. 'Can you give me a better price?' That's negotiating."
  • "Bargain" — clarify it's both a noun (good deal) and a verb (to negotiate). Keep simple: "A bargain = cheap and good quality."
  • Best production trigger: "Three words about the last thing you bought. Was it cheap or expensive? Did you negotiate?"
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "These words are perfect for any market, any shop, any situation where you need to talk about prices in English."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention

§ 03 · Opinion Wall
🧱 4 Real Voices. 4 Ways to Shop.
Read each card · Pairs 60 sec · Group shares · Same question each time
03
10 min
Processing Question — Same for all 4
"Who do you agree with? Why?"
Keep it simple
🔁 Processing Question — Pairs → Group
"Who do you agree with? Why? Use 2 or 3 sentences."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • The provocateur is Rodrigo — his "I always negotiate, even in restaurants" position is the most extreme and will generate reactions ranging from laughter to disagreement. At A2.2, even a simple "No, that's too much!" is a great response.
  • Read all 4 before opening. The contrast between Rodrigo (negotiate everything) and Andrea (pay the price) drives the simple debate.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • After all 4: "And you — are you more like Rodrigo, or more like Andrea?" That one question keeps the energy moving.
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Testimonial trigger: "Which person is most like someone in your family? Tell me in one sentence."
§ 04 · The Conversation Engine
🗣 10 Questions. 4 Blocks. 23 Minutes.
Past → Opinion → Future → Past habits · Short answers OK · Keep moving
04
23 min
⚡
A2.2 Rule: Short answers are OK. 1 or 2 sentences is great. Don't wait for perfect English — just talk.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Block 1 past simple — "Did you ask for a discount?" is a yes/no question at the right A2.2 level. Always push for the follow-up: "Where? What did they say?"
  • Block 3 future — "Are you going to ask for a discount the next time you buy something?" Give students time to think. Point to a chip if they're stuck.
  • Block 4 "used to" — Very accessible here: "I used to never ask for discounts. Now I always ask." Model it with a personal story first.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • Q5 is the most fun question: "Do you know someone who always asks for discounts?" Point at different students. Laughter will happen. Let it.
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • After Q10: "You talked about shopping, prices, and money — in English — for 23 minutes. That's progress. That's A2.2."
§ 05 · The Exit Shot
💣 One Question. Your Answer.
5 min · No chips · Answer in 2–3 sentences · Then the loop
05
5 min
EXIT
💣 Exit Shot
The next time you buy something at a market or a small shop — are you going to ask for a discount? What are you going to say?
Answer in 2 or 3 sentences. Tell me yes or no — and tell me exactly WHAT you are going to say. In English.
📌 A2.2 Model Answer — Reveal AFTER student speaks
"Yes, I am going to ask. I am going to say: 'Is this your best price? Can you give me a discount?' And if they say no, I am going to say 'OK, thank you' and maybe walk away slowly."
🔁 Retention Loop — WhatsApp Homework "Before next session — record a 60-second voice note in English. Your answer to today's Exit Shot. Don't write it first. Just talk. Send it to [teacher number]."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • The Exit Shot asks for actual English phrases — "what are you going to SAY?" This makes it a communicative output task, not just a planning one. At A2.2, producing even one real phrase is a win.
  • If a student says "I don't know what to say" — give them the model phrases: "Is this your best price?" and "Can you give me a discount?"
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • The model answer has a real negotiation strategy: "walk away slowly." Point out: "That's a real negotiation technique — even in English, 'walk away slowly' is a strategy!"
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Closing: "You now know how to ask for a discount in English. Use it this week. Record your answer tonight."
