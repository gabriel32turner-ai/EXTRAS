Phase 4
Question Cards
The teacher answers first. Then it's your turn.
12 MIN
Question 1
Who is your favorite famous person? Where is he or she from?
Teacher: "My favorite famous person is Guillermo del Toro. He is from Guadalajara, México. He is a director. He is very creative and very intelligent."
Now you: "My favorite famous person is _______ . He/She is from..."
Tip: If student says only the name, push for full identity: "Where is he/she from? What is he/she? An actor? A singer?" Build the description piece by piece.
Question 2
Is this person talented or just famous?
Teacher: "Del Toro is very talented. His movies are beautiful and original. He is not just famous — he is a real artist. Some famous people are only famous because of social media. That is different."
Now you: "He/She is talented because... He/She is not just famous, he/she is..."
Tip: Introduce the contrast "talented vs. just famous" — it forces an opinion with "to be." If student says "yes, talented," push: "Talented HOW? In what? Give me an example."
Question 3
Is there a famous person who is overrated for you?
Teacher: "Hmm... for me, some influencers are overrated. They are famous but they are not talented. They are just popular on Instagram. That is not the same for me."
Now you: "For me, _______ is overrated because he/she is..."
Tip: This is where it gets fun — students love giving honest opinions. If they are shy, offer: "OK, not a name — just a TYPE of famous person. Influencers? Reggaeton singers?" Let them be honest.
Question 4
Is your favorite famous person humble or arrogant?
Teacher: "Del Toro is very humble. In interviews, he is funny and normal. He is not arrogant. That is why I like him — he is famous but he is still a real person."
Now you: "He/She is _______ . In interviews, he/she is..."
Tip: Push the "humble vs. arrogant" contrast. Ask: "Is that important for you? When a famous person is arrogant, is that a problem?" Generates opinion with simple structures.
Question 5
Is there a famous person from your city or state?
Teacher: "Hmm, I am from Argentina. Messi is from Argentina! He is the most famous Argentine in the world. He is quiet and humble. For us, he is not just an athlete — he is a hero."
Now you: "From my city / state, there is _______ . He/She is..."
Tip: Students from Guanajuato might struggle with this — help them: "From your state? From México in general? Who is famous from México that you are proud of?" Broad is OK.
Question 6
What is the most important thing about a famous person — talent, personality, or looks?
Teacher: "For me, talent is the most important thing. Looks are nice, but they are not everything. Personality is also important — when a famous person is kind and humble, that is beautiful."
Now you: "For me, the most important thing is _______ because..."
Tip: This is the philosophical peak. Let the student take time. If they say "talent," ask "Why? Is a talented but arrogant person OK?" Force the reasoning.
