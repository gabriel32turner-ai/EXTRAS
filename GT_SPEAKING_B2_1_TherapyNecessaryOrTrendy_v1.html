Therapy: Necessary or Trendy?

Everyone's talking to a therapist. But are we genuinely healing — or just making trauma a personality?

🪝
Phase 1
The Trigger
⏱ 4 min
"42% of Americans have seen a therapist — a record high. But mental health professionals warn that 'therapy culture' on social media is creating a generation of people who self-diagnose, over-pathologize normal emotions, and avoid real-life challenges by reframing them as trauma."
— The Atlantic / American Psychological Association, 2024
Teacher script: "Quick show of hands — or just tell me: has anyone here ever seen a therapist, or considered it? No judgment at all." [Pause, let students respond naturally.] "And what about your parents? Would they have gone to therapy? Let's talk about where that shift happened — and whether it's a good thing."

Phase 4
The Conversation Engine
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 — Personal Experience
Q1
Have you ever talked to a therapist, a coach, or even a close friend about something you were struggling with? How did it help — or not?
Very open first question. Don't push for therapy specifically — "a close friend" gives an exit for students who feel uncomfortable. Present perfect triggers naturally here.
Q2
Growing up, how did people in your family deal with stress or emotional problems? Was talking about it encouraged?
This surfaces generational and cultural differences around mental health. Mexican culture often has a "strong family but no therapy" pattern. Let students explore this without judgment.
Q3
Have you ever felt like you couldn't talk about your real problems at work? What did you do instead?
Bridges personal and professional worlds. Strong present perfect + past usage. Model: "I've never felt safe enough to be honest about stress at work. I just pushed through."
Block 2 — Opinion
Q4
Do you think therapy is more effective than talking to a good friend? What can a therapist do that a friend can't?
Draws out the "professional objectivity" vs "genuine human connection" tension. Both are valid. Push: "What are the limits of each?"
Q5
Should companies provide free mental health support to employees? Or is that too personal for the workplace?
Great for modals of obligation + professional opinions. Many Mexican companies now offer EAP programs. "Should companies...?" = natural B2 debate territory.
Q6
Is it a problem that mental health language — "triggered," "toxic," "trauma" — has become so casual? Or is that a sign of progress?
This is the "therapy speak" debate. Both sides are strong. Normalization = good. Trivialization = bad. Push them to take a clear side, not sit in the middle.
Block 3 — Hypothetical
Q7
If therapy were completely free and anonymous, would you go? What would you want to talk about?
Second conditional. The anonymity angle often unlocks honesty — "If it were anonymous, I would..." removes the shame barrier. Don't press for what specifically they'd discuss.
Q8
If you found out a colleague was seeing a therapist, would it change how you see them — even slightly?
This tests actual vs stated attitudes toward stigma. Most people say "no" — then pause. Follow up: "What about a boss? A direct report? Would that change anything?" Very revealing.
Block 4 — Provocative
Q9
Is therapy a privilege? If it costs $80–$150 a session, who actually gets to "work on themselves" — and who just has to cope?
Class and access angle. Very real in Mexico. The irony: the people who need it most can afford it least. Strong opinions here. Let the room breathe.
Q10
Have you ever used "I need to take care of my mental health" as an excuse to avoid something hard — instead of actually dealing with it?
The self-reflection bomb. Honest answer: yes, most people have. This is about the difference between genuine self-care and avoidance dressed up in therapeutic language. Let the silence work.

Phase 5
The Exit Shot
⏱ 5 min
Final Question
Your 16-year-old son or daughter tells you they want to see a therapist. They say they feel "overwhelmed and anxious" — but from what you can see, their life is fine. Do you pay for it without question, or do you talk to them first to understand if they really need it?
