§ 01 · The Trigger
🪝 How Mexico Sleeps
Read aloud · First reaction · No debate yet · 4 min max
01
4 min
😴
IMSS Sleep Health Report + Philips Sleep Survey México 2023
6 hrs
The average sleep time for an adult Mexican worker — doctors recommend 7 to 9 hours
63%
Of Mexican adults say they feel tired when they wake up in the morning — every single day
1 in 3
Mexicans look at their phone in bed for more than 30 minutes before they sleep
12am
The average time Mexicans go to bed — much later than recommended for a healthy sleep routine
Sources: IMSS Reporte de Salud del Sueño 2023 · Philips Global Sleep Survey · UNAM Estudio de Hábitos de Sueño
🪝 Opening Question — Read and point at someone
"What time did you go to bed last night? And what time did you wake up? Are you tired right now?"
Teacher Mechanics — 4 min Very simple. Three short questions — time, wake up, tired now. Point at one student. Accept one-word or one-sentence answers. 2–3 students. Then: "OK. Let's talk about sleep."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Sleep is deeply universal — every student has a sleep experience, good or bad. Most will have a bad one. The topic activates immediately.
  • The 63% stat is the most relatable — point at the room: "63% of Mexicans wake up tired. Who here is in that 63%? Raise your hand."
  • Accept any answer for the opening question — "I went to bed at 11" or just "late" are both fine at A2.2.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "We sleep only 6 hours on average. That's not enough. Does that sound like you?"
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Retention: "Next lab is coffee and tea — another daily habit, same personal level."

⚡ Production Challenge
Pick 3 words. One sentence each about your sleep last night or this week. Short and honest. Just talk.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • "Alarm" — very high frequency at A2.2. Most students know it. Confirm: "The thing that wakes you up — alarm clock or phone alarm. Same word."
  • "Nightmare" — students often know this word from movies. It's a fun one to demonstrate with a face.
  • Best trigger: "Three words that describe YOUR sleep. Be honest. Go."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Every word here is something that happens in your bedroom every night. In English now."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "If you ever go to a doctor in English, or talk about your health — these words are essential."

Processing Question — Same for all 4
"Who do you agree with? Why?"

🔁 Processing Question — Pairs → Group
"Who do you agree with? Why? Say 2 or 3 sentences."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • The provocateur is Andrés — his "I sleep 5 hours and I feel fine" position is warmly challenging. Students who are always tired will react. Even: "No! I need 8 hours!" is a great A2.2 output.
  • At A2.2, "I agree with Valeria because I also look at my phone before bed" is a complete, excellent answer.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • After reading all 4: point at students: "You — are you more like Andrés or more like Carmen? Be honest."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Testimonial trigger: "Which person sleeps most like you? Tell me in one sentence."
§ 04 · The Conversation Engine
🗣 10 Questions. 4 Blocks. 23 Minutes.
Past → Opinion → Future → Past habits · Short answers OK · Keep moving
04
23 min
⚡
A2.2 Rule: Short answers are OK. 1 or 2 sentences is great. Don't wait for perfect English — just talk.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Block 1 past simple — sleep questions produce very short, specific answers. "I went to bed at midnight" is perfect. Don't push for more unless they have a story.
  • Block 3 "going to" — sleep plans are easy: "I am going to sleep earlier" or "I am going to put my phone away." Both are excellent A2.2 future plans.
  • Block 4 "used to" — childhood sleep is a great topic: "I used to sleep at 8pm when I was a child." Very accessible memory.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • Q5 is the most immediately funny: "Is a nap good or bad?" Every Mexican adult has a nap opinion. Let the energy be playful.
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • After Q10: "You talked about sleep, health, and habits — in English. That's real conversation."
§ 05 · The Exit Shot
💣 One Question. Your Answer.
5 min · No chips · Answer in 2–3 sentences · Then the loop
05
5 min
EXIT
💣 Exit Shot
Are you going to change one thing about your sleep this week? What is it?
Answer in 2 or 3 sentences. Use "I am going to..." Be specific — one real change, not a general idea.
📌 A2.2 Model Answer — Reveal AFTER student speaks
"I am going to put my phone in another room before bed. I think that is my biggest problem. I look at it for one hour and then I can't sleep. This week I am going to try."
🔁 Retention Loop — WhatsApp Homework "Before next session — record a 60-second voice note in English. Your answer to today's Exit Shot. Don't write it first. Just talk. Send it to [teacher number]."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • "One specific change" forces A2.2 students to go beyond "I am going to sleep better." Push: "OK — but HOW? What exactly are you going to change?"
  • If a student says nothing: give them a chip — "Put the phone away? Sleep earlier? No coffee at night? Pick one."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • The model answer names a specific habit (phone in another room) and a reason. Point out: "One specific thing. That's the difference between a plan and a wish."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Closing: "Record tonight. Tell me your sleep plan in English. 60 seconds. Go."
