B2.1 Money & Finance 🟡 Medium 50 min
Lifestyle Inflation
You earn more every year. But somehow, you never have more. Why?
🪝
The Trigger
Phase 1 · Provocation & Opening
⏱ 4 min
McKinsey & Company · Consumer Survey · 2023
"As incomes rise, spending rises at the same rate or faster for 74% of professionals — meaning higher earnings rarely translate into actual financial progress."
Teacher script: Read it. Then: "Think about the last time you got a raise. What changed in your spending? Did you actually feel richer — or did the money just disappear into new things?" Pause for 10 seconds. Let them sit with the question before anyone speaks.

The Opinion Wall
Phase 3 · 4 Perspectives · Who sounds like you?
⏱ 10 min
🛍️
Lucía · 32
Account Executive · Querétaro
I doubled my salary in three years. I also doubled my rent, bought a car I don't need, and upgraded my phone twice. I'm not ashamed of it. I worked hard and I deserve to enjoy the money. Life is short. I can invest when I'm 40. Right now I want to live.
💰
Javier · 45
Finance Director · Monterrey
I've seen colleagues earn 4 times what I earn and retire with nothing. Lifestyle inflation is the most expensive trap professionals fall into. Every peso you spend upgrading your life when you get a raise is a peso you'll never get back. The only thing that should inflate with your income is your investment portfolio.
🌶 Provocateur
🎭
Valeria · 38
Brand Manager · CDMX
Most people don't have a spending problem — they have an income problem. The conversation about lifestyle inflation is mostly used to shame people who earn average salaries for wanting to live like human beings. If you earn $15,000 pesos a month and your expenses go up, that's not inflation — that's inflation and poverty, which are two completely different things.
🤔
Daniel · 51
Operations Manager · Guadalajara
I think some lifestyle inflation is healthy. When you earn more, spending a little more on quality, health, and experiences genuinely improves your life. The problem isn't upgrading — it's upgrading on things that don't matter: the car that's 30% more expensive but gets you to the same destination.

The Conversation Engine
Phase 4 · 10 Questions · 4 Blocks
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 · Personal Experience
Q1
Has your salary increased significantly in the last 3 years? Did your savings increase at the same rate — or did your spending absorb most of the raise?
Teacher Tip Most people will admit the spending absorbed it. Don't let them generalize — push for a specific example. "What specifically did you start spending on?"
Q2
What's something you spend money on now that felt like a luxury three years ago but feels completely normal today?
Teacher Tip This is the core of lifestyle inflation — normalization. Great answers: Uber instead of bus, restaurants twice a week, Spotify AND Netflix AND Apple TV. Let them list it out.
Q3
Have you ever made a financial decision — a car, apartment, phone — mainly because of what other people would think? Be honest.
Model Answer "Yes. I bought a car that was way above my budget partly because I was embarrassed about the one I had. I told myself it was an investment. It wasn't. It was ego. And I paid for that ego for four years."
Block 2 · Opinion
Q4
Is lifestyle inflation inevitable — or is it a choice? Do you think most people are aware they're doing it?
Teacher Tip Push them on the "choice" angle. "If it's a choice, why don't more people choose differently?" Great entry into social pressure and marketing psychology.
Q5
Do you think companies take advantage of lifestyle inflation — raising your cost of living so you can't afford to quit?
Model Answer "I think it's not always intentional, but it works that way. If you've upgraded your apartment, your car, your kids' school — you need your job more than you did when you were earning less. That's leverage for the company, even if they didn't plan it."
Q6
At what point does enjoying your money become a financial trap? Is there a number, a percentage, a feeling?
Teacher Tip Encourage creative answers. There's no right answer. Push for specifics: "Is it when you can't say no to a bad job? When you have no savings buffer? When you feel trapped?"
Block 3 · Hypothetical
Q7
If you got a 40% raise tomorrow, what would you actually do with it — and what do you think you should do with it? Are those two answers the same?
Model Answer "If I'm honest — I'd upgrade my apartment. What I should do is invest 70% and keep my lifestyle exactly the same. Those two answers are very different, and the distance between them is probably what's keeping me from being financially free."
Q8
If you had to go back to the lifestyle you had 5 years ago — same apartment, same phone, same budget — could you do it? How would it feel?
Teacher Tip This is the trap question. If they can't go back — lifestyle inflation has already worked on them. A great moment to use the word "trap" and ask: "Does that bother you?"
Block 4 · Provocative
Q9
Do you think your current spending reflects your values — or are you spending to manage stress, impress others, or feel in control?
Teacher Tip This is the deepest question in the block. Give it time. If they go quiet, that's good data. "Take your time. This one's worth thinking about."
Q10
If you kept earning exactly what you earn now for the next 10 years — no raises, no promotions — would your current lifestyle be sustainable? Would you be okay?
Model Answer "Honestly, no. And that's the problem. I've built a lifestyle that depends on continuous income growth. If that growth stops — for any reason — I'm in trouble. That's not financial security. That's a very expensive kind of anxiety."
💣
The Exit Shot
Phase 5 · Final Dilemma · No right answer
⏱ 5 min
💣 Exit Shot
You've just calculated that you spend 92% of your monthly income — and you've been doing it for years. You have almost no savings, but you live comfortably, eat well, travel twice a year, and feel good day to day. Your colleague earns 30% less, lives more modestly, and has 18 months of savings in the bank. Who has the better life — and whose choices would you actually make if you could restart?
📱 WhatsApp Homework Record 60 seconds in English: What is one expense in your life right now that you know, deep down, is lifestyle inflation — not a need? What would you do with that money if you cut it?
