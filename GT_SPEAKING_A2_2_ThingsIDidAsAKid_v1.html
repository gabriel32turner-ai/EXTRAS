The Trigger
Phase 1 · Opening Question
⏱ 4 min
A simple memory question
"Close your eyes for 5 seconds. Think about something you did every day as a child — something you loved. Now open your eyes. Do you still do that thing?"
Teacher script: Read it slowly. Give them 5 actual seconds to think. Then share yours: "When I was a kid, I used to [play football / read comics / ride my bike]. Now I never do it. It's strange when I think about it." Then ask: "What did you think about? What did you use to do?" This exercise works perfectly every time.

The Conversation Engine
Phase 4 · 10 Questions · 4 Blocks
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 · Past Experience
Q1
What did you use to do every day after school when you were a child?
Model Answer "I used to go to my grandmother's house. She always had food ready. Then I played in the street with my cousins until dark. Every day. For years. I loved it."
Q2
What food did you use to love as a kid that you don't eat now — or what food did you hate that you like now?
Teacher Tip Universal and fun topic. "Vegetables? Spicy food? Things your mom made that you refused to eat? When did that change?" Great for simple past and "used to" in one question.
Q3
What did you use to be afraid of when you were young? Are you still afraid of that thing?
Model Answer "I used to be very afraid of the dark. I slept with the light on until I was 10. Now... I still prefer the light on. My wife laughs at me."
Block 2 · Opinion
Q4
Do you think children today have a better or worse childhood than you did? Why?
Teacher Tip Simple comparison — easy to discuss. "More technology, less outside time. More options, less freedom? What do you think children miss today?" Keep answers in simple present and past simple.
Q5
Do you think adults should do more "child things" — like play games, draw, or play outside? Or is that silly?
Model Answer "I think yes. When I play with my kids, I feel... lighter. Like the problems are small for a moment. I don't think it's silly. I think it's necessary."
Q6
Is it good to miss your childhood — or is it better to focus on the present?
Teacher Tip Simple reflective question. No wrong answer. "Is nostalgia a good thing or a bad thing for you? Does thinking about the past make you happy or sad?" Let them explore with simple vocabulary.
Block 3 · Future Plans
Q7
Is there something from your childhood you are going to start doing again — a hobby, a game, a food, a habit?
Model Answer "Yes! I'm going to start riding my bike again. I used to ride every weekend. Now my bike is in the garage with dust on it. I'm going to clean it this Saturday. Really."
Q8
If you have children now, or if you want them in the future, what childhood experience do you want to give them?
Teacher Tip Simple "want to" and "going to" for future. "More time outside? Less phone? A specific sport or game? Tell me one thing you will do differently from how you grew up — or one thing you will keep the same."
Block 4 · Past Habits & Reflection
Q9
Did you use to spend a lot of time with your grandparents when you were young? What did you do together?
Model Answer "Yes. My grandmother's house was the center of the family. Sunday lunch, every week. She cooked for 15 people. I used to help her in the kitchen. I learned to cook from her. I miss those Sundays."
Q10
What did you use to think you wanted to be when you grew up? Did that happen?
Teacher Tip Classic A2.2 topic — "used to" with childhood dreams. "Did you say doctor, astronaut, singer, football player? When did you change your mind? Are you happy with what you do now?" Keep it light — this is fun, not sad.

The Exit Shot
Phase 5 · Final Question · Warm & Personal
⏱ 5 min
💣 Exit Shot
You have one free Saturday with no plans, no work, no phone, no responsibilities. You can do ANYTHING you did as a child. What are you going to do — and who are you going to call to do it with you?
📱 WhatsApp Homework Record 60 seconds in English: What are you going to do on that Saturday? Describe it — and explain why you chose that thing.
