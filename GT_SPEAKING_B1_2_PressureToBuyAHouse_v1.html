The Pressure to Buy a House
Your parents say rent is "throwing money away." Your friends just bought. Are you behind — or is buying a house not for everyone?
🪝
The Trigger
Phase 1 · Provocation & Opening
⏱ 4 min
BBC News · 2023
"For the first time in 30 years, more adults under 40 in Mexico are renting than owning. But 78% of them say they still feel they 'should' buy a house — even when they don't want to."
Teacher script: Read it. Then ask: "Do you own a house or do you rent? How do you feel about that?" Wait. Then: "Has anyone — your parents, your partner, a friend — ever made you feel bad for renting? Or for not buying yet?" This gets personal fast. Let it.

The Conversation Engine
Phase 4 · 10 Questions · 4 Blocks
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 · Personal Experience
Q1
Have you ever felt pressure from your family to buy a house? What did they say — and how did it make you feel?
Teacher Tip Very common in Mexican families. Push for specifics: "Who said it — your mom, your dad, your grandparents? What exactly did they say? Did you feel guilty, angry, or were they right?"
Q2
Have you ever compared yourself to a friend or colleague who bought a house — and felt behind or stressed about it?
Model Answer "Yes. My friend bought at 30 and I felt like I was failing. But when I looked at his mortgage payments, I realized I had more money at the end of every month. Still — the feeling was there. It was not rational."
Q3
Have you ever moved to a new city or changed jobs and thought: "I'm glad I don't have a mortgage right now"?
Teacher Tip This is the flexibility argument for renting. Great for students who are mobile. Push: "Would a mortgage have stopped you from making that move? How would your life be different?"
Block 2 · Opinion
Q4
Do you think renting is really "throwing money away" — or is that just something older generations say?
Teacher Tip Great for critical thinking. Push: "Is it possible that this idea was true 30 years ago but is not true today? What changed?" Mortgage rates, house prices, job mobility — all useful vocabulary here.
Q5
In your opinion, at what age should a person buy a house — or does age have nothing to do with it?
Model Answer "I don't think age matters. I think stability matters — a stable job, a stable relationship, a clear idea of where you want to live. If you have those things at 25, go ahead. If you don't have them at 45, wait."
Q6
Do you think a house is an investment — something that makes you money — or just a place to live?
Teacher Tip This divides people cleanly. Some see it as pure investment, others as purely personal. Push: "Have you ever seen someone lose money on a house? Or make a lot? What happened?"
Block 3 · Hypothetical
Q7
What would you do if your parents offered to give you money for a down payment — but only if you bought in the city where they live?
Model Answer "That's really hard. The money would help a lot. But I don't want to live in their city — I want to build my life where I am. I think I would say thank you and no. But it would not be easy to say that to my parents."
Q8
What would you do if you could buy a house right now — but it would mean no vacations, no eating out, and no savings for 5 years?
Teacher Tip Real trade-off question. Push: "Is the house worth 5 years of that life? What would you be giving up that matters most to you?" Quality of life vs. financial security — no easy answer.
Block 4 · Provocative
Q9
Do you think you actually want to buy a house — or do you just feel like you should because everyone says so?
Teacher Tip The core question of the whole session. Give it real space. "If nobody in your life had an opinion about it — your parents, your friends, your partner — would you still want to buy? Or is it mostly social pressure?" Silence is a good answer here.
Q10
When you imagine your life at 60, do you picture yourself in a house you own — or does it not really matter to you?
Model Answer "When I really think about it — I picture being somewhere comfortable and safe. I don't think I picture owning specifically. I think I picture not worrying about money. Maybe a house is one way to get there. But it's not the only way."

The Exit Shot
Phase 5 · Final Dilemma · No right answer
⏱ 5 min
💣 Exit Shot
Your partner really wants to buy a house. You are not sure. You found one you both like — $1,800,000 pesos. The mortgage payment would be $14,000 pesos a month for 20 years. Your rent right now is $8,500. You would have to say goodbye to your savings, your vacations, and your emergency fund for at least 3 years. Your partner says: "If we don't buy now, we never will." What do you do?
