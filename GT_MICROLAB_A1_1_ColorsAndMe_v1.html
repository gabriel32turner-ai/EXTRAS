Colors and Me

Describe your favorite colors. Say why you like them. Use only "to be" and adjectives.

🎪 Show & Tell 💉 Vocab 🔀 Choose & Speak 🃏 Questions ✨ My Sentence

Phase 1
Show & Tell
Show something with your favorite color and describe it
5 MIN
Today's Object

Show your phone case, shirt, bag, or any object with a color you like and describe it.

Teacher models first:
"Look, this is my coffee cup. It is blue. Blue is my favorite color. I like blue because it is calm and beautiful. My phone is also blue. I am a blue person! OK, now you — show me something with YOUR favorite color."

Phase 4
Question Cards
The teacher answers first. Then it's your turn.
12 MIN
Question 1
What is your favorite color?
Teacher: "My favorite color is blue. Dark blue. It is calm and elegant. My room is blue. My car is blue. I am a blue person!"
Now you: "My favorite color is..."
Tip: If student says only one word ("blue"), push: "OK, blue is... what? Beautiful? Calm? Dark? Give me more!" Expect 2-3 sentences minimum.
Question 2
Is your car / phone / bag a color you like?
Teacher: "My phone is black. I like black for phones. But my car is white — white is OK, not my favorite. It is boring but clean."
Now you: "My _______ is _______ . It is..."
Tip: Ask follow-up: "Is that color YOUR choice or was it the only option?" Student practices: "It is / It is not my choice."
Question 3
What color is ugly for you?
Teacher: "For me, orange is ugly. Sorry, orange! It is too bright. It is not elegant. Brown is also ugly for me — it is sad and boring."
Now you: "For me, _______ is ugly because..."
Tip: This is where students laugh and relax. Push for reasons: "WHY is it ugly? Is it too bright? Too dark? Too boring?" Model the adjectives they just learned.
Question 4
What color is your house? Is it nice?
Teacher: "My house is white outside. Inside, my room is blue and my kitchen is yellow. Yellow is warm and happy for a kitchen."
Now you: "My house is _______ . Inside, my _______ is..."
Tip: Great opportunity for "there is": "There is a red wall in my kitchen." If student produces this naturally, celebrate it.
Question 5
What is a happy color? What is a sad color?
Teacher: "For me, yellow is a happy color. It is the color of the sun. Gray is a sad color — rainy days are gray and boring."
Now you: "_______ is a happy color because... _______ is a sad color because..."
Tip: Connect to mood vocab from Phase 2: "So when you are sad, what color is your mood? When you are happy?" This bridges vocab to production.
Question 6
Who is a person with great colors in their clothes?
Teacher: "My friend Ana — she is always in bright colors. Red, pink, yellow. She is a fun person. Her clothes are always beautiful. I am boring — always black!"
Now you: "_______ is a person with... His/Her clothes are..."
Tip: If student mentions a colleague, push: "Is your boss a color person? What color is he/she always in?" Transition to real-life characters.

Phase 5
My Sentence
Build one perfect sentence. Say it 3 times. Record it.
5 MIN
Teacher says: "OK, last part. Build ONE perfect sentence about colors and your life. Use this structure. I go first: 'The most important color in my life is blue because it is calm and it is everywhere in my house.' Now you."
