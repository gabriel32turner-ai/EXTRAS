Phase 4
Question Cards
The teacher answers first. Then it's your turn.
12 MIN
Question 1
What is your boss's name? How old is he or she?
Teacher: "My boss is Ricardo. He is 52 years old. He is from Monterrey. He is tall and always in a suit."
Now you: "My boss is _______ . He/She is _______ years old."
Tip: Start simple — name + age + city. If student gives only the name, push: "How old? Where is he/she from?" Build the description step by step.
Question 2
Is your boss nice or difficult?
Teacher: "My boss is nice, but he is also very demanding. In meetings, he is serious. But in the morning, he is funny. He is two things at the same time!"
Now you: "My boss is _______ . But sometimes he/she is also _______ ."
Tip: Push the "but" structure — it forces 2 adjectives in one sentence. "He is nice BUT demanding" is A1.1 gold. Celebrate it.
Question 3
Is your boss fair with the team?
Teacher: "Hmm... my boss is fair with most people. But sometimes he is not fair with new people. New people are nervous, and he is not patient with them."
Now you: "My boss is fair / not fair with... because..."
Tip: If student says "yes" or "no" only, push: "Fair with WHO? With you? With everyone? With new people?" Force specifics.
Question 4
Where is your boss's office? Is it big or small?
Teacher: "His office is on the second floor. It is big — there are two desks and many chairs. There is a big window. It is a nice office."
Now you: "His/Her office is _______ . There is / There are..."
Tip: Natural opportunity for "there is/there are" — don't force it, but if student says "it has a window," redirect to "there is a window." Model the difference.
Question 5
How is your boss in meetings? Calm or intense?
Teacher: "In meetings, my boss is intense. He is very fast. Sometimes he is impatient. But when the meeting is good, he is happy and positive."
Now you: "In meetings, my boss is _______ . He/She is _______ when..."
Tip: Introduce "when" as a simple connector: "He is happy WHEN the results are good." Student can produce this at A1.1 with support.
Question 6
Is your boss a good boss? What is the best thing about him or her?
Teacher: "Yes, Ricardo is a good boss. The best thing is — he is honest. When there is a problem, he is direct. That is important for me."
Now you: "My boss is a good / not good boss. The best thing is..."
Tip: This is the emotional peak. Let the student talk freely. If they smile or laugh, you win — they just described a real person in English. Say: "You just described your boss in English. That is impressive for A1."
