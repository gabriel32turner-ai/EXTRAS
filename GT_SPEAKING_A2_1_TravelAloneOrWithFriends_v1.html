Travel Alone
or With Friends?

Who decides the restaurant. Who picks the hotel. Who wants to sleep when you want to go out. Let's talk about it.

The Trigger
Phase 1 · Open the conversation
4 min
Travel survey · 2023
"63% of travelers say they had a fight with a friend or partner during a vacation. The most common reason: decisions about food and activities."
Teacher script: Read the stat. Then: "63 percent. That's most people. Did you ever fight with a friend or your partner on a trip? Just yes or no." Quick show of hands. Then: "Who said yes? Tell me in 2 sentences — what was the fight about?"

The Conversation Engine
Phase 4 · 10 questions · 4 blocks
23 min
Block 1 — Past Experience
Q1
Did you ever travel with friends or family and have a problem or fight? What happened?
Teacher tip: The 63% stat opened this already. Let the story come naturally in past simple. Push: "Who started the fight? How did it end? Did you enjoy the rest of the trip?"
Q2
Did you ever travel alone — even for one day or one night? How did you feel?
Model answer: "Yes. One time I went to a conference alone in another city. I was there for 2 days. I ate dinner alone both nights. I felt a little lonely at first. But then I relaxed. I did what I wanted. It was actually nice."
Q3
What was the best trip you took with another person? Who went with you and why was it good?
Teacher tip: Past simple for narration. Push: "What did you do together? What was the best moment? Is that person easy to travel with?"
Block 2 — Opinion
Q4
What is better — traveling with 1 person, a group of friends, or alone?
Teacher tip: Force a ranking. "Number 1, 2, 3 — which is the best for you? Why?" Model: "For me: 1 — with one person. 2 — alone. 3 — with a group. A group is too complicated. Someone always complains."
Q5
Is it OK to travel without your partner — with friends only?
Teacher tip: Strong opinion question. Very relatable in Mexican culture. Push: "Do YOU do this? Does your partner agree? Is there a fight when you go with friends?"
Q6
Do you think it's important for a couple to travel together — or is it better to travel separately sometimes?
Model answer: "I think both are important. Together is good for the relationship — you make memories. But separate trips are also good. You miss each other more. And you have your own stories to tell."
Block 3 — Future Plans
Q7
Are you going to travel with friends, family, or alone on your next vacation?
Teacher tip: "Going to" practice with real personal stakes. Follow up: "Is that your first choice — or the plan because no one else wants to go?"
Q8
If a friend asks you to travel together next month, what are you going to say?
Model answer: "I'm going to say yes — but first I'm going to ask: Where are we going? For how many days? And how much money are we going to spend? I need to know before I say yes. Some friends are easy to travel with. Others are not."
Block 4 — Provocative
Q9
Be honest — are YOU a difficult person to travel with? What do you do that bothers people?
Teacher tip: Best question of the session. Give silence. Common honest answers: "I always want to sleep late," "I spend too much time on my phone," "I complain about the food," "I get lost." Celebrate honesty.
Q10
Your friend asks you to go on a 10-day trip, but you know this person is very difficult to travel with. What are you going to do?
Teacher tip: Fun final scenario. Push with "going to": "Are you going to say yes and suffer? Are you going to say no and lose the friendship? Are you going to make excuses? Or are you going to say: 'Yes, but we need rules'?"

The Exit Shot
Phase 5 · One final question
5 min
💣 Exit Shot
You are on vacation with your best friend. It's day 3. You want to visit museums. Your friend only wants to go to the beach and eat. You have 4 more days. What are you going to do?

Homework — WhatsApp Voice Note
