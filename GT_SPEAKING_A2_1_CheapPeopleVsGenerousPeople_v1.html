A2.1 Money & Finance 🟡 Medium 50 min
Cheap People vs.
Generous People
Do you know someone who never pays? Or someone who always pays for everyone? Which one are you?
🪝
The Trigger
Phase 1 · Warm-Up
⏱ 4 min
Simple question to start
"Think about the last time you went to a restaurant with friends or family. Who paid? Did everyone pay the same? Did someone try to NOT pay?"
Teacher script: Read it slowly. Then share a quick personal story: "Last week I went to lunch with a friend. He looked at his phone when the bill came. Classic!" Then ask: "What about you? What happened the last time you went out to eat with people?" Keep it light and funny — this topic opens fast.

The Conversation Engine
Phase 4 · 10 Questions · 4 Blocks
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 · Past Experience
Q1
Did someone cheap person make you angry recently? What happened?
Teacher Tip Push for the story: "Who was it — a friend, coworker, family member? What did they do exactly? Did you say something or stay quiet?" All in simple past.
Q2
When was the last time you paid for someone else — a friend, your family? How did it feel?
Model Answer "Last Saturday I paid for my brother. We went to a taco place. He didn't have money. I paid — no problem. He is my brother. It felt good."
Q3
Did someone generous help you with money in your life? Who was it?
Teacher Tip A warm question — most people have a parent, friend, or mentor who helped them. "What did they do? How did you feel? Did you say thank you? Did you pay them back later?"
Block 2 · Opinion
Q4
Do you think cheap people know they are cheap — or do they think they are just "careful" with money?
Teacher Tip Fun debate point. "Is there a difference between cheap and careful? What is the line?" Keep answers in simple present — no complex structures needed.
Q5
When you go to a restaurant with friends, do you prefer to split the bill or does one person pay? What is better?
Model Answer "I prefer to split. It's easy and fair. But sometimes one person pays and the other pays next time. That's OK too. The problem is when one person never pays."
Q6
Is it OK to stop being friends with someone because they are cheap? Or is that too extreme?
Teacher Tip Easy yes/no with a reason. "What is your limit? One time? Five times? Never?" Encourages simple opinion sentences: "I think it's OK because..." / "I don't think so because..."
Block 3 · Future Plans
Q7
Are you going to go out for food or drinks with friends this week? Who is going to pay?
Model Answer "Yes, I'm going to go out with my coworkers on Friday. We always split. One person pays and the next time a different person pays. It works."
Q8
Is there a cheap person in your life? Are you going to talk to them about it — or just accept it?
Teacher Tip Practical "going to" question. "What are you going to say? How are you going to say it without a fight?" If they say "I don't have one" — ask: "Lucky you! Is that because you choose your friends carefully?"
Block 4 · Honest Reflection
Q9
Be honest — are YOU cheap sometimes? When?
Teacher Tip The confession question — always generates good language. Most people admit to some situation. "Is it with specific people? With money for yourself? With tips at a restaurant?" No judgment, just honesty.
Q10
Your friend says: "You always pay for everyone. That's not good. People use you." Do you agree — or is being generous always a good thing?
Model Answer "I think both. Generous is good. But some people use generous people. So you need to be generous with the right people. Not with everyone."

The Exit Shot
Phase 5 · Final Situation
⏱ 5 min
💣 Exit Shot
You go to a restaurant with four friends. The bill is $800 pesos. Three friends pay $200 each. Your fourth friend says: "I only have $50 today. Sorry." This is the third time. Are you going to pay for them again — or are you going to say something this time?
📱 WhatsApp Homework Record 60 seconds in English: What are you going to do? Pay — or talk to your friend? What are you going to say?
