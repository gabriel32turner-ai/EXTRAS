My House

Describe where you live. Your rooms, your things, your space — in English.

🎪 Show & Tell 💉 Vocab 🔀 Choose & Speak 🃏 Questions ✨ My Sentence
Phase 1
🎪 Show & Tell
Show a photo of your house or your favorite room
5 min
Teacher Models First
"OK, look. This is my house. It is small but it is nice. My favorite room is the kitchen because it is big and there is a lot of light. OK, now you — show me a photo of YOUR house or your favorite room. Tell me about it."
Student Sentence Starters
This is my house / apartment.
It is ___ (big / small / new / old).
My favorite room is the ___.
It is ___ because ___.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Teach "house" vs "apartment" right away. Most urban Mexican professionals live in apartments or houses in fraccionamientos.
  • If no photo available: "OK, imagine your house. What is your favorite room?" — visualization works too.
  • Common error: "My house have 3 rooms" → "My house HAS 3 rooms" or better yet: "There ARE 3 rooms."
  • Accept "departamento" and teach "apartment" on the spot.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Your kitchen is your favorite? Me too! The kitchen is the heart of the house."
  • "You just described your home in English. That is personal and that is powerful."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Every room in your house now has an English name. You will think about that tonight!"

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • MODEL pair 1: "I choose HOUSE. A house is big and there is a garden. An apartment is OK but a house is better for my family."
  • "Garden vs Garage" is perfect for Celaya/Guanajuato context — car culture is strong. Strong opinions guaranteed.
  • If student says "near to everything" → "NEAR everything" (no "to"). Quick fix, don't belabor.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "A quiet street? Me too! Noise is terrible for me." — relate personally.
  • "Garden or garage — that is the Mexican dilemma!" — humor connects.
Phase 4
🃏 Question Cards
Teacher models first — then you answer
12 min
Question 1
Is your home a house or an apartment?
"My home is a house. It is in a fraccionamiento. It is not very big but it is comfortable. There are 3 bedrooms."
Now you: "My home is a..."
Tip: Teach "home" as the emotional word, "house/apartment" as the physical word. Both are valid here.
Question 2
How many rooms are there in your house?
"There are 5 rooms in my house. There are 3 bedrooms, 1 kitchen, and 1 living room. There are also 2 bathrooms."
Now you: "There are ___ rooms..."
Tip: "There is / there are" drill. Count rooms together out loud. Point to fingers for numbers.
Question 3
What is your favorite room?
"My favorite room is the living room. It is big and comfortable. There is a big TV and a nice sofa. It is my space to relax."
Now you: "My favorite room is the..."
Tip: "Relax" is acceptable as a chunk verb here. If student expands naturally, let them. Don't cut good production.
Question 4
Is your street quiet or noisy?
"My street is noisy! There are many cars and dogs. It is not ideal but it is normal in Mexico."
Now you: "My street is..."
Tip: "Dogs" — every Mexican street has them. This will get a laugh. Let the humor flow naturally.
Question 5
Is there a garden in your house?
"Yes, there is a small garden. It is not beautiful but there are some plants. My wife is the garden person — not me!"
Now you: "There is a..." / "There is no garden."
Tip: If no garden: "There is no garden but there is a..." — teach "no" + noun construction. Always redirect to what they DO have.
Question 6
Is your house your favorite place?
"Yes! My house is my favorite place. It is where my family is. It is safe and comfortable. My house is my world."
Now you: "My house is... because..."
Tip: Emotional close. "My house is my world" — if a student says something like this, celebrate it. That is real English.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • "There is / there are" is the grammatical engine of this session. By Q6, students should be producing it naturally.
  • Watch for: "In my house have" → "In my house there are..." Extremely common transfer from Spanish. Recast every time.
  • Preposition: "IN my house" (not "on" or "at" for the inside).
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Noisy street with dogs? That is very Mexican! I understand."
  • "You described your entire house in English — rooms, street, garden. That is impressive."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Five topics done: phone, family, desk, food, house. You are building a complete description of YOUR life in English."
  • Testimonial trigger: "Could you describe your house in English last month? Now look at you."
Phase 5
✨ My Sentence
Build one perfect sentence — practice it — record it
5 min
Complete This Sentence
My favorite room in my house is the ___ because it is ___ and ___.
📱 WhatsApp Homework: Record this sentence as a voice note (30 seconds). Say it like you are giving a tour of your house. Send it to your teacher.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Model: "My favorite room in my house is the kitchen because it is big and comfortable."
  • Two adjectives connected with "and" — same pattern as My Favorite Food. Building consistency.
  • Pronunciation: "fa-vo-rite", "KITCH-en", "com-FOR-ta-ble" (3 syllables, not 4).
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Tonight when you walk into that room, think: this is my favorite room — and I said it in English."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Next time: your city. Same structure but bigger — your world is expanding."
