🪝 Opening question — Point at one student and ask:
"This morning — how many coffees did you drink before you came here? And can you work without coffee?"
Teacher Script — 4 min Two short questions — number of coffees + yes/no. Point at one student. Accept any answer. "Zero? Really? Tell me about that." "Four? Wow." 2–3 students. Say: "We talk about the full story in §4."
Pedagogical Coffee is the lowest-anxiety, highest-engagement topic for Mexican professionals — everyone has a ritual, a habit, an opinion. "Before breakfast" activates immediate recognition in most of the group. Note who drinks a lot and who doesn't — these are your organic provocateur and countervoice for §3. Retention "Next session is our last of the Health series — fast food. Same energy, same honesty."

🔁 Processing Question — Ask every student
"Who do you agree with most? Use: I agree with [name] because..."
Provocateur: Karla Medrano Karla quit coffee and says she feels better — her position challenges every heavy drinker in the room. The provocateur isn't aggressive, but for someone on their third coffee today, "I stopped and I feel great" is deeply uncomfortable. Push: "Is Karla right — or is she just different from us?" That question generates natural debate.

💣 One Scenario. Your Real Answer.

05
5 min
EXIT
💣 Exit Shot
Your doctor says: "No more coffee. Zero. Starting tomorrow." What are you going to do? Are you going to follow the advice — or not?
Answer in 2–3 sentences. Start with yes or no. Be honest — not what you think you should say, what you are actually going to do.
📌 A2.1 Model Answer — Show AFTER student speaks
"Honestly? No. I am not going to stop completely. I am going to try to drink only one cup in the morning. But zero coffee? That is not possible for me. I know it's bad. But I like it too much."
