A Trip That Went Wrong

Tell me about a trip with a problem. A bad hotel, a missed bus, terrible weather — what happened?

🪝
The Trigger
Open the Conversation
⏱ 4 min
A simple truth about travel
"The best travel stories are never about the perfect trip. They are always about the day everything went wrong."
🎙 Teacher Script

Read the quote and smile. Then ask simply: "Did you ever have a trip with a big problem? Tell me — what happened?" Point to one student to start. Keep it light and fun — bad travel stories are usually funny in retrospect. No need to analyze. Just tell the story.

The Conversation Engine
23 Minutes of Real Talk
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 — Personal Experience
Q1
Did you ever have a big problem on a trip? What happened?
Let them tell the story freely — don't interrupt for grammar. After they finish, ask: "And then what happened?" Help them sequence: "First... then... after that... in the end..."
Q2
When was the last time you missed a bus, train, or flight? What did you do?
Model answer: "Last year I missed my bus to Guadalajara. I was very angry. I waited four hours for the next one. It was terrible. But I watched a movie and ate a lot of tacos, so it was OK in the end."
Q3
Did you ever stay in a hotel that was terrible? What was wrong with it?
Great vocabulary practice for adjectives: dirty, small, noisy, expensive, cold, dark, smelly. Help students use these naturally. Ask: "Was it like the photos?" Most students have a story about this!
Block 2 — Opinion
Q4
Do you think it's important to plan every detail of a trip — or is it OK to be spontaneous?
Patricia vs. Mateo debate. Simple vocabulary needed: "I think it's better to plan because..." / "I like surprises because..." Keep it simple. No complex structures needed.
Q5
Is travel better alone or with other people? Why?
Model answer: "I prefer to travel with my family. Alone is a little sad for me. But with friends, sometimes we argue about what to do. You can't win!" Fun and relatable — most students have strong feelings about this.
Q6
Do you think travel problems make a trip more memorable — or do they just make you angry?
Simple personal opinion question. Help students structure: "I think..." / "In my experience..." / "For me, it depends on..." Don't over-complicate. Two or three sentences is a great answer at this level.
Block 3 — Future Plans
Q7
Are you going to travel somewhere this year? Where do you want to go?
Practice "going to" naturally: "I'm going to visit my family in..." / "I want to go to... but I don't have the money yet." Fun, personal, easy to answer. Let them dream a little.
Q8
If you travel somewhere new, will you make a reservation before — or will you decide when you arrive?
Simple "will" practice. Model answer: "I will always make a reservation. After my experience in Cancún, never again without a reservation!" This connects back to their bad trip story from Block 1 — nice full-circle.
Block 4 — Used To & Reflection
Q9
Did you use to travel a lot when you were younger — or not much? Is it different now?
Good "used to" practice. Model answer: "I used to travel every year with my parents. Now I don't travel much — I don't have the money or the time. I miss it." Simple and real.
Q10
Did you use to think that travel was always fun and easy? What do you think now?
Nice closing reflection. Model answer: "I used to think travel was very romantic. Now I know it's a lot of work — bags, lines, delays. But it's still worth it." Connects all the session topics into one simple conclusion.

The Exit Shot
One Question. Simple but Real.
⏱ 5 min
🎯 Final Question
Think about your best travel memory and your worst travel memory. Which one do you remember more — and why?
