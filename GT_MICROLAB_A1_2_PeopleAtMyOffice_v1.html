People at My Office

Describe the people you work with every day. What they do, what they are like, and how you work together.

🎪 Show & Tell 💉 Vocab 🔀 Choose & Speak 🃏 Questions ✨ My Sentence
Phase 1
🎪 Show & Tell
Think of one person at your office — describe them
5 min
Teacher Models First
"OK, I want to tell you about someone at my office. His name is Carlos. He works in the operations department. He is always busy — he checks his email every 5 minutes! He is nice but sometimes he talks a lot. I like working with him. OK, now you — tell me about ONE person at your office."
Student Sentence Starters
His / Her name is ___.
He / She works in the ___ department.
He / She is ___ (nice / strict / funny / quiet).
He / She always ___ (talks / works / helps / eats lunch alone).
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Third person -s is the grammar star here: "He workS", "She talkS". Model it clearly and watch for omissions.
  • Common error: "She work in production" → "She WORKS in production." Recast firmly but warmly.
  • If student doesn't know department names: teach "production", "quality", "HR", "sales" as quick chunks.
  • Accept mixed descriptions: "He is nice but he talks a lot" — that "but" is gold at A1.2.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "He talks a lot? Ha! Every office has one! Me too — I know that person."
  • "You just described a real coworker in English. That is not a textbook exercise — that is YOUR life."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Imagine introducing your team in English to a foreign visitor. After today — you can."

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • MODEL pair 1: "I choose COOL BOSS. A cool boss helps you and doesn't check your phone. My boss asks questions but he doesn't get angry. I like that."
  • Push for adverbs: "Do you ALWAYS eat alone or SOMETIMES with coworkers?" — natural drilling.
  • Watch for: "I working in a big team" → "I WORK in a big team." No present continuous at A1.2.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Eat alone with headphones? I do that too! Sometimes you need your space."
  • "Talkative office? Tell me about it! That can be fun but sometimes it is difficult to work."
Phase 4
🃏 Question Cards
Teacher models first — then you answer
12 min
Question 1
Do you like the people at your office?
"Yes, I like most of the people at my office. They are nice. Some people talk too much but in general the team is good."
Now you: "I like / I don't like the people because..."
Tip: "Most of" and "some" are useful quantifiers here. Model them naturally.
Question 2
What does your boss do every day?
"My boss checks emails in the morning. He goes to meetings. He talks to clients on the phone. He is always busy."
Now you: "My boss... every day."
Tip: Third person -s drill. Every sentence is a chance to practice: "checkS", "goeS", "talkS". Recast errors gently.
Question 3
Do you eat lunch with your coworkers?
"Sometimes. I usually eat with two coworkers — Ana and Pedro. We go to the cafeteria. We talk about work and sometimes about football."
Now you: "I usually / sometimes / never eat lunch with..."
Tip: Adverbs of frequency practice. Ask: "ALWAYS or SOMETIMES?" to push precision.
Question 4
Can you describe your best friend at work?
"My best friend at work is Luis. He works in quality. He is funny and he always helps me. We eat lunch together every day."
Now you: "My best friend at work is..."
Tip: This is personal and emotional — students produce more language when they care about the topic. Give space.
Question 5
Is there someone difficult at your office?
"Yes, there is a person in my team. He doesn't help. He never shares information. He is not a bad person but he is difficult to work with."
Now you: "There is a person who..."
Tip: Negative sentences: "doesn't help", "never shares". Model the negative form clearly. Don't force names — keep it anonymous.
Question 6
Do you talk to your coworkers in English or Spanish?
"I always speak Spanish at work. But sometimes I read emails in English. I can understand a little but I can't speak much. That is why I study English!"
Now you: "I usually speak... at work. I can / can't..."
Tip: META moment — they are talking about why they are HERE. This is retention gold. Let them reflect.
Question 7
Do you have meetings every day?
"Not every day. I have meetings on Monday and Wednesday. They are sometimes long and boring. I don't like long meetings!"
Now you: "I have meetings on... They are..."
Tip: Days of the week practice. Ask: "On Monday or every day?" Natural frequency drilling.
Question 8
What is the best thing about the people at your office?
"The best thing is that they help each other. When I have a problem, I ask my team and they always help. We are a good team."
Now you: "The best thing is..."
Tip: Positive close. Let them appreciate their team. This builds emotional connection to the lesson AND to continuing classes.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • 8 questions = ~90 seconds each. Keep pace. If one question generates long answers, let it breathe and skip the next tip.
  • Key grammar targets: third person -s, adverbs of frequency, negative "doesn't", "can/can't".
  • Watch for: "He don't help" → "He DOESN'T help." Critical A1.2 error to address.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "You described your boss, your best friend, the difficult person, AND your meetings — all in English. That is a full day at the office!"
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Next time a foreign client visits your office, you can introduce your team. In English. That is a professional skill."
  • Testimonial trigger: "Could you describe your coworkers in English before this class? Now you can — and with details."
Phase 5
✨ My Sentence
Build one perfect sentence — practice it — record it
5 min
Complete This Sentence
I like the people at my office because they ___ and they always ___.
📱 WhatsApp Homework: Record this sentence as a voice note (30 seconds). Say it naturally, like you are talking to a friend. Send it to your teacher.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Model: "I like the people at my office because they help me and they always share information."
  • Two clauses with "they" + verb. No third-person -s needed here — relief after the drilling above.
  • Pronunciation: "peo-ple" (2 syllables), "be-CAUSE", "al-ways".
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "That sentence is real. You mean it. And you said it in English. That is powerful."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "A1.2 Micro Labs: you can now describe people AND what they DO. That is a huge step from A1.1."
