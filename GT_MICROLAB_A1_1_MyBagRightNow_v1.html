My Bag Right Now

Open your bag. Describe what is inside. Say why each thing is important. Only "to be" structures.

🎪 Show & Tell 💉 Vocab 🔀 Choose & Speak 🃏 Questions ✨ My Sentence
🎪
Phase 1
Show & Tell
Open your bag right now and describe 3 things inside
5 MIN
Today's Object

Open your real bag, backpack, or purse right now. Take out 3 things and describe them.

Teacher models first (opens own bag on camera or in class):
"OK, look — this is my bag. It is black and old. Let me see... OK, number one: this is my wallet. It is brown and small. Number two: these are my keys. There are 4 keys — my house, my car, my office, and... I don't know what this one is! Number three: this is my charger. It is very important — my phone is always at 5%! OK, now you — open YOUR bag."
Your Turn — Sentence Starters
This is my _______ . It is _______ and _______ .
These are my _______ . There are _______ .
This is very important because _______ .
This is old / new / ugly / necessary.

Phase 4
Question Cards
The teacher answers first. Then it's your turn.
12 MIN
Question 1
What is the most important thing in your bag? Why?
Teacher: "My phone. My phone is essential. My contacts, my photos, my music, my maps — everything is in my phone. Without my phone, I am lost."
Now you: "The most important thing is my _______ because..."
Tip: Almost everyone says "phone." Push: "OK, but WHY? Is it for work? For family? For music? What is it for YOU specifically?" Forces personalization beyond the obvious answer.
Question 2
Is there something useless in your bag right now?
Teacher: "Yes! There are 5 old receipts in my bag. They are useless. And there is a pen that is broken. Why is it still in my bag? I don't know! I am lazy."
Now you: "Yes, there is / there are _______ . It is / They are useless because..."
Tip: This gets laughs — everyone has trash in their bag. Perfect for "there is/there are" practice. Push: "HOW MANY useless things? Count them!" Produces natural numbers + "there are."
Question 3
Is your bag heavy or light? Is that a problem?
Teacher: "My bag is very heavy. There are too many things inside. My laptop is heavy, my water bottle is heavy, my charger is heavy. My shoulder is not happy!"
Now you: "My bag is _______ . The heaviest thing is..."
Tip: Good for physical comedy — if in person, ask student to lift their bag. "Is it heavy? What is the heaviest thing?" Kinesthetic moment that breaks routine.
Question 4
Is there something surprising or strange in your bag?
Teacher: "Hmm... yes, there is a toy dinosaur in my bag. It is my son's. It is green and small. He is always putting his toys in my bag. It is funny but also annoying!"
Now you: "Yes, there is _______ . It is there because..."
Tip: The "surprise" factor is the engagement peak. If student says "no, nothing strange," push: "Come on — check the small pockets! Is there an old candy? A receipt from 2023? Something from your kids?" Everyone finds something.
Question 5
What is NOT in your bag today but is normally there?
Teacher: "My sunglasses! Today they are not in my bag. They are on my desk at home. That is a problem because it is very sunny today. I am always forgetting things!"
Now you: "Today, my _______ is not in my bag. It is in / at _______ ."
Tip: Forces "is not" / negative "to be" — important A1.1 structure. Also generates location prepositions naturally: "It is on my desk / in my car / at home."
Question 6
What is your bag like? Is it a good bag?
Teacher: "My bag is black and big. It is 3 years old. It is not new, it is not beautiful, but it is practical and comfortable. For me, a good bag is not about the brand — it is about the space inside."
Now you: "My bag is _______ . It is _______ years old. For me, a good bag is..."
Tip: Close with a personal opinion about what matters: brand? size? color? comfort? This is the student describing their values with simple adjectives — powerful for A1.1. Celebrate: "You just described your personality through your bag — in English!"
