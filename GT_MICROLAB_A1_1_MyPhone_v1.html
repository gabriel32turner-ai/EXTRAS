My Phone

Describe your phone with simple sentences. Show it, talk about it, own it — in English.

🎪 Show & Tell 💉 Vocab 🔀 Choose & Speak 🃏 Questions ✨ My Sentence
Phase 1
🎪 Show & Tell
Show your phone — describe it with simple words
5 min
Teacher Models First
"OK, look. This is MY phone. It is big. It is black. It is old — very old! But I like it. It is my favorite thing. OK, now you — show me YOUR phone. Tell me about it."
Student Sentence Starters
This is my phone.
It is ___ (color).
It is ___ (new / old / big / small).
I like it because it is ___.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Model SLOWLY. Point at your phone while speaking. Exaggerate adjectives with gestures.
  • If student freezes, prompt: "OK, what color is your phone?" — they only need ONE word to continue.
  • Accept: "My phone is Samsung" — do not correct brand usage as noun, just model naturally.
  • Common errors: "Is black" (missing subject). Gently recast: "It IS black, yes!"
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Wow, your phone is really nice!" — immediate positive feedback after first sentence.
  • "You just described your phone in English — that is impressive for day one!"
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Next class you can describe your family the same way. Same structure, new words."
  • Testimonial trigger: "How do you feel? You just spoke English about something real!"

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • MODEL the first pair yourself: "I choose NEW PHONE. A new phone is exciting. Money is boring for me. I am a technology person." Then gesture to student.
  • If student says only one word, scaffold: "OK, Apple. Apple IS...?" — wait for adjective.
  • Accept "I like" as a memorized chunk here: "I like Apple" is fine.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Great choice! You explained that really well."
  • React genuinely: "Oh, Android? Interesting! I am Apple. We are different!"
Phase 4
🃏 Question Cards
Teacher models first — then you answer
12 min
Question 1
What is your phone?
"My phone is a Samsung Galaxy. It is big and it is black. It is two years old."
Now you: "My phone is a..."
Tip: Accept brand names as-is. Help with colors if needed. Model: "It is [color]."
Question 2
Is your phone new or old?
"My phone is old. It is 3 years old. But it is good! The camera is nice."
Now you: "My phone is... It is..."
Tip: If student says "is old" without subject, recast: "IT is old, yes!"
Question 3
What is your favorite app?
"My favorite app is WhatsApp. It is important for my work. It is fast and easy."
Now you: "My favorite app is..."
Tip: Apps they know: WhatsApp, YouTube, Instagram. Ask: "Is it fun or important for work?"
Question 4
Is your phone expensive or cheap?
"My phone is expensive. It is an iPhone. iPhones are very expensive in Mexico!"
Now you: "My phone is..."
Tip: This is a natural conversation topic for Mexican professionals. Let them react and expand.
Question 5
Where is your phone right now?
"My phone is here, on the table. It is next to my coffee. It is always with me!"
Now you: "My phone is..."
Tip: Teach "here", "on the table", "in my hand" as fixed phrases. Gesture helps.
Question 6
Is your phone important for your work?
"Yes! My phone is VERY important for my work. WhatsApp is essential. Email is on my phone. It is like my office!"
Now you: "My phone is... for my work because..."
Tip: This connects personal to professional. If they say "I use", gently recast to "It is useful." Keep to "to be" structures.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • ALWAYS answer the question yourself FIRST. Read the model answer naturally, then say "Now you."
  • Spend ~2 minutes per question. If student gives one sentence, ask a follow-up: "Is it good?"
  • Watch for: "My phone have" → correct to "My phone IS" / "I have a phone" (chunk).
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Me too!" and "Really?" — use the colloquial phrases from Vocab to model natural reactions.
  • "You just answered 6 questions about your phone — all in English. That is real progress!"
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Next time, same structure but about your family. You already know how to do this."
  • Testimonial trigger: "If someone asks you — can you describe your phone in English? Now you can say YES."
Phase 5
✨ My Sentence
Build one perfect sentence — practice it — record it
5 min
Complete This Sentence
The most important thing in my phone is ___ because ___.
📱 WhatsApp Homework: Record this sentence as a voice note (30 seconds). Say it clearly, with confidence. Send it to your teacher.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Model first: "The most important thing in my phone is WhatsApp because it is essential for my work."
  • Student repeats 2-3 times. Correct pronunciation of "important" and "because."
  • If student struggles with "most important" — simplify to "The best thing in my phone is..."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Perfect! That sentence is YOUR sentence now. Nobody else has it."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "You described your phone, chose preferences, answered questions, and built a perfect sentence — all in English. In 35 minutes."
  • "Next session we describe your family. Same system, new words. See you there."
