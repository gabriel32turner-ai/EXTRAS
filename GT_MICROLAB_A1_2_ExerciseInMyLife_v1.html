Exercise in My Life

Talk about what you do to stay active. What can you do? What can't you do? Be honest about your habits.

🎪 Show & Tell 💉 Vocab 🔀 Choose & Speak 🃏 Questions ✨ My Sentence

Phase 1
Show & Tell
Show something on your phone related to exercise — a fitness app, a gym photo, or your step counter
5 MIN
Today's Object

Show your step counter, a gym selfie, your running app, or your sneakers — anything related to exercise (or the lack of it!).

Teacher models first:
"OK, look — this is my step counter. Today I have 2,400 steps. That's terrible! I usually walk about 5,000 steps a day, but I need 10,000. I go to the gym 3 times a week — Monday, Wednesday, and Friday. I can run 5 km but I can't run more than that. I also walk my dog every morning — that's my best exercise! OK, now you — show me something about YOUR exercise."

Phase 4
Question Cards
The teacher answers first. Then it's your turn.
12 MIN
Question 1
Do you exercise? How often?
Teacher: "I exercise 3 times a week. I go to the gym on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday. On Tuesday and Thursday I rest. On weekends I sometimes walk my dog for 30 minutes, but that's not really exercise!"
Now you: "I exercise _______ times a week. I usually _______ on..."
Tip: If student says "never" — no judgment. Ask: "Why not? No time? No gym? You don't like it?" The reason IS the conversation. If they do exercise, push for specifics: "WHAT do you do? WHERE? WHEN?"
Question 2
Can you run 5 kilometers without stopping?
Teacher: "Yes, I can run 5 km, but it takes me 35 minutes. I can't run 10 km — that's too much for me. I run on the treadmill because I can watch TV at the same time. I can't run outside — Celaya is too hot!"
Now you: "I can / can't run 5 km. I can run _______ but I can't..."
Tip: Perfect can/can't drill. Stack the abilities: "Can you run? Can you swim? Can you do 20 push-ups? Can you touch your toes?" Each answer is a full sentence with real physical context.
Question 3
Is there a gym near your house? Do you like it?
Teacher: "Yes, there is a gym 10 minutes from my house. It's called Smart Fit. There are many machines and there is a yoga area. But there are too many people at 7 PM — I always go at 6 AM because it's empty."
Now you: "There is _______ near my house. There are _______ . I go at..."
Tip: Great for "there is/are" stacking. Push: "What is INSIDE? Are there weights? Is there a pool? Are there classes? How many people are there at your time?"
Question 4
What sport or exercise do you hate?
Teacher: "I hate yoga. I know it's good for you, but I can't sit still for one hour. I also don't like swimming because I'm not a good swimmer — I can float but I can't really swim fast. I look ridiculous!"
Now you: "I hate _______ . I can't _______ because..."
Tip: Hating something produces animated language. Push: "WHY do you hate it? Is it boring? Is it too hard? Is it too slow?" The reason is more interesting than the hate.
Question 5
Do you walk a lot every day or do you drive everywhere?
Teacher: "I drive everywhere. I live in Celaya — you need a car! I walk about 3,000 steps on a normal day. That's not a lot. I sometimes walk to the store, but I usually drive because it's faster."
Now you: "I usually _______ everywhere. I walk about _______ steps. I _______ because..."
Tip: Very relatable for Mexican professionals — car culture is strong in cities like Celaya. Push: "Can you walk to work? Can you walk to a store? Or do you NEED the car for everything?"
Question 6
What is your excuse for not exercising?
Teacher: "My excuse is time. I always say 'I don't have time,' but that's not true — I have time for Netflix! I think the real problem is energy. After work, I don't want to go to the gym. I just want to sit on the sofa."
Now you: "My excuse is _______ . I always say '_______ ' but I know..."
Tip: The "excuse" question is the engagement peak — everyone laughs because everyone has excuses. Push: "Is that a REAL excuse or a fake excuse? Do you believe your own excuse?" Gets honest, funny answers.
Question 7
Do you exercise more on weekdays or on weekends?
Teacher: "I exercise on weekdays — Monday, Wednesday, Friday at the gym. On weekends I rest. I sometimes walk, but I never go to the gym on Saturday or Sunday. Weekends are for food and Netflix!"
Now you: "I exercise more on _______ . On weekdays I _______ . On weekends I..."
Tip: Contrast weekday vs. weekend. Push: "Is that a good balance? Do you think you need MORE exercise or is it enough? What do you do on your rest days?"
Question 8
Can you do 10 push-ups right now?
Teacher: "Right now? Hmm... I can do 10 but they're not perfect. I can do 5 good push-ups and 5 bad push-ups! I can also do 30 sit-ups. But I can't do pull-ups — my arms are not strong enough."
Now you: "I can do _______ push-ups. I can also _______ but I can't..."
Tip: If in person — challenge them to actually try! Even 2 push-ups creates energy and laughter. This is kinesthetic engagement at its best. Online: "OK, be honest — how many CAN you do? Really?"
