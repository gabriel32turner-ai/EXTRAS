§ 01 · The Trigger
🪝 A Simple Question About Work
Read aloud · First reaction · No debate yet · 4 min max
01
4 min
⏰

30 hrs

Sources: OECD Work-Life Balance Index 2023 · INEGI Encuesta Nacional de Ocupación y Empleo
🪝 Opening Question — Read and point at someone
"How many hours do you work every day? Is that enough — or too many?"
Teacher Mechanics — 4 min Read the numbers. Point at one student: "How many hours do YOU work?" Simple past or simple present — both OK. 2–3 reactions. Keep it light. Say: "We talk more about this in §4."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • At A2.2, keep the trigger simple. The Germany comparison (30 hrs, more productive) is the one that generates a reaction — even at this level. Just point to the numbers.
  • Accept any answer: "I work 8 hours," "I work a lot," "My boss is always there." All are valid A2.2 outputs.
  • Don't push for complex opinions yet — just activate the topic.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • Ask with curiosity, not judgment: "43 hours a week — that's almost 9 hours a day! Is that you?"
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Retention: "Next lab we talk about money — cash or card. Very different topic, same pace."

⚡ Production Challenge
Pick 3 words. One sentence each about your job right now. Short and simple. Just talk.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • At A2.2, one-sentence production is the goal. Don't push for more. "I finish work at 6pm. I am tired." — that is a perfect A2.2 production moment.
  • "Overtime" is a target word — some students know it in Spanish (tiempo extra). Confirm and use it.
  • Best trigger: "Three words about YOUR job. Say one sentence for each. Very simple."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "These are words you can use tomorrow morning at work — in English."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Every session we build 20 words. After 10 sessions, you have 200 real words. That's how it works."
§ 03 · Opinion Wall

10 min
Processing Question — Same for all 4
"Who do you agree with? Why?"
Keep it simple
🔁 Processing Question — Pairs → Group
"Who do you agree with? Why? Use 2 or 3 sentences."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • At A2.2, the opinion wall is for comprehension + simple production. Don't expect debate. Expect: "I agree with Sofía because I am also tired."
  • The provocateur is Marcos — his "more hours = more money" position is simple but challenges the other three. Even A2.2 students can react to that.
  • If a student only says "I agree with her" — model the next step: "Because...?" Give a simple sentence starter.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • Read the cards clearly and slowly. Let students follow along. For A2.2 this is also reading comprehension practice — that's OK.
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Testimonial trigger: "Which person talks like someone from YOUR job? Tell me in one sentence."
§ 04 · The Conversation Engine
🗣 10 Questions. 4 Blocks. 23 Minutes.
Past → Opinion → Future → Past habits · Short answers OK · Keep moving
04
23 min
⚡
A2.2 Rule: Short answers are OK. 1 or 2 sentences is great. Don't wait for perfect English — just talk.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • At A2.2, the chips are sentence starters — use them actively. If a student is silent, point to a chip: "Start with that one. Just finish the sentence."
  • Block 3 uses "going to" and "will" — both are A2.2 targets. Don't correct the confusion between them at this level. Just celebrate production.
  • Block 4 "used to" — model with a gesture if needed: "Before — in the past — I used to work 10 hours. Now I work 8." The before/now contrast makes it clear.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • When a student gives a complete sentence: celebrate immediately. "Good! That's a complete English sentence about your life. Perfect."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • After Q10: "You answered 10 questions about your work — in English. All correct. That's A2.2."
§ 05 · The Exit Shot
💣 One Question. Your Answer.
5 min · No chips · Answer in 2–3 sentences · Then the loop
05
5 min
EXIT
💣 Exit Shot
If you can choose, are you going to work fewer hours next year — or the same? What is your plan?
Answer in 2 or 3 sentences. Use "I am going to..." or "I want to..." Simple and honest.
📌 A2.2 Model Answer — Reveal AFTER student speaks
"I am going to try to work fewer hours next year. I want more time for my family. My plan is to finish work at 5pm and not check my phone after that."
🔁 Retention Loop — WhatsApp Homework "Before next session — record a 60-second voice note in English. Your answer to today's Exit Shot. Don't write it first. Just talk. Send it to [teacher number]."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • The Exit Shot at A2.2 is a personal future plan — simple and achievable to answer. "Going to" is the target structure. Accept "I want to" as well — both are valid here.
  • If a student says "I don't know" — give a sentence starter: "OK — say: 'I am going to work... hours.' Just finish that sentence."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • The model answer is short and real — 3 sentences, simple words. Point this out: "Three sentences. Simple words. Perfect A2.2 English. That's the goal."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Closing: "Record that answer tonight on WhatsApp. 60 seconds. I want to hear YOUR plan in English."
