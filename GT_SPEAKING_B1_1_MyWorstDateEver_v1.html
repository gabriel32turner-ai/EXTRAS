My Worst
Date Ever

Everyone has a story. The person who didn't stop talking. The restaurant that was closed. The moment you wanted to disappear. Let's hear yours.

🪝
The Trigger
Phase 1 · True Story Starter
⏱ 4 min
YouGov Survey · Dating Habits · 2022
"76% of people say they have had at least one date so bad they still talk about it years later. The most common reasons: the other person talked only about themselves, someone arrived very late, and one person was clearly still in love with their ex."
Teacher Script: Read the stat, then laugh a little. Ask: "76% — that's almost everyone. Have you ever had a date like that?" If the student is single or has been single, they'll have a story. If they've been with their partner for a long time, ask: "What about before your relationship — or a friend's story?" The goal is to get them laughing and talking fast. Bad date stories are the perfect B1.1 warm-up: they're short, funny, personal, and don't require abstract vocabulary.

The Conversation Engine
Phase 4 · 10 Questions · 4 Blocks
⏱ 23 min
Block 1 · Personal Experience
Q1
Have you ever had a really bad date? What happened?
The main story question. Let the student tell it from start to finish without interrupting. Then ask short follow-up questions: "How did you feel?" / "What did you do?" / "Did you see that person again?" Target: past simple storytelling. Key verbs: arrived, said, left, felt, thought.
Q2
Have you ever left a date early — or wanted to but didn't? Why?
Push: "What stopped you from leaving?" Key vocab: excuse, polite, escape, stay, leave. Model: "I wanted to leave after 20 minutes but I felt bad. So I stayed 2 hours and checked my phone the whole time." Target: past simple + past continuous for what was happening at the time.
Q3
Did you ever go on a date with someone you already knew it was a bad idea — but you went anyway?
Most people have a "I knew it was a bad idea" story. Push: "Why did you go?" / "Was it as bad as you thought?" Target: past simple + simple reason clauses (because, so, but). Fun, low-pressure storytelling question.
Block 2 · Opinion
Q4
Is it OK to leave a date early if it's clearly not going well — or is that rude?
Good for: should, can, it's OK to / it's not OK to. Push: "How would you feel if someone left YOUR date early?" Model: "I think it's OK to leave if the person is rude or you feel uncomfortable. But if it's just boring — maybe stay 1 hour and be polite." Target: modals + opinion phrases.
Q5
Do you think you can know in the first 10 minutes if a date is going to work — or do you need more time?
Push: "What do you feel in those first 10 minutes? Is it the same as love at first sight?" Target: present simple for beliefs + can/can't. Model: "I think I know very fast — not about love, but about if I want to see this person again. It's a feeling."
Q6
Who should pay on the first date? Is the old rule still OK today?
Polarizing but fun for B1.1. Push: "Has this ever been awkward — the moment of paying?" Target: should + cultural comparison. Key phrases: split the bill, take turns, offer to pay, feel awkward. No single right answer — the goal is opinion production.
Block 3 · Hypothetical
Q7
What would you do if your date arrived and looked completely different from their photos?
Target: second conditional (would + base verb). Push: "Would you stay? Would you say something?" Model: "I would stay for one coffee — because it's not nice to leave immediately. But I wouldn't go on a second date." Good for activating: honest, polite, fair, disappointed, surprised.
Q8
What would you do if a friend asked you: "How was your date?" — but it was a disaster? Would you tell the truth?
Light and fun. Target: second conditional + reported speech basics. Push: "What would you actually say?" Get the student to produce a short sentence: "I would probably say 'it was fine' and then change the subject." Role-play with teacher if possible.
Block 4 · Provocative
Q9
Is online dating a good way to meet people — or does it make everything worse?
Broad opinion question. Push: "Have you or someone you know tried it? What happened?" Target: present simple + past simple example. Key vocab: app, profile, swipe, match, meet in person, fake. Good for generating 3-4 exchanges if the student has a story or strong opinion.
Q10
Diego says maybe YOU were the bad date in someone's story. Could he be right about you?
The self-reflection closer. Let the student think. Push: "Was there ever a date where you know you weren't your best? What were you like?" Most people will admit to one bad-date-self story — being too nervous, too distracted, too cold. Target: past simple + honest self-description adjectives: nervous, distracted, boring, unfair.

The Exit Shot
Phase 5 · One Final Dilemma
⏱ 5 min
Final Question
You are on a first date. After 20 minutes you know — 100% — that this is not the person for you. But you can see that they are really excited. They think it's going well. Dinner is just starting. What do you do?
Homework Loop: "Record a 60-second WhatsApp voice message. Tell me what you do — and give me the exact words you would say to that person. In English. No script."
