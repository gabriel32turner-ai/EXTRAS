My Favorite Food

Talk about what you love to eat. Simple words, real flavors, your life in English.

🎪 Show & Tell 💉 Vocab 🔀 Choose & Speak 🃏 Questions ✨ My Sentence
Phase 1
🎪 Show & Tell
Show a photo of your favorite food on your phone
5 min
Teacher Models First
"OK, look at my phone. This is my favorite food — tacos! Tacos are delicious. They are Mexican food. I like tacos with salsa. They are perfect. OK, now you — show me a photo of YOUR favorite food. Or just tell me!"
Student Sentence Starters
My favorite food is ___.
It is ___ (delicious / good / amazing).
It is ___ food (Mexican / Italian / Chinese / American).
I like it because it is ___.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Food is UNIVERSAL. Every student has an opinion. This is a low-anxiety opener.
  • If student says "pozole" or "enchiladas" — accept the Spanish word and model: "Pozole! Pozole IS delicious."
  • Common error: "My favorite food are tacos" → "My favorite food IS tacos." Treat "food" as singular concept.
  • Accept "I like" as memorized chunk throughout this session.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Oh, tacos! Me too! Tacos are the best!" — react with energy. Food talk is fun.
  • "You just described your favorite food in English — and it sounds delicious!"
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Next time you eat that food, think: I can describe this in English. That is real."

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • MODEL pair 1: "I choose MEXICAN FOOD. Mexican food is the best in the world. It is spicy and delicious. Italian food is good but Mexican food is number one!"
  • "Spicy vs Sweet" is perfect for Mexican professionals — everyone has a strong opinion.
  • If student says "I like the both" → "I like BOTH" (no article). But don't overcorrect — just model.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Only coffee for breakfast? Really? You are brave!" — keep it playful and real.
  • "Home food vs restaurant — that is a hard choice!"
Phase 4
🃏 Question Cards
Teacher models first — then you answer
12 min
Question 1
What is your favorite food?
"My favorite food is tacos al pastor. They are delicious. They are spicy and they are cheap. I like them with pineapple."
Now you: "My favorite food is..."
Tip: Let them use the Spanish name for the food. Your job: help them describe it with "it is" + adjectives.
Question 2
What is your breakfast?
"My breakfast is simple — coffee and bread. Sometimes eggs. It is fast because my morning is busy."
Now you: "My breakfast is..."
Tip: "My breakfast" keeps it in "to be" territory. If they say "I eat eggs" — gently recast: "Your breakfast IS eggs. Nice!"
Question 3
Is the food at your work good or bad?
"The food at my work is... OK. The cafeteria is not bad but it is not delicious. It is normal."
Now you: "The food at my work is..."
Tip: Corporate context — they all have opinions about the comedor. This is gold for manufacturing clients. Let them vent!
Question 4
What is the best restaurant in Celaya?
"The best restaurant in Celaya is La Trucha Loca. The food is fresh and delicious. It is not cheap but it is amazing."
Now you: "The best restaurant is..."
Tip: Localize to student's city if not in Celaya. They LOVE recommending places. Ask: "Is it expensive?"
Question 5
Is there a food you think is terrible?
"Yes! Sushi is terrible for me. I am sorry but it is not good. The fish is cold and it is strange. No thank you!"
Now you: "___ is terrible for me because..."
Tip: Negative opinions generate MORE language than positive ones. Let them complain — in English!
Question 6
Is food important for your family?
"Yes, food is very important for my family. Sunday lunch is special. My mom's food is the best in the world. It is love."
Now you: "Food is... for my family because..."
Tip: Emotional close. Sunday lunch is sacred in Mexican culture. This will produce long, heartfelt answers. Give them space.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Food questions are HIGH MOTIVATION — students want to talk about this. Your job: scaffold with "to be" structures.
  • Watch for: "The food of my work" → "The food AT my work." Preposition fix — recast, don't lecture.
  • "My mom's food" — possessive 's is acceptable at A1.1 as a chunk. Don't over-explain the grammar.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Your mom's food is the best? I believe you! That is beautiful."
  • "Sushi is terrible? Oh no! But I respect that. Me too — I am a taco person."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Phone, family, desk, food — you can describe four parts of your life in English now."
  • Testimonial trigger: "If a foreign colleague asks about food in Mexico — you can answer now!"
Phase 5
✨ My Sentence
Build one perfect sentence — practice it — record it
5 min
Complete This Sentence
My favorite food is ___ because it is ___ and it is ___.
📱 WhatsApp Homework: Record this sentence as a voice note (30 seconds). Say it with feeling — like you are recommending it to a friend. Send it to your teacher.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Model: "My favorite food is enchiladas because they are delicious and they are spicy."
  • Two adjectives connected with "and" — simple but produces a complete, impressive sentence.
  • Pronunciation drill: "fa-vo-rite", "de-LI-cious", "be-CAUSE".
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "That sentence sounds REAL. If someone asks you in English — you have the answer."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Four Micro Labs done. You can describe your phone, family, desk, and food. Next: your house!"
